Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: Arturo Medina Vela v. Mexico
- Case ID: CRPD/C/22/D/32/2015
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Key Parties Involved: Arturo Medina Vela (author), Mexico (State party)
- Date of Decision: 19 August 2015 (initial submission), 6 September 2019 (adoption of Views)

Procedural History:
- The author, Arturo Medina Vela, a Mexican national with an intellectual and psychosocial disability, was deprived of his liberty at a men's psychosocial rehabilitation center in Mexico City.
- The author claims that the State party violated his rights under the Convention, including the right to enjoy legal capacity on an equal basis with others, the right to be free from discrimination, and the right to be treated with dignity.
- The State party has ratified the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) and its Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee considered that the author was deprived of his liberty without due process and that his rights under the Convention were violated.
- The Committee found that the author was not given the opportunity to dispute the charges against him and that his right to legal capacity was not respected.
- The Committee further found that the State party's decision to apply the special procedure for persons exempt from criminal liability violated the author's rights under the Convention.
- The Committee reasoned that the application of the special procedure resulted in discriminatory treatment of the author and denied him the opportunity to participate directly in the proceedings and exercise his right to appeal.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee adopted the Views that the State party violated the author's rights under the Convention.
- The Committee recommended that the State party provide the author with an effective remedy, including compensation, and take measures to prevent similar violations in the future.
- The Committee also recommended that the State party review its legislation and practices to ensure that persons with disabilities are provided with appropriate support and reasonable accommodations to enable them to exercise their legal capacity on an equal basis with others.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision highlights the importance of ensuring that persons with disabilities are afforded equal protection under the law and are not subjected to discrimination or unequal treatment.
- The decision emphasizes the need for States to provide reasonable accommodations and support to persons with disabilities to enable them to exercise their legal capacity and participate fully in legal proceedings.
- The decision sets a precedent for future cases involving the rights of persons with disabilities and underscores the obligations of States parties under the Convention.
