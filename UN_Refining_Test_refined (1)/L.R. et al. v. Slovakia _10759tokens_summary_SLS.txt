Combined Summary:

Case Details:
The case, L. R. and 26 other Slovak citizens of Roma ethnicity vs. Slovak Republic, was brought before the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD). The petitioners alleged violations of their rights under the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (CERD) and the Slovak Constitution. The petitioners claimed that they have exhausted all effective domestic remedies and that the municipal council resolution violated their rights to equal treatment, dignity, freedom from discrimination, and the right to housing. The Committee found the petition admissible at its sixty-fourth session on February 27, 2004.

Procedural History:
The case was filed by L. R. and 26 other Slovak citizens of Roma ethnicity against the Slovak Republic. The Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD) had jurisdiction over the case. The key parties involved were the petitioners and the respondent, Slovak Republic. The case ID is Communication No. 31/2003.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case raises issues related to the protection of the rights of minority groups, particularly the Roma community, from discrimination. The petitioners argued that the Slovak Republic failed to ensure that all public authorities and institutions act in conformity with the obligation to engage in no act or practice of racial discrimination. They also claimed that the State party failed to nullify any laws or regulations that perpetuated racial discrimination. The case highlights the importance of ensuring access to effective remedies for victims of discrimination and the duty of States parties to prohibit and end racial discrimination.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the petitioners had exhausted available and effective domestic remedies and were victims of an absence of a remedy. The Committee also found that the claims advanced by the petitioners were sufficiently substantiated for purposes of admissibility. The Committee requested the Slovak Republic to take measures to address the issues raised.

Impact and Significance:
This case emphasizes the importance of protecting the rights of minority groups and ensuring that all public authorities and institutions act in conformity with the obligation to engage in no act or practice of racial discrimination. The decision sets a precedent for future cases related to racial discrimination and the exhaustion of domestic remedies.
