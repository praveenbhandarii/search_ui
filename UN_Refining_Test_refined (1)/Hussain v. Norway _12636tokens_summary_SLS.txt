Summary:

Case Details:
The case, Sajjad Hussain v. Norway, was brought before the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD) under communication No. 61/2016. Sajjad Hussain, a Norwegian citizen of Pakistani descent, was held in pretrial detention for 14 months from February 4, 2016, to April 13, 2016. He claimed that his rights under articles 2 (1) (a), 5 (a), and 6 of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination were violated. The Committee adopted an opinion on April 17, 2023, finding that the author's rights had been violated.

Procedural History:
The case was brought before the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD) under communication No. 61/2016. The key parties involved in the case were Sajjad Hussain as the author and Norway as the State party. The jurisdiction and competence of the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination were invoked in this case.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The Committee found that the author's rights under articles 2 (1) (a), 5 (a), and 6 of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination had been violated. The violations included the author being accused of crimes without evidence, being held in pretrial detention without sufficient reasons, and the prolongation of his detention being disproportionate to the alleged offense. The Committee considered the author's association with Norway and the risk of flight if released, but still found that his rights had been violated.

It emphasizes the importance of ensuring individuals' rights are protected under international law and not discriminated against based on their background.
