Summary:

Case Details:
- Case name: N.B. and M.W.J. v. the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
- Case ID: CRPD/C/22/D/43/2017
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities under the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: N.B. and M.W.J. (authors/claimants) and the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (State party)

Procedural History:
- The authors, N.B. and M.W.J., are disabled individuals who rely on the Independent Living Fund for support.
- The Fund was closed to new applicants in 2010 without adequate consultation and impact assessment, resulting in a reduction in support for the authors.
- The authors claimed that the closure of the Fund violated their rights under Articles 17, 19, 20, 30, and 31 of the Convention.
- The State party argued that the authors had not exhausted all available domestic remedies and that the complaint was an abuse of the right of submission.
- The Committee rejected the State party's arguments and found that the authors had demonstrated a prima facie case that their rights had been violated.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee held that the closure of the Independent Living Fund without adequate consultation and impact assessment was a violation of the authors' rights under the Convention.
- The Committee also found that the failure to provide an effective remedy for the authors was a further violation of their rights.
- The decision emphasizes the importance of consultation and impact assessment in policies and programs affecting individuals with disabilities.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee ruled in favor of the authors, finding that the closure of the Independent Living Fund violated their rights under the Convention.
- The Committee emphasized the need for an effective remedy for the authors.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision has implications for the rights of individuals with disabilities to equal access to support services and independent living.
- It highlights the importance of ensuring that policies and programs affecting individuals with disabilities are developed and implemented in a way that respects their rights and dignity.
- The decision also emphasizes the importance of ensuring that domestic remedies are available and accessible to individuals who claim to be victims of discrimination or violations of their rights.
