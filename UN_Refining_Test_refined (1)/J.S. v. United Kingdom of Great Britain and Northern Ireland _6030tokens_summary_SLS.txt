Procedural History:
The case was initially brought before the European Court of Human Rights (ECtHR), which found no violation of the rights and freedoms set out in the Convention or the Protocols thereto. The decision of the ECtHR was then considered by the Committee against Torture. The case falls under the jurisdiction of the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

The case involves the interpretation and application of human rights law, specifically the rights of persons with disabilities. The ECtHR's decision was based on its assessment of whether there was a violation of the Convention or the Protocols thereto. The Committee against Torture's decision was based on its analysis of the ECtHR's decision and its own consideration of the merits of the case.

The Committee against Torture found that the ECtHR's decision was not solely based on procedural issues but also on reasons that indicated a sufficient consideration of the merits of the case. The Committee rendered a decision based on the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights.
