Case Details:
- Case Name: Miroslav Lacko v. Slovak Republic
- Case ID: Unknown
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Human Rights Committee
- Document Details: Order or Judgment issued by the European Commission against Racism and Intolerance (ECRI)
- Case Summary: The petitioner, a member of the Romani ethnic group, was refused service at a restaurant in Slovakia. The petitioner filed a complaint under the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination. The Regional Prosecution Office initially found the respondent justified in refusing service, but the Prosecutor General disagreed and indicted the respondent for incitement to national or racial hatred. The court found the respondent guilty and imposed a fine or imprisonment.

Procedural History:
- The petitioner filed a complaint under Article 14, paragraph 7 (a) of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination.
- The Regional Prosecution Office initially found the respondent justified in refusing service based on ethnicity.
- The Prosecutor General disagreed and indicted the respondent for incitement to national or racial hatred.
- The court found the respondent guilty and imposed a fine or imprisonment.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The ECRI had previously stated that there were no criminal remedies for acts of discrimination in Slovakia.
- The Committee considered all the information submitted by the petitioner and the State party and found that the penalty imposed on the respondent constituted sanctions compatible with the obligations of the State party.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The court found the respondent guilty of incitement to national or racial hatred.
- The court imposed a fine or imprisonment on the respondent.

Impact and Significance:
- This case highlights the importance of addressing discrimination in access to public accommodations.
- The Committee's decision emphasizes the need for the State party to complete its legislation to guarantee the right of access to public places without racial discrimination.
- The case has implications for the efficiency and effectiveness of the legal system in addressing discrimination cases.
