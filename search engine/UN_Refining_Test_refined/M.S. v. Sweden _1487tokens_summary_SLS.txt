Combined Summary:

Case Details:
The case, Communication No. 66/2019 (UNCRPD), was brought before the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The author, M.S., a national of Afghanistan, claimed that his deportation to Afghanistan would violate articles 10, 15, 16, and 25 of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The Committee found the communication admissible and revealed a clear violation of the Convention. The State party's request to discontinue the consideration of the communication was accepted due to the statute-barred decision to expel the author.

Procedural History:
The author submitted the communication on 20 August 2019, and the Committee requested interim measures to prevent the author's removal to Afghanistan while the case was pending. The State party, Sweden, submitted observations on admissibility and the merits, arguing for the inadmissibility of the communication. The author also submitted comments on the State party's observations, asserting the admissibility and violation of the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The Committee declared the communication admissible in all its parts and found a clear violation of the Convention. The State party's arguments for inadmissibility were rejected. The decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication was based on the statute-barred expulsion decision.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee declared the communication admissible and found a violation of the Convention. The decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication was accepted due to the statute-barred expulsion decision.

Impact and Significance:
The decision emphasizes the importance of protecting the rights of persons with disabilities, particularly in the context of deportation and expulsion. It also highlights the need to consider the specific circumstances of individuals with disabilities in immigration proceedings.
