Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: M.R. i V. v. Spain
- Case ID: CRPD/C/26/D/48/2018
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Views adopted by the Committee on 24 March 2022
- Case Summary: The author, M.R. i V., filed a complaint against Spain for violating their rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD). The author's application for modified duty employment was denied, and their appeals to the Barcelona Administrative Court No. 13 and the High Court of Justice of Catalonia were dismissed. The case was brought before the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

Procedural History:
- Date of Initial Submission: 17 November 2015
- Key Parties Involved: M.R. i V. (author), Spain (State party)
- Jurisdiction and Competence: The case was brought before the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities, which has jurisdiction to consider complaints alleging violations of the CRPD by States parties.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: The Committee examined whether Spain violated the author's rights under the CRPD by denying them access to modified duty employment and failing to provide reasonable accommodation.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: The Committee considered articles 5 and 27 of the CRPD, which guarantee the right to equality and non-discrimination and the right to work for persons with disabilities.
- Court's Analysis: The Committee found that Spain violated the author's rights under the CRPD. It concluded that the State party's denial of access to modified duty employment and failure to provide reasonable accommodation constituted discrimination and a violation of the author's rights. The Committee also noted that the State party's failure to engage in dialogue with the author and assess the obstacles preventing them from taking up a non-police post with generic support duties further violated their rights under the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
- Court Decisions: The Committee held that Spain violated the author's rights under articles 5 and 27 of the CRPD.
- Legal Reasoning: The State party's denial of access to modified duty employment and failure to provide reasonable accommodation were deemed as violations. The Committee emphasized the importance of reasonable accommodation for individuals with disabilities and the obligation of States parties to engage in dialogue to identify and address obstacles to their full participation in the workforce.

Impact and Significance:
- Implications: This case highlights the importance of providing reasonable accommodation for individuals with disabilities in the workplace. It emphasizes the need for States parties to engage in dialogue with individuals with disabilities to identify and address obstacles to their full participation in the workforce.
- Precedents Set: The decision sets a precedent for the implementation of the CRPD and the rights of persons with disabilities in Spain and globally.
- Broader Legal Impact: The decision underscores the importance of ensuring that laws and policies are designed and implemented in a manner that promotes the rights of persons with disabilities and prevents discrimination based on disability.
