    "Case Summary": "This case involves an appeal filed by Bahrain, Egypt, and the United Arab Emirates (Appellants) against the decision of the Council of the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO Council) regarding a dispute with Qatar (Respondent). The dispute revolves around the jurisdiction of the ICAO Council to decide disputes related to the interpretation or application of the International Air Services Transit Agreement (IASTA). The Appellants raised preliminary objections, arguing that the ICAO Council lacked jurisdiction and that Qatar's application was inadmissible. The ICAO Council rejected the preliminary objections, leading to the appeal. The Appellants also alleged that the aviation restrictions imposed by Qatar violated their obligations under the IASTA and international law. The International Court of Justice (ICJ) upheld the ICAO Council's jurisdiction and dismissed the appeal, finding that the Council did not err in rejecting the preliminary objections and that Qatar's claims were admissible.",
