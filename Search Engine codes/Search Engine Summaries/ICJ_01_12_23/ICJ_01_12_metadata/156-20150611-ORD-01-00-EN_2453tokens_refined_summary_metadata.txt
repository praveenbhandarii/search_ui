The items were allegedly seized from the offices of one of Timor-Leste's legal advisers in the Australian Capital Territory. Timor-Leste claimed that the seized items included correspondence between the Timor-Leste government and its legal advisers related to a pending arbitration under the Timor Sea Treaty.
