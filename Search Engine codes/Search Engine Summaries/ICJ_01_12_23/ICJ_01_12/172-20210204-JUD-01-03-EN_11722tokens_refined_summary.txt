Case ID: ICJ/172
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2021
Key Parties Involved: Qatar, United Arab Emirates (UAE)
Case Name: Application of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (Qatar v. United Arab Emirates)

Case Summary:
Qatar filed a case against the UAE alleging violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (CERD). The dispute revolves around the UAE's travel bans and expulsion orders targeting Qatari nationals based on their national origin. Qatar claims that the UAE's actions constitute direct and indirect discrimination against Qatari nationals.

Context:
- The case was filed on June 11, 2018, under the compromissory clause contained in Article 22 of CERD.
- The UAE objected to the court's jurisdiction, arguing that the contested measures do not fall within the scope of CERD.
- Qatar argues that the term "national origin" in CERD encompasses differential treatment based on current nationality.
- The majority of the court rejected the UAE's objection and found that the court has jurisdiction to entertain Qatar's application.
