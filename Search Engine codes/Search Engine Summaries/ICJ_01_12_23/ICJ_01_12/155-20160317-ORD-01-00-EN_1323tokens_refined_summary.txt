Case ID: ICJ/155
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2016
Key Parties Involved: Republic of Nicaragua, Republic of Colombia
Case Name: Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Summary:
Nicaragua filed an Application against Colombia, alleging violations of its sovereign rights and maritime zones as declared in the ICJ's 2012 Judgment in the case Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia). Colombia raised preliminary objections to the jurisdiction of the Court. The ICJ found that it had jurisdiction to adjudicate the dispute based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogot. The Court fixed the timelimit for filing Colombia's CounterMemorial.

Context:
- Nicaragua filed the Application on 26 November 2013.
- The violations of Nicaragua's sovereign rights and maritime zones were declared in the Court's Judgment of 19 November 2012.
- Colombia threatened to use force to implement these violations.
- The ICJ's jurisdiction was based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogot.
- The timelimit for filing Colombia's CounterMemorial was set for 17 November 2016.

Citations:
- Article 79, paragraph 5 of the Rules of Court (regarding preliminary objections)
- Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogot (basis of the Court's jurisdiction)
