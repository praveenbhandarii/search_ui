Case ID: ICJ/158
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2016
Key Parties Involved: Republic of the Marshall Islands, India
Case Name: Obligations concerning Negotiations relating to Cessation of the Nuclear Arms Race and to Nuclear Disarmament (Marshall Islands v. India)

Case Summary:
The Republic of the Marshall Islands filed applications against nine states, including India, accusing them of not fulfilling their obligations regarding the cessation of the nuclear arms race and nuclear disarmament. The case revolves around India's alleged breach of its obligations under the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons (NPT). The Marshall Islands argues that India has violated its obligations under customary international law and Article VI of the NPT. India, on the other hand, objects to the jurisdiction of the ICJ, claiming that there is no existing dispute between the parties.

Context:
- The Marshall Islands alleges that India has failed to comply with its obligations under customary international law and the NPT regarding nuclear disarmament and the cessation of the nuclear arms race.
- India argues that there is no legal dispute between the parties and objects to the jurisdiction of the ICJ.
- The ICJ held that the statements made by the Marshall Islands and India's conduct did not establish the existence of a dispute.
- The Court dismissed the case for lack of evidence demonstrating the existence of a dispute.
