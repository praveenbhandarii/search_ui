Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 20140303
Key Parties Involved: Timor-Leste, Australia
Case Name: Questions relating to the Seizure and Detention of Certain Documents and Data (Timor-Leste v. Australia)

Case Summary:
Timor-Leste initiated proceedings against Australia regarding the seizure and detention of documents, data, and other property belonging to Timor-Leste or protected under international law. The items were allegedly seized from the offices of Timor-Leste's legal advisers in Australia. Timor-Leste claimed that the seized materials included correspondence related to a pending arbitration under the Timor Sea Treaty. The jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice (ICJ) was invoked by both parties based on their respective declarations under Article 36(2) of the Statute.

Context:
- Timor-Leste alleged that Australia seized its documents and data under a warrant issued under the Australian Security Intelligence Organisation Act of 1979.
- The seized materials were claimed to contain correspondence between Timor-Leste's government and its legal advisers regarding the Timor Sea Treaty.
- Timor-Leste invoked its declaration under Article 36(2) of the Statute as the basis for the ICJ's jurisdiction.
- Australia also invoked its declaration under the same provision.
