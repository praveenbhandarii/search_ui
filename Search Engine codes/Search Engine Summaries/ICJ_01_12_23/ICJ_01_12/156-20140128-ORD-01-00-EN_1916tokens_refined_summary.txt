Case ID: ICJ/156
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2014
Key Parties Involved: Timor-Leste, Australia
Case Name: Questions relating to the Seizure and Detention of Certain Documents and Data (Timor-Leste v. Australia)

Case Summary:
Timor-Leste initiated proceedings against Australia regarding the seizure and detention of documents, data, and other property belonging to Timor-Leste or protected under international law. These items were allegedly seized from the offices of one of Timor-Leste's legal advisers in the Australian Capital Territory. Timor-Leste claimed that the seized items included correspondence between the Timor-Leste government and its legal advisers related to a pending arbitration under the 2002 Timor Sea Treaty between Timor-Leste and Australia. Timor-Leste invoked its declaration under Article 36, paragraph 2 of the Statute as the basis for the Court's jurisdiction.

Context:
- Timor-Leste filed the case on December 17, 2013.
- The seizure and detention were carried out by Australian agents.
- The warrant for the seizure was issued under Article 25 of the Australian Security Intelligence Organisation Act of 1979.
- Timor-Leste argued that the seized items were protected under international law.
- The case involves a dispute between Timor-Leste and Australia regarding the Timor Sea Treaty of 2002.
- Timor-Leste requested the Court to address the lack of reasons for Australia's request for a stay, insufficient time to address the matter, and additional time to prepare a memorial.
- The Court denied Australia's request for a stay, set timelimits for filing written pleadings, and reserved the subsequent procedure for further decision.
- The document was signed by Peter Tomka, President, and Philippe Couvreur, Registrar.
- The case will be heard in English and French.
- The document was issued on January 28, 2014, and published by the United Nations.
- The treaty between Timor-Leste and Australia was signed on October 20, 2014, and published by the United Nations.
- The document includes the ICJ case number, names of judges involved, relevant case-related details, and was printed in France.
