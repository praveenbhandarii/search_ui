Case ID: ICJ/156
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2014
Key Parties Involved: Timor-Leste, Australia
Case Name: Questions relating to the Seizure and Detention of Certain Documents and Data (Timor-Leste v. Australia)

Case Summary:
Timor-Leste filed a case against Australia regarding the seizure and detention of documents, data, and other property belonging to Timor-Leste or protected under international law. The items were allegedly seized from the offices of one of Timor-Leste's legal advisers in Australia. Timor-Leste claimed that the seized items included correspondence related to a pending arbitration under the Timor Sea Treaty. Timor-Leste invoked its jurisdiction under Article 36, paragraph 2 of the Statute, as did Australia.

Context:
- Timor-Leste instituted proceedings against Australia on 17 December 2013.
- The seizure occurred under a warrant issued under Article 25 of the Australian Security Intelligence Organisation Act of 1979.
- The seized items include documents and data containing correspondence between Timor-Leste's government and its legal advisers.
- The correspondence is related to a pending arbitration under the 2002 Timor Sea Treaty between Timor-Leste and Australia.

Citations:
- Article 25 of the Australian Security Intelligence Organisation Act of 1979
- Article 36, paragraph 2 of the Statute
