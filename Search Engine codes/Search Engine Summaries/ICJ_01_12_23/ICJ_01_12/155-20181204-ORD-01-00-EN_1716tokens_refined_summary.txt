Case ID: ICJ/155
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2018
Key Parties Involved: Republic of Nicaragua (Nicaragua), Republic of Colombia (Colombia)
Case Name: Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Summary:
Nicaragua filed an Application on 26 November 2013, initiating proceedings against Colombia. The dispute revolves around the violations of Nicaragua's sovereign rights and maritime zones, as declared by the Court's Judgment of 19 November 2012 in the case concerning Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia). Nicaragua also alleges that Colombia has threatened to use force to implement these violations.

Context:
- The case concerns alleged violations of Nicaragua's sovereign rights and maritime zones by Colombia.
- The ICJ declared that it has jurisdiction to rule on the dispute, based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogotá.
- Colombia's first two counterclaims were found inadmissible, while the third and fourth counterclaims were found admissible.
- Various timelimits were set by the Court for the filing of pleadings, including the CounterMemorial of Colombia on 17 November 2016, Nicaragua's Reply on 15 May 2018, and Colombia's Rejoinder on 15 November 2018.
- Nicaragua requested permission to file an additional pleading solely related to the Colombian counterclaims, which was granted by the Court with a timelimit of 4 March 2019.
- The document was issued on 4 December 2018.

Citations:
- Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogotá
