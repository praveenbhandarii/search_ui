Case ID: ICJ/153
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 15 July 2014
Key Parties Involved: Plurinational State of Bolivia, Republic of Chile
Case Name: Obligation to Negotiate Access to the Pacific Ocean (Bolivia v. Chile)

Case Summary:
The case involves a dispute between Bolivia and Chile regarding Chile's obligation to negotiate in good faith and effectively with Bolivia to grant it fully sovereign access to the Pacific Ocean. Bolivia claims that Chile has committed itself to negotiate a sovereign access to the sea for Bolivia through agreements, diplomatic practice, and declarations by its highest-level representatives. Bolivia asserts that Chile has not fulfilled this obligation and denies its existence. Bolivia invoked Article XXXI of the American Treaty on Pacific Settlement as the basis for the jurisdiction of the Court.

Context:
- Bolivia instituted proceedings against Chile before the International Court of Justice on 24 April 2013.
- Bolivia asserts that Chile has not complied with its obligation to negotiate access to the Pacific Ocean.
- The jurisdiction of the Court is invoked based on Article XXXI of the American Treaty on Pacific Settlement.
- Chile filed a preliminary objection to the jurisdiction of the Court on 15 July 2014.
- The Plurinational State of Bolivia was given until 14 November 2014 to present its written statement on the preliminary objection.
- The subsequent procedure for further decision was reserved.
- Timelimits were fixed for the filing of a Memorial by Bolivia and a Counter-Memorial by Chile.
- The document is available in French and English, with the French text being authoritative.

Citations:
- Article 48 of the Statute of the Court
- Article 79, paragraphs 1 and 5 of the Rules of Court.
