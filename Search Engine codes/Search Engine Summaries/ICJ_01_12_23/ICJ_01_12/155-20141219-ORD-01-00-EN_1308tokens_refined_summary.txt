Case ID: ICJ/155
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2014
Key Parties Involved: Nicaragua, Colombia
Case Name: Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Summary:
Nicaragua filed an application against Colombia, alleging violations of its sovereign rights and maritime zones as declared by the Court's Judgment in a previous case. Nicaragua also claimed that Colombia threatened to use force to implement these violations. Colombia raised preliminary objections to the jurisdiction of the Court. The proceedings on the merits were suspended until Nicaragua presents its written statement of observations and submissions on the preliminary objections.

Context:
- Nicaragua filed an application against Colombia regarding violations of its sovereign rights and maritime zones.
- Colombia raised preliminary objections to the jurisdiction of the Court.
- Proceedings on the merits were suspended until Nicaragua presents its written statement on the preliminary objections.
- The timelimit for Nicaragua to present the written statement was set for April 20, 2015.

Citations:
- Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia), Order of December 19, 2014, I.C.J. Reports 2014, p. 485.
- Article 79, paragraph 5 of the Rules of Court (suspending proceedings on the merits until Nicaragua presents a written statement of its observations and submissions on the preliminary objections).
- Practice Direction V (setting a timelimit of four months from the date of the filing of preliminary objections for the presentation of such a written statement).
