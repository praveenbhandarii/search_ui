Case ID: ICJ/172
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2019
Key Parties Involved: Qatar, United Arab Emirates (UAE)
Case Name: Application of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (Qatar v. United Arab Emirates)

Case Summary:
Qatar instituted proceedings against the UAE for alleged violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination. The dispute revolves around whether the present case is premature and whether the dispute resolution mechanism provided by the Convention should be exhausted before bringing the case to the ICJ. Qatar argues that the relief sought before the ICJ is different from that sought before the CERD Committee. The document discusses the doctrine of lis pendens and the quasijudicial character of the dispute resolution mechanism established by CERD.

Context:
- The case concerns alleged racial discrimination by the UAE against Qatar.
- Qatar requests the ICJ to indicate provisional measures with respect to the UAE.
- The document cites the case of Georgia v. Russian Federation as a reference.
- The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (CERD) provides multiple methods of dispute settlement, including ad hoc conciliation commissions and the CERD Committee.
- The document explores the possible interpretation of Article 22 of CERD, which suggests that the dispute resolution mechanism provided by the Convention should be exhausted before bringing the case to the ICJ.
