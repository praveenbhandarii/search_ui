Case ID: ICJ/155
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2017
Key Parties Involved: Nicaragua, Colombia
Case Name: Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Summary:
Nicaragua filed an application against Colombia in 2013, alleging violations of its sovereign rights and maritime zones as declared by a previous court judgment. The dispute also involved the threat of force by Colombia to implement these violations. This document is an order from the ICJ related to the case.

Context:
- The case concerns violations of Nicaragua's sovereign rights and maritime zones declared in a previous court judgment.
- Nicaragua alleges that Colombia threatened to use force to implement these violations.
- The document discusses the jurisdiction of the ICJ over counterclaims and the requirement of a direct connection between the counterclaim and the subject matter of the principal claim.
- Christopher Greenwood, one of the judges, disagrees with the main decision regarding Colombia's fourth counterclaim, stating that it lacks a direct connection with the subject matter of the principal claim.
- The dispute also involves Nicaragua's decree establishing straight baselines.
