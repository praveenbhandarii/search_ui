Case Name: Appeal relating to the Jurisdiction of the ICAO Council under Article II, Section 2, of the 1944 International Air Services Transit Agreement (Bahrain, Egypt and United Arab Emirates v. Qatar)

Case Summary:
This case involves an appeal filed by the Kingdom of Bahrain, the Arab Republic of Egypt, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates (Appellants) against the decision rendered by the Council of the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO Council) on 29 June 2018. The decision was made in proceedings commenced by the State of Qatar against these four States on 30 October 2017. The appeal challenges the jurisdiction of the ICAO Council under Article II, Section 2, of the 1944 International Air Services Transit Agreement (IASTA). The Appellants raise grounds of appeal related to the rejection of their preliminary objections and alleged lack of due process in the procedure before the ICAO Council. The International Court of Justice (ICJ) dismissed the appeal in its entirety.

Context:
- The dispute arose from the alleged violation of the International Air Services Transit Agreement (IASTA) by the State of Qatar.
- The State of Qatar initiated proceedings against the Kingdom of Bahrain, the Arab Republic of Egypt, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates before the ICAO Council.
- The ICAO Council rejected the preliminary objections raised by the Appellants regarding its jurisdiction under Article II, Section 2, of the IASTA.
- The Appellants appealed the decision of the ICAO Council to the ICJ, challenging its jurisdiction and alleging lack of due process.
