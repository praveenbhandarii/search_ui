Case ID: ICJ/158
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2014
Key Parties Involved: Republic of the Marshall Islands, India
Case Name: Obligations concerning Negotiations relating to Cessation of the Nuclear Arms Race and to Nuclear Disarmament (Marshall Islands v. India)

Case Summary:
The Republic of the Marshall Islands filed Applications against nine states, including India, accusing them of not fulfilling their obligations regarding the cessation of the nuclear arms race and nuclear disarmament. This document provides information about the case, including the judges, registrar, legal provisions, jurisdiction, agents, and important dates.

Context:
- The Republic of the Marshall Islands accused India of breaching obligations related to the cessation of the nuclear arms race and nuclear disarmament.
- The Court's jurisdiction is based on declarations made by both parties in accordance with Article 36, paragraph 2, of the Statute.
- India informed the Court that it believes the ICJ does not have jurisdiction in the alleged dispute.
- The written pleadings will focus on the Court's jurisdiction, with specific deadlines for the Memorial of the Republic of the Marshall Islands and the Counter-Memorial of the Republic of India.

Citations:
- Article 48 of the Statute of the Court
- Articles 44, 48, and 79, paragraphs 2 and 3, of the Rules of Court
- Obligations Relatives des Negotiations Concernant La Cessation de la Course aux Armes Nuclaires et Le Dsarmement Nuclaire (Les Marshall c. Inde), Ordonnance du 16 juin 2014 (ICJ Reports 2014, p. 464)
