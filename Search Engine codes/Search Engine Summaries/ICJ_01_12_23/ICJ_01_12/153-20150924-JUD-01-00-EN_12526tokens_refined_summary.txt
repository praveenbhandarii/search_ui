Case ID: ICJ/153
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2015
Key Parties Involved: Bolivia, Chile
Case Name: Obligation to Negotiate Access to the Pacific Ocean (Bolivia v. Chile)

Case Summary:
The case revolves around Bolivia's claim that Chile has an obligation to negotiate in good faith and effectively to grant Bolivia fully sovereign access to the Pacific Ocean. Bolivia argues that Chile has not complied with this obligation and denies its existence. Bolivia invoked Article XXXI of the American Treaty on Pacific Settlement (Pact of Bogotá) as the basis for the jurisdiction of the Court. Chile raised a preliminary objection regarding the Court's jurisdiction.

Context:
- Bolivia instituted proceedings against Chile before the ICJ in 2013, alleging Chile's obligation to negotiate access to the Pacific Ocean.
- Bolivia asserts that Chile committed itself to negotiate sovereign access to the sea for Bolivia through agreements, diplomatic practice, and declarations.
- The ICJ's jurisdiction is invoked based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogotá.
- The case involves the interpretation of the 1904 Peace Treaty and the existence and content of an alleged obligation to negotiate sovereign access to the sea.
- Chile objects to the Court's jurisdiction, claiming that the matters in dispute are settled by arrangement between the parties and governed by the 1904 Peace Treaty.

Citations:
- Article XXXI of the American Treaty on Pacific Settlement (Pact of Bogotá)
- 1904 Peace Treaty between Chile and Bolivia
