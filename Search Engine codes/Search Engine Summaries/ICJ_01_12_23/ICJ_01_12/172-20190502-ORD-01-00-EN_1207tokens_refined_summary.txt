Case ID: ICJ/172
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2019
Key Parties Involved: Qatar, United Arab Emirates (UAE)
Case Name: Application of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (Qatar v. United Arab Emirates)

Case Summary:
Qatar filed a case against the UAE on June 11, 2018, alleging violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination. This document is an order issued by the ICJ in response to preliminary objections raised by the UAE regarding the court's jurisdiction and the admissibility of the application. The order requires Qatar to present a written statement addressing the objections within four months. The subsequent procedure is reserved for further decision.

Context:
- The ICJ is the primary judicial organ of the United Nations.
- Preliminary objections are procedural challenges to the court's jurisdiction or the admissibility of a case.
- The case reference cited at the end of the order indicates the specific court document where the preliminary objections were raised.
