Case ID: ICJ/158
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: October 5, 2016
Key Parties Involved: Marshall Islands, India
Case Name: Obligations concerning Negotiations relating to Cessation of the Nuclear Arms Race and to Nuclear Disarmament (Marshall Islands v. India)

Case Summary:
The Republic of the Marshall Islands filed applications against nine states, including India, accusing them of not fulfilling their obligations regarding the cessation of the nuclear arms race and nuclear disarmament. The case revolves around India's alleged failure to pursue negotiations in good faith for nuclear disarmament. The International Court of Justice (ICJ) found that there is no jurisdiction to consider the application lodged by the Marshall Islands and cannot proceed to the merits of the case. However, Judge Canado Trindade dissented from the majority decision and provided a dissenting opinion, arguing that a dispute exists between the parties and that the ICJ should have jurisdiction to consider the application.

Context:
- The Marshall Islands filed applications against nine states, including India, for not fulfilling their obligations regarding nuclear disarmament.
- The ICJ found that there is no jurisdiction to consider the application lodged by the Marshall Islands.
- Judge Canado Trindade dissented from the majority decision and provided a dissenting opinion, arguing for the existence of a dispute and the ICJ's jurisdiction.
