Case ID: ICJ/153
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 20181001
Key Parties Involved: Republic of Bolivia, Republic of Chile
Case Name: Obligation to Negotiate Access to the Pacific Ocean (Bolivia v. Chile)

Case Summary:
The case involves a dispute between Bolivia and Chile regarding Chile's obligation to negotiate in good faith and effectively with Bolivia to grant it fully sovereign access to the Pacific Ocean. Bolivia claims that Chile has committed itself to negotiate a sovereign access to the sea for Bolivia but has not fulfilled this obligation. Bolivia invoked Article XXXI of the American Treaty on Pacific Settlement as the basis for the jurisdiction of the Court.

Context:
- Bolivia instituted proceedings against Chile before the ICJ on April 24, 2013.
- Bolivia asserts that Chile has not complied with its obligation to negotiate access to the Pacific Ocean and denies the existence of this obligation.
- Bolivia argues that Chile has made commitments through agreements, diplomatic practice, and declarations to negotiate a sovereign access to the sea for Bolivia.
- The ICJ judgment addresses the legal obligation of Chile to negotiate Bolivia's sovereign access to the Pacific Ocean.
