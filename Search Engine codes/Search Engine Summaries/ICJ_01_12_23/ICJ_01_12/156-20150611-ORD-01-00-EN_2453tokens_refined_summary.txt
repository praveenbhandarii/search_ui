Case ID: ICJ/156
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2015
Key Parties Involved: Timor-Leste, Australia
Case Name: Questions relating to the Seizure and Detention of Certain Documents and Data (Timor-Leste v. Australia)

Case Summary:
Timor-Leste initiated proceedings against Australia regarding the seizure and detention of documents, data, and other property belonging to Timor-Leste or protected under international law. The items were allegedly seized from the offices of one of Timor-Leste's legal advisers in the Australian Capital Territory. Timor-Leste claimed that the seized items included correspondence between the Timor-Leste government and its legal advisers related to a pending arbitration under the Timor Sea Treaty. Timor-Leste invoked its jurisdiction under Article 36, paragraph 2, of the Statute, as well as Australia's jurisdiction declaration made under the same provision.

Context:
- Timor-Leste instituted proceedings against Australia on 17 December 2013.
- The seizure of documents and data took place on 3 December 2013.
- The International Court of Justice indicated provisional measures on 3 March 2014.
- Timelimits for filing the Memorial and CounterMemorial were fixed on 28 January 2014.
- Oral proceedings were scheduled to open on 17 September 2014 but were postponed upon request.
- Australia indicated its intention to return the seized documents on 25 March 2015.
- The ICJ modified the provisional measures on 22 April 2015.
- Australia returned the seized documents and data on 12 May 2015.
- Timor-Leste discontinued the proceedings on 2 June 2015.
- The President of the Court fixed a time limit for Australia to state whether it objected to the discontinuance on 10 June 2015.

Citations:
