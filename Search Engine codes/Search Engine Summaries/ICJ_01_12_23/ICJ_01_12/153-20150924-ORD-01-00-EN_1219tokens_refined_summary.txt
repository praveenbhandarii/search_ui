Case ID: ICJ/153
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: September 24, 2015
Key Parties Involved: Plurinational State of Bolivia, Republic of Chile
Case Name: Obligation to Negotiate Access to the Pacific Ocean (Bolivia v. Chile)

Case Summary:
The case involves a dispute between Bolivia and Chile regarding Chile's obligation to negotiate in good faith and effectively with Bolivia to grant it fully sovereign access to the Pacific Ocean. Bolivia claims that Chile has committed itself to negotiate such access through agreements, diplomatic practice, and declarations by its highest-level representatives. Bolivia argues that Chile has not fulfilled this obligation and denies its existence. Bolivia invoked Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogotá as the basis for the jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice (ICJ).

Context:
- Bolivia filed the case against Chile on April 24, 2013.
- Bolivia asserts that Chile has not complied with its obligation to negotiate access to the Pacific Ocean.
- The ICJ found that it has jurisdiction based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogotá.
- The ICJ fixed July 25, 2016, as the deadline for the filing of the CounterMemorial by the Republic of Chile.
- The subsequent procedure was reserved for further decision.
- The document is written in English and French, with the English text being authoritative.
- The judgment was issued on September 24, 2015, and the deadline for filing the CounterMemorial was set for July 25, 2016.
