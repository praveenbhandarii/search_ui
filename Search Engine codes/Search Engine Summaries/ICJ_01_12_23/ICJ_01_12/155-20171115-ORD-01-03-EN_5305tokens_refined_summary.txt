Case ID: ICJ/155
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 2017
Key Parties Involved: Nicaragua, Colombia
Case Name: Alleged Violations of Sovereign Rights and Maritime Spaces in the Caribbean Sea (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Summary:
Nicaragua filed an application against Colombia in 2013, alleging violations of its sovereign rights and maritime zones. The dispute arose from a previous judgment by the ICJ in 2012 regarding a territorial and maritime dispute between the two countries. Nicaragua also claimed that Colombia threatened to use force to implement these violations.

Context:
- The ICJ has the authority to consider counterclaims in interstate disputes.
- Counterclaims have the same status as the main claims.
- The ICJ can make its own findings and determinations beyond the submissions of the parties.
- The ICJ has previously considered the needs of local populations in interstate disputes.
- Relevant cases include the Pulp Mills on the River Uruguay (Argentina v. Uruguay) and the Maritime Dispute (Peru v. Chile).

Citations:
- ICJ Reports 2010 (I)
- Case concerning Pulp Mills on the River Uruguay (Argentina v. Uruguay)
- Case concerning the Maritime Dispute (Peru v. Chile)

Additional Information:
- Judge Canado Trinidade made a declaration in 2017 emphasizing the importance of human solidarity and the need for juridical security of boundaries, land, and maritime spaces in the context of state sovereignty and artisanal fishing for subsistence.
- The statement argues for a new jus gentium that considers the needs of human beings and the principles of human solidarity, supported by the foundation of jus gentium in the thinking of the founding fathers of the law of nations.
- The statement is addressed to the ICJ.
