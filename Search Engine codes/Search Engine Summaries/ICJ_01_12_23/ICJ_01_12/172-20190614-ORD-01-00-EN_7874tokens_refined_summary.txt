Case ID: ICJ/172
Court/Tribunal: International Court of Justice (ICJ)
Document Date: 23 July 2018
Key Parties Involved: Qatar, United Arab Emirates (UAE)
Case Name: Application of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (Qatar v. United Arab Emirates)

Case Summary:
Qatar instituted proceedings against the UAE for alleged violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (CERD). Qatar accused the UAE of expelling all Qataris from the country, prohibiting their entry, and engaging in other unlawful actions. The case is ongoing, with Qatar seeking the ICJ's jurisdiction on Article 36, paragraph 1, of the Statute of the Court and Article 22 of CERD.

Context:
- Qatar filed the application on 11 June 2018.
- The ICJ ordered provisional measures in favor of Qatar on 23 July 2018.
- The UAE requested Qatar to withdraw its communication submitted to the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD Committee) and take measures to terminate consideration of the case by the committee.
- The UAE also requested Qatar to unblock access to a website for Qatari citizens to apply for a permit to return to the UAE and to stop disseminating false accusations regarding the UAE.
- The ICJ rejected the UAE's request for provisional measures, stating that the conditions for indication of provisional measures were not met and the UAE's rights asserted were not plausible under CERD.

Citations:
- Articles 2, 4, 5, 6, and 7 of CERD
- Order of 23 July 2018 (I.C.J. Reports 2018 (II)), pp. 420-421, para. 39
