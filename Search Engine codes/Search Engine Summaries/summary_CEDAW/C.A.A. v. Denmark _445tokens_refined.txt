Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/81/D/163/2020
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved: NielsErik Hansen (alleged victim) vs. Denmark (state party)
- Case Name: NielsErik Hansen v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The alleged victim, NielsErik Hansen, submitted a communication to the UNCEDAW claiming that his deportation to Iraq would violate his rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by NielsErik Hansen on November 18, 2020. The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication at its eighty-first session (February 7-25, 2022) since the author was granted a residence permit in Denmark and does not face a risk of removal.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the deportation of NielsErik Hansen to Iraq, claiming a violation of his rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. However, the Committee's decision to discontinue consideration was based on the author being granted a residence permit in Denmark and no longer facing a risk of removal.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 163/2020 at its eighty-first session on February 23, 2022, due to the author being granted a residence permit in Denmark and no longer facing a risk of removal.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for cases involving the deportation of individuals claiming a violation of their rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. It highlights the importance of considering the current circumstances of the alleged victim in such cases.
