Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/87/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: O.M. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Valentina Mudrik and Mariana Yevsyukova)
  - Party B: Ukraine
- Case Name: O.M. v. Ukraine
- Case Summary: O.M., a Ukrainian citizen, alleges that Ukraine violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) by subjecting her to physical, psychological, and financial abuse during her marriage to a Jordanian national, and by failing to provide her with adequate protection and support after she fled the country.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted by O.M. on September 1, 2014.
- No procedural issues were identified.
- The substantive issue was the violation of rights under CEDAW.
- The subject matter included domestic violence, marital rape, and discrimination against women in marriage and family relations.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal details of the case included the violation of articles 2(a)(d) and (f), 5(a) and (b), and 16(c)(e) and (g) of CEDAW.
- The case addressed the failure of Ukraine to protect O.M.'s rights under the Convention and the general recommendations of CEDAW.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee declared part of the communication admissible and proceeded with its examination of the merits.
- The Committee found that the State party is responsible for obligations regarding individuals under its jurisdiction and cannot be held responsible for discrimination within the jurisdiction of another country.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for the responsibility of a State party to protect its citizens from discrimination and violence, even in foreign jurisdictions.
- The Committee's decision highlights the importance of consular protection in cases of gender-based violence and child custody disputes.

Citations and References:
- The legal citations and references include the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Committee's general recommendations on violence against women, equality in marriage and family relations, and the core obligations of States parties under the Convention.
