Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/65/D/61/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2016
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Alleged victim: P.H.A. (represented by counsel, Niels-Erik Hansen)
  - State party: Denmark
- Case Name: P.H.A. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: P.H.A., a national of the Islamic Republic of Iran, faces deportation from Denmark after her asylum application was rejected. She claims that her deportation would constitute a violation by Denmark of articles 1, 2 (c) and (d), 3, 12, and 15 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and of the Committee's general recommendations No. 12 (1989) and No. 19 (1992) on violence against women.

Procedural History:
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women adopted a decision on admissibility on 7 November 2016.
- The Committee found that the author's communication was admissible and that her deportation would constitute a violation of the Convention and the Committees general recommendations.
- The Committee requested the State party not to deport the author pending the examination of her case on the merits.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The case addresses the admissibility of the communication and the substantive issue of violation of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and the Committee's general recommendations No. 12 (1989) and No. 19 (1992) on violence against women.
- The legal details include the articles of the Covenant, the procedural issue of admissibility, and the substantive issue of violation of the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found the author's communication admissible and requested the State party not to deport the author pending the examination of her case on the merits.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the examination of asylum cases involving gender-based discrimination and violence against women under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Citations and References:
- The decision is based on the legal provisions of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and the Committee's general recommendations No. 12 (1989) and No. 19 (1992) on violence against women.
