Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/81/D/160/2020
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: M. M. (represented by counsel, S. Motz, F. de Weck, and L. Hungerbhler)
  - Party B: State party, Switzerland
- Case Name: M. M. v. Switzerland
- Case Summary: The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women considered a communication (No. 160/2020) submitted by M.M., represented by counsel, regarding the risk of discrimination and gender-based violence upon deportation to Greece. The communication was submitted under the Optional Protocol of the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication since the author was granted a residence permit in Switzerland and no longer faced the risk of removal.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted on August 24, 2020, under the Optional Protocol of the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee adopted the decision at its eighty-first session (7-25 February 2022).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issues addressed in the communication included the risk of discrimination and gender-based violence upon deportation to Greece. The Committee considered the communication and decided to discontinue its consideration due to the fact that the author was granted a residence permit in Switzerland and no longer faces the risk of removal.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication, as the author was no longer at risk of removal from Switzerland.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for cases involving the risk of discrimination and gender-based violence upon deportation, particularly in the context of the Optional Protocol of the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Citations and References:
