  - Party A: O. V. J. (the author and victim)
  - Party B: Denmark

Case Name: O. V. J. v. Denmark

Procedural History:
The author, O. V. J., a Russian national, submitted a communication to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women alleging violations of her and her daughter's rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The author was not represented by counsel.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The author claimed a violation of articles 1, 2 (d), 5 and 16 (1) (d) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, arguing that the decisions of the Danish courts were discriminatory and violated her and her daughter's rights under the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found the communication inadmissible under article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol as insufficiently substantiated. The Committee decided not to examine any other inadmissibility grounds and declared the communication inadmissible.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the challenges faced by foreign women in seeking remedy for denial of custody and alleged gender-based discrimination in Denmark. It also underscores the importance of providing sufficient and relevant documentation and information in support of a communication to the Committee.
