Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/82/D/149/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: Year 2022
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: N.A.E. (the author and victim)
  - Party B: Spain (the state party)
- Case Name: N.A.E. v. Spain

Procedural History:
The case involves a Spanish national, N.A.E., who alleges that Spain violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) due to obstetric violence experienced during childbirth. The communication was submitted by N.A.E. on 10 November 2018, and the views were adopted by the Committee on 27 June 2022.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include violations of articles 2, 3, 5, and 12 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee's analysis focused on the lack of informed consent, failure to provide adequate care, and the violation of the author's autonomy and dignity during the birthing process.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the State party violated N.A.E.'s rights under the Convention and recommended appropriate reparation, including financial compensation for the physical and psychological harm suffered. The Committee also recommended measures to ensure women's rights to safe motherhood and access to appropriate obstetric services, as well as the provision of adequate information and obtaining free, prior, and informed consent during childbirth.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for addressing obstetric violence as a form of discrimination and violence against women. It highlights the need for improved patient care and respect for human rights in healthcare settings, as well as the importance of informed consent and autonomy during childbirth.

Citations and References:
The Committee's decision is based on the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, relevant international human rights instruments, and reports from experts in the field of obstetric violence. The case also references previous rulings and recommendations by the Committee on similar cases.
