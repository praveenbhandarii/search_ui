Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/76/D/116/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 26 August 2020
- Key Parties Involved: S.H. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, TRIAL International) vs. Bosnia and Herzegovina
- Case Name: S.H. v. Bosnia and Herzegovina
- Case Summary: S.H., a Bosnian national of Croat ethnicity, alleges that Bosnia and Herzegovina has violated her rights under articles 1, 2 (a)(f), 3, 12, 13 (a) and (b) and 15 (1) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. She claims that she was subjected to sexual violence by a member of the Bosnian Serb forces in 1995, during the conflict in the former Yugoslavia, and that the State party has failed to act with due diligence for the timely and effective investigation of the crime.

Procedural History:
- The author, S.H., submitted the communication to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) on 27 January 2017.
- The Committee adopted its views on 9 July 2020.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The case addresses issues related to the State party's failure to act with due diligence for the timely and effective investigation of sexual violence, as well as the denial of recognition as a civilian victim of armed conflict and access to social support and benefits.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the State party violated the author's rights under the Convention and the Optional Protocol by failing to provide her with adequate compensation, support, and redress for the harm she suffered.

Impact and Significance:
- The Committee's decision sets a precedent for addressing cases of sexual violence during armed conflicts and emphasizes the importance of providing effective remedies and support to victims.
