Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/82/D/148/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved: A.F. (the author and victim) vs. Italy (the state party)
- Case Name: A.F. v. Italy
- Case Summary: A.F., an Italian national, alleges violations by the State party of her rights under articles 2 (b), (d), and (f); 5 (a); and 15 (1) of the Convention. She was assaulted by her ex-husband and subsequently sexually assaulted by a police officer, C.C., who had been sent to her home to investigate the domestic violence case.

Procedural History:
- The Optional Protocol entered into force for Italy on 22 September 2000.
- The case was examined by the Committee at its eighty-second session (13 June-1 July 2022).
- The author is represented by counsel, M. Teresa Manente and M. Ilaria Boiano.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Violence against women, sexual assault, failure to protect and investigate.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 2 (b), (d), and (f); 5 (a); and 15 (1) of the Convention.
- Court's Analysis: The Committee found that the Regional Court's decision to overturn the conviction of the perpetrator was based on distorted perceptions and preconceived beliefs and myths rather than relevant facts, leading to a miscarriage of justice and the revictimization of the author.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee concluded that the author's rights under articles 2(b), (d), and (f), 3, 5, and 15 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women were violated.
- The Committee recommended that the State party recognize the author's suffering and provide appropriate compensation, ensure impartial and fair legal proceedings, and take concrete legislative measures to ensure the burden of proof is not unduly onerous.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for addressing gender-based stereotypes and biases in the judicial system and ensuring equal access to justice for victims of gender-based violence.
- The Committee's recommendations have broader legal implications for addressing discrimination and revictimization in cases of sexual and gender-based violence.

Citations and References:
- The Committee's views and recommendations will be published and widely distributed to reach all relevant sectors of society.
