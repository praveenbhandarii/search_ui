Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/50/D/22/2009
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2011
- Key Parties Involved:
  - T.P.F. (represented by the Centre for Reproductive Rights and the Centre for the Promotion and Protection of Sexual and Reproductive Rights)
  - L.C. (daughter of T.P.F.)
  - Peru (State party)
- Case Name: T.P.F. v. Peru
- Case Summary: T.P.F. submitted a communication to the UNCEDAW on behalf of her daughter L.C., claiming that Peru had violated articles 1, 2 (c) and (f), 3, 5, 12, and 16 (e) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The communication alleged that L.C. had been a victim of sexual abuse and had become pregnant at the age of 13, and that the hospital had postponed her surgery due to her pregnancy. T.P.F. requested the hospital officials to carry out a legal termination of the pregnancy in accordance with article 119 of the Penal Code.

Procedural History:
The case involves a communication submitted by T.P.F. to the UNCEDAW on behalf of her daughter L.C., alleging violations of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women by Peru. The communication was initially submitted in 2009, and the Committee adopted Views under article 7, paragraph 3, of the Optional Protocol, finding Peru in violation of the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the denial of access to therapeutic abortion, the lack of effective judicial and administrative remedies, and the failure of the State party to provide adequate and effective guarantees in its legislation to protect the rights of women. The relevant legal provisions include articles 1, 2 (c) and (f), 3, 5, 12, and 16 (e) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that Peru had violated the rights of L.C. under the Convention, including the right to access safe and legal abortion, the right to health, and the right to freedom from discrimination. The Committee also declared the communication admissible and proceeded to consider the merits of the case.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the protection of women's reproductive rights and access to safe and legal abortion. It highlights the importance of effective judicial and administrative remedies in cases of violations of women's rights under international conventions.

Citations and References:
The Committee's decision is based on the provisions of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and its general recommendation No. 24. The case also references international jurisprudence and the Code of Constitutional Procedure to support the arguments presented.
