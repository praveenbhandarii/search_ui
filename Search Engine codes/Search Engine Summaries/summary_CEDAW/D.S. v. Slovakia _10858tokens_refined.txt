Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/65/D/66/2014
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2016
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: D.S. (represented by counsel, Vanda Durbakova, Center for Civil and Human Rights)
  - Party B: Slovakia
- Case Name: D.S. v. Slovakia

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: 8 February 2013 (initial submission)
- Date of adoption of decision: 7 November 2016
- Jurisdiction and Competence: Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Discrimination based on gender and marital and family status
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 2 (a), (c) and (e) read in conjunction with articles 1 and 11 (1) (a) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Court's Analysis: The author claimed that her dismissal was discriminatory and violated the principle of equal treatment under national legislation.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the State party violated the author's rights under the Convention and recommended an effective remedy, including monetary compensation and legal costs and expenses.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for addressing gender-based discrimination in employment and highlights the importance of shifting the burden of proof in cases of discrimination.
