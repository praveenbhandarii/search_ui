Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/53/D/38/2012
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2012
- Key Parties Involved: Mr. J.S. (the author and victim) vs. United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (the state party)
- Case Name: Mr. J.S. v. United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland

Procedural History:
The author, Mr. J.S., an Indian national, submitted a communication to the Committee claiming a violation of his rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) by the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. The Committee considered the communication and decided that it was inadmissible under article 4, paragraph 1, of the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include discrimination based on gender in the acquisition of nationality, the State party's compliance with the Convention, and the author's failure to exhaust domestic remedies. Relevant legal provisions include Articles 1, 2, 3, and 9 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW). The Committee's analysis focused on the author's claims of discrimination in the acquisition of British citizenship through his mother and the State party's arguments regarding the author's failure to meet the conditions set out by the State party's nationality laws.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee declared the communication inadmissible under article 4, paragraph 1, of the Optional Protocol. The decision was communicated to the State party and to the author.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for cases involving discrimination in the acquisition of nationality based on gender. It also highlights the importance of exhausting domestic remedies before submitting a communication to the Committee.

Citations and References:
The legal document references Articles 1, 2, 3, and 9 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Optional Protocol to the Convention. It also includes references to the British Nationality Act 1948, the Immigration Act 1971, the British Nationality Act 1981, and the Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002.
