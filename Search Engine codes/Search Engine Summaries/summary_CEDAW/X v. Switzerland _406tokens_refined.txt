Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/79/D/156/2020
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2020
- Key Parties Involved: X (alleged victim) vs. Switzerland (state party)
- Case Name: X v. Switzerland
- Case Summary: The author, X, submitted a communication to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women alleging that Switzerland had violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication as the author had obtained temporary authorization to stay in Switzerland and was not then at risk of removal.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted on September 5, 2019, and the Committee considered it at its meeting on 28 June 2021. The participating members of the Committee were listed.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue was the alleged violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee's decision was based on the author's temporary authorization to stay in Switzerland and the lack of risk of removal at that time.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the author's temporary authorization to stay in Switzerland and the absence of risk of removal.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for cases where the alleged victim has obtained temporary authorization to stay in the state party and is not at risk of removal. It highlights the importance of the author's current situation in determining the Committee's consideration of the communication.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 156/2020. The participating members of the Committee were also listed.
