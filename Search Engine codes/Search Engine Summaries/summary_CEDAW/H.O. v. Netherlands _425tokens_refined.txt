Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/85/D/178/2022
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2023
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Alleged victim: H.O. (represented by counsel, Jolla Bravo Mougn)
  - State party: Kingdom of the Netherlands
- Case Name: H.O. v. Kingdom of the Netherlands
- Case Summary: H.O. submitted a communication alleging discrimination against women by the Kingdom of the Netherlands. The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration after H.O. was granted a residence permit and was no longer at risk of deportation.

Procedural History:
The communication (No. 178/2022) was submitted by H.O. on 18 August 2021. The Committee considered the communication and decided to discontinue its consideration at its meeting on 19 May 2023.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed was discrimination against women in the context of a residence permit and deportation. The specific article of the Covenant and procedural issues were not specified in the document.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication after H.O. was granted a residence permit in the State party and was no longer at risk of deportation.

Impact and Significance:
The decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication has implications for similar cases involving discrimination against women in the context of immigration and deportation. It sets a precedent for the Committee's approach to cases where the alleged victim is no longer at risk.

Citations and References:
The United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Optional Protocol, as well as the participating members of the CEDAW Committee, are referenced in the document.
