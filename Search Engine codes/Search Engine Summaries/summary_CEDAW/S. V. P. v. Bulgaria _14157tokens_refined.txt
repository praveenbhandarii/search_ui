Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/53/D/31/2011
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2012
- Key Parties Involved:
  - S. V. P. (the author and victim)
  - Bulgaria (the state party)
- Case Name: S. V. P. v. Bulgaria
- Case Summary: The author, S. V. P., a Bulgarian national, alleges that her daughter, V. P. P., was a victim of sexual violence and that the State party violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
- The case involves a minor girl who was sexually molested by a man in 2004.
- The victim's family filed a civil lawsuit against the perpetrator for moral damages.
- The Regional Court in Pleven issued a ruling in favor of the victim's family, sentencing the perpetrator to pay 30,000 leva (15,000 euros) for moral damages.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The case raises questions about the adequacy of the criminal justice system's response to sexual violence against children.
- The delay in issuing the indictment and the relatively lenient sentence imposed on the perpetrator have been criticized by the victim's family and human rights advocates.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women found that Bulgaria violated the victim's rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Impact and Significance:
- The case illustrates the complexities and challenges of addressing sexual violence against children, particularly in cases where the perpetrator is known to the victim.
- It also underscores the need for a comprehensive approach that addresses both the criminal justice and civil law aspects of such cases.

Citations and References:
- Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Optional Protocol to the Convention
