Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/66/D/54/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 28 February 2017
- Key Parties Involved: E.W. (represented by counsel, Daniel Nrrung and Helge Nrrung) vs. State party: The author Denmark
- Case Name: E.W. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: E.W., a Chinese national, alleges that her deportation from Denmark would constitute a violation by Denmark of articles 1, 2, 3, 5 and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
The case involves a communication submitted by E.W. to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) against the State party, Denmark. The communication was initially submitted on 7 June 2013, and the decision was adopted on 28 February 2017.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the violation of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, specifically articles 1, 2, 3, 5, and 16. The Court analyzed the substantive issue of the deportation of E.W. from Denmark and the alleged violations of her rights.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the author's claims were insufficiently substantiated and lacked relevant information to support her claims. The Committee concluded that the communication was inadmissible under Article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for the admissibility of communications related to gender-based violence and persecution in asylum cases. It emphasizes the importance of providing relevant and substantiated information to support asylum claims.

Citations and References:
The decision is based on the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and relevant legal provisions related to asylum and gender-based violence. It also references the specific details of the case, including the author's claims and the State party's responses.
