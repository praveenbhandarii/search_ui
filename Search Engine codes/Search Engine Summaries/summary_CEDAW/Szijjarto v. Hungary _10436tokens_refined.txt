Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/36/D/4/2004
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2006
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Ms. A. S. (represented by the European Roma Rights Center and the Legal Defence Bureau for National and Ethnic Minorities)
  - Hungary
- Case Name: Ms. A. S. v. Hungary
- Case Summary: Ms. A. S., a Hungarian Roma woman, alleges that she was subjected to coerced sterilization by medical staff at a Hungarian hospital. The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women found that Hungary violated Ms. A. S.'s rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
The case was submitted by Ms. A. S. (represented by the European Roma Rights Center and the Legal Defence Bureau for National and Ethnic Minorities) to the United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women. The communication was considered admissible by the Committee, and the State party (Hungary) was given the opportunity to respond to the allegations.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include coerced sterilization, lack of informed consent, and violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee analyzed the facts presented by the author and the State party, as well as relevant international and regional human rights standards.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that Hungary violated Ms. A. S.'s rights under articles 10(h), 12, and 16(1)(e) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee recommended that Hungary provide appropriate compensation to Ms. A. S. and take measures to prevent similar violations in the future.

Impact and Significance:
The Committee's decision sets a precedent for cases of coerced sterilization and lack of informed consent, emphasizing the importance of upholding reproductive rights and preventing discrimination against women, particularly marginalized groups such as the Roma community.

Citations and References:
The Committee's decision is based on the relevant provisions of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, as well as international human rights standards and medical ethics principles. The decision also takes into account the submissions and arguments presented by both parties in the case.
