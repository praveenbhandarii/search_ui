Case Name: R.M. et al v. Switzerland
Case Summary:
The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women, during its seventy-second session, considered communication No. 97/2015, submitted by alleged victim R.M. et al, regarding an asylum case in Switzerland. The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication after being informed that the asylum case had been reopened and the family had been issued a permit to remain in Switzerland, thereby eliminating the risk of deportation. The decision was adopted under the Optional Protocol concerning communication No. 97/2015.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted on 6 October 2016, and the decision was adopted on 25 February 2019. The case was considered during the seventy-second session of the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the case was discrimination against women in the context of asylum and deportation. The Committee's decision was based on the information that the asylum case had been reopened and the family had been issued a permit to remain in Switzerland, leading to the discontinuation of the consideration of the communication.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 97/2015, as the asylum case had been reopened and the family had been issued a permit to remain in Switzerland, eliminating the risk of deportation.

Impact and Significance:
The decision has significant implications for the protection of women's rights in the context of asylum and deportation cases. It sets a precedent for addressing discrimination against women in similar situations and highlights the importance of ensuring the safety and security of individuals at risk of deportation.
