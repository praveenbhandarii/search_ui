Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/61/D/45/2012
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2015
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Anna Belousova (the author and victim)
  - Party B: Kazakhstan (the state party)
- Case Name: Anna Belousova v. Kazakhstan

Procedural History:
- The case was submitted by Anna Belousova, represented by counsel, Evgeny Tsepennikov and Anastasiya Miller, to the UNCEDAW in 2012.
- The case was related to the alleged violation of Anna Belousova's rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women by Kazakhstan.
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women adopted its views on the case in 2015.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal issues addressed in the case included violations of articles 2 (e), 5 (a), 11 and 14 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Court's analysis focused on the allegations of sexual harassment and exploitation in the workplace, as well as the lack of adequate investigation and protection for the author.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that Kazakhstan had violated Anna Belousova's rights and recommended that the state take appropriate measures to protect her from sexual harassment and exploitation in the workplace.

Impact and Significance:
- The case set a precedent for addressing sexual harassment and exploitation in the workplace under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Committee's decision highlighted the importance of providing adequate protection for victims of gender-based violence and discrimination in the workplace.
