Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/59/D/59/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2014
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Y. C. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, CityAdvokaterne)
  - Party B: Denmark
- Case Name: Y. C. v. Denmark

Procedural History:
- The author, Y. C., a Chinese national, sought asylum in Denmark and claimed that her deportation would constitute a violation of her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- Y. C. submitted the communication on 16 January 2013, and the decision was adopted by the Committee on 24 October 2014.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Admissibility of the communication, substantive issue of whether the State party's deportation of the author would constitute a violation of her rights under the Convention
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 1 to 3 and 5 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Court's Analysis: The Committee examined the admissibility of the communication separately from its merits and found it to be admissible.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee concluded that the State party's decision to deport Y. C. did not constitute a violation of her rights under the Convention or the Committee's general recommendation No. 19.
- The Committee decided not to accede to Y. C.'s request for interim measures of protection and found the communication to be admissible.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for cases involving asylum, deportation, freedom of religion, and violence against women under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
