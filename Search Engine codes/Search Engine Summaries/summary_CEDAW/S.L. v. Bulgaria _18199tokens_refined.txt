Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/99/2016
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: S.L. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Milena Kadieva and Zhaneta Borisova)
  - Party B: Bulgaria
- Case Name: S.L. v. Bulgaria
- Case Summary: S.L., a Bulgarian national, alleges that Bulgaria has violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) by failing to prevent and effectively investigate the severe physical and psychological violence committed by her former husband against her.

Procedural History:
The case involves a complaint submitted by S.L. to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) against Bulgaria for violating her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The State party has provided observations on admissibility and the merits of the case, and the Committee has found the communication admissible and proceeded to consider its merits.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal details of the case include the specific articles of the Covenant that were alleged to have been violated, the substantive issue of violence against women, including domestic violence, and the state's obligation to protect and investigate such violence. The case also addresses the lack of effective legal protection against gender-based violence and domestic violence in Bulgaria, as well as the failure of the authorities to prevent and effectively investigate the violence committed against S.L.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee has issued recommendations to Bulgaria, including providing the victim with adequate compensation, revising legislation to bring it into compliance with international human rights standards, and taking measures to prevent similar violations from being repeated. The Committee has also recommended amending the Protection against Domestic Violence Act to remove the one-month time limit and ease the burden of proof in favor of the victim.

Impact and Significance:
The case has broader legal implications for the protection of women's rights and the prevention of gender-based violence and domestic violence. The recommendations issued by the Committee have the potential to set precedents and have a significant impact on the legal framework and practices related to domestic violence in Bulgaria.

Citations and References:
The legal analysis and recommendations in the case are based on the relevant provisions of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, as well as the Committee's general recommendations and jurisprudence in similar cases.

Overall, the case of S.L. v. Bulgaria highlights the importance of addressing gender-based violence and domestic violence, and the need for effective legal protection and remedies for victims of such violence. The recommendations issued by the Committee have the potential to bring about positive changes in the legal framework and practices related to domestic violence in Bulgaria.
