Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/102/2016
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 10 September 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - J.D. et al. (the authors and victims)
  - Czech Republic (the state party)
- Case Name: J.D. et al. v. Czech Republic
- Case Summary: The authors, J.D. et al., are Czech nationals of Roma ethnicity who were subjected to sterilization without their informed consent. They submit that this is a violation of article 2 (b) and (e) of the Convention, read in conjunction with articles 5, 10 (h), 12 and 16 (1) (e) of the Convention.

Procedural History:
- The Optional Protocol entered into force for the Czech Republic on 26 February 2001.
- The authors submitted their communication on 17 February 2016.
- The views were adopted on 16 July 2019.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The authors argue that they were victims of an ongoing breach of the Convention due to the lack of informed consent for sterilization.
- They claim that the failure to ensure compensation for victims of forced sterilization continues to this day, constituting an ongoing breach of the Convention.
- The State party argues that the authors did not exhaust all available domestic remedies, specifically the civil action for protection of personal rights and the constitutional appeal.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee finds that the communication is inadmissible under article 4 (1) of the Optional Protocol.
- The Committee orders the State party to ensure that the present decision is communicated to the authors.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the exhaustion of domestic remedies in cases of forced sterilization.
- It highlights the ongoing challenges in seeking redress and compensation for victims of forced sterilization.

Citations and References:
