Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/81/D/137/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 23 February 2022
- Key Parties Involved: A (represented by counsel, Ivan Zhavoronkov and Mikhail Golichenko) vs. Russian Federation
- Case Name: A v. Russian Federation
- Case Summary: A, a citizen of the Russian Federation, alleges that her rights under articles 2 (f) and (g) and 12 of the Convention were violated by the Russian Federation. She claims that she was denied gender-sensitive drug dependence treatment, and was instead sentenced to three years imprisonment.

Procedural History:
The author, A, a Russian citizen, claims that Russia has violated her rights under Articles 2(f) and (g) and 12 of the Convention. The case was submitted to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women under Article 4(1) of the Optional Protocol. The author is a 28-year-old mother of two children, including an 8-year-old boy and an infant born in 2017.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The author alleges that the State party failed to provide her with gender-sensitive drug dependence treatment and discriminated against her based on her gender and health condition. She also claims that her right to equality before the law and equal protection of the law under article 12 of the Convention was violated.

Decisions and Rulings:
The State party denied the authors' claims and provided additional information on the facts of the case. The Committee found that the author has not exhausted available domestic remedies and declared the communication inadmissible under Article 4 (1) of the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for the exhaustion of domestic remedies in cases of alleged violations of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. It highlights the importance of raising issues in substance at the national level before seeking international intervention.
