Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/63/D/46/2012
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 22 February 2016
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Alleged victims: M.W. (not represented by counsel) and her child O.W.
  - State party: Denmark
- Case Name: M.W. v. Denmark

Procedural History:
The author, M.W., alleged that the Danish authorities violated her rights and those of her son O.W. under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The communication was declared admissible on 3 November 2014, and the State party's observations on the merits were received on 8 June 2015. The author's application for leave to appeal to the Supreme Court was rejected on 13 November 2014.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The Committee considered the communication under article 7 (3) of the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The issues addressed included custody and visitation rights, discrimination against women, and the alleged violations of the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the State party had not violated M.W.'s rights under the Convention. The State party's legislation was deemed gender-neutral, and the best interests of the child were considered paramount in all decisions related to parental responsibility.

Impact and Significance:
The Committee's decision sets a precedent for cases involving alleged violations of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. It emphasizes the importance of gender-neutral legislation and the consideration of the best interests of the child in custody and visitation matters.

Citations and References:
The decision is based on the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Optional Protocol to the Convention. The Committee's views and recommendations are also referenced in the document.
