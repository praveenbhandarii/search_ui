Case Name: Gonzlez Carreo v. Spain

Procedural History:
The case was submitted by Angela Gonzlez Carreo, represented by counsel Womens Link Worldwide, to the United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW) under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The initial submission was made on 19 September 2012, and the decision was adopted on 16 July 2014.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case addressed violations of Articles 2(af), 5(a), and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women by Spain. The substantive issues included violence against women and the failure to protect victims of domestic violence. The author alleged that Spain failed to protect her and her daughter from physical and psychological violence by her husband, F.R.C.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that Spain violated the author's and her daughter's rights under the Convention. It held that Spain's failure to protect them from domestic violence and its discriminatory treatment of women in the context of family relationships amounted to a violation of their rights to life, security of person, non-discrimination, and equal protection under the law.

Impact and Significance:
The case set a precedent for the obligation of states to exercise due diligence in addressing and preventing violence against women and girls. It emphasized the importance of providing effective remedies and redress for victims of gender-based violence and discrimination.
