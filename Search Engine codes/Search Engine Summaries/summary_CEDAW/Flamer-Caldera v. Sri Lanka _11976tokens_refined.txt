Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/81/D/134/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 24 March 2022
- Key Parties Involved: Rosanna Flamer-Caldera (represented by counsel, the Human Dignity Trust; Christine Chinkin, of the London School of Economics; Karon Monaghan QC, of Matrix Chambers; Keina Yoshida, of Doughty Street Chambers; and Olivia Clark, of DLA Piper)
- Case Name: Rosanna Flamer-Caldera v. Sri Lanka
- Case Summary: The author, Rosanna Flamer-Caldera, a lesbian and prominent activist for LGBTIQ+ rights in Sri Lanka, alleges that the State party has violated her rights under articles 2 (a) and (c)(g), 5 (a) and 16 of the Convention.

Procedural History:
- The author, a lesbian woman, is challenging the constitutionality of Section 365A of the Penal Code of 1883, which criminalizes same-sex sexual activity between women.
- The State party argues that the author has failed to exhaust domestic remedies and that the law is constitutional.
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) found that the Sri Lankan government has violated the woman's rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The author argues that the law violates her rights under the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR) and the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW).
- The State party argues that the author has failed to exhaust domestic remedies and that the law is constitutional.
- The Committee finds that the State party has subjected the author to direct and indirect discrimination emanating from the Penal Code of 1883 as amended.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee finds that the State party has breached the author's rights under Article 2(a) and (d)(g) of the Convention.
- The Committee notes that the author has been targeted by State and non-State actors due to her activism and being known as being lesbian, including through frequent threats, abuse, attacks, and harassment.

Impact and Significance:
- The Committee made several recommendations to the Sri Lankan government, including decriminalizing consensual same-sex sexual conduct between women and providing effective protection against gender-based violence against women, including legislation prohibiting discrimination against LBTI women.

Citations and References:
- The document is related to the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW).
- The Committee, together with the State party, is responsible for ensuring the implementation of the Optional Protocol and the elimination of discrimination against women.
