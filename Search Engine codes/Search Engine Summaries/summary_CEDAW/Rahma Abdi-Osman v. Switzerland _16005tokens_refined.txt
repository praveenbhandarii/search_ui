  - Rahma AbdiOsman (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Gabriella Tau)
  - Switzerland (the state party)

Case Name: Rahma AbdiOsman v. Switzerland

Procedural History:
The case involves Rahma AbdiOsman, a Somali national, who was denied asylum in Switzerland and faced deportation to Italy. The communication was submitted by Rahma AbdiOsman, represented by counsel Gabriella Tau, to the UNCEDAW. The case addresses issues related to deportation to Italy, discrimination against women, and the risk of trafficking, exploitation, and prostitution.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the violation of articles 2(d) and 6 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee analyzed the author's claims and the State party's arguments regarding the risk of gender-based violence and discrimination in Italy, the assessment of the author's asylum claim, and the protection of her rights under the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee adopted views under article 7(3) of the Optional Protocol, concluding that the author's rights under the Convention had been violated. It recommended that Switzerland refrain from returning the author to Italy and ensure her access to adequate medical care and psychological support.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the protection of the rights of asylum seekers, particularly women, and highlights the importance of considering the risk of gender-based violence and discrimination in asylum decisions. It emphasizes the obligation of State parties to provide adequate support and protection for vulnerable individuals.

Citations and References:
The case references the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, the Optional Protocol to the Convention, and relevant reports and recommendations from international organizations, including the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees and the Council of Europe.
