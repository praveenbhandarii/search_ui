Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/75/D/138/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 28 February 2020
- Key Parties Involved: S.F.M. (represented by counsel, Francisca Fernndez Guilln) vs. Spain
- Case Name: S.F.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, S.F.M., a Spanish national, alleges that Spain violated her rights under articles 2, 3, 5 and 12 of the Convention through the obstetric violence she experienced during childbirth.

Procedural History:
The author gave birth in a Spanish hospital in 2010 and was subjected to obstetric violence and mistreatment. She filed complaints with the hospital, the Galician Health Service, and the administrative courts, but did not receive a satisfactory response. The author exhausted all domestic remedies available to seek redress for the poor obstetric care she received.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include violations of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) articles 2, 3, 5, and 12. The court analyzed the author's claims of obstetric violence, lack of informed consent, and gender-based discrimination during childbirth.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that Spain violated the author's rights under CEDAW articles 2, 3, 5, and 12. The Committee recommended appropriate reparation for the author and urged Spain to ensure women's rights to safe motherhood and access to appropriate obstetric services.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for addressing obstetric violence and gender-based discrimination in healthcare settings. It highlights the importance of informed consent and the need for effective remedies for violations of women's reproductive health rights.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee under article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 138/2018. The case references the United Nations Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Optional Protocol thereto.
