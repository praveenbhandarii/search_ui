Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/70/D/92/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2015
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: N.M. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Howard Kennedy)
  - Party B: Turkey
- Case Name: N.M. v. Turkey
- Case Summary: N.M., a citizen of Singapore, alleges violations by Turkey of articles 2, 3, 15 and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The author claims that she was a victim of discrimination and violence by her husband, who abducted their two daughters and took them to Turkey. The author seeks return of the children to her care and compensation.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted by N.M. to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) against Turkey in 2015.
- The Committee considered the communication at its seventieth session in July 2018.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal issues addressed include violations of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, child abduction, domestic violence, and discrimination against women.
- Relevant legal provisions include Articles 2, 3, 15, and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee declared that the part of the communication related to the author's failure to exhaust domestic remedies is inadmissible under Article 4(1) of the Optional Protocol.
- The Committee concluded that the author has not provided sufficient information and explanations in support of her claims under the Convention, and therefore declared that that part of the communication is inadmissible under Article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol.
- The Committee decided that the communication is inadmissible under Articles 4(1) and 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the admissibility of communications related to violations of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- It highlights the importance of providing sufficient information and explanations to support claims under the Convention.
