Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/109/2016
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 27 February 2020
- Key Parties Involved: N.A.S. (author) vs. Denmark (State party)
- Case Name: N.A.S. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The author, N.A.S., submitted a communication to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women, alleging that Denmark had violated her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women by issuing a residence permit that did not cover the entire period of her stay in Denmark and by failing to provide her with adequate protection against deportation.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by N.A.S. on behalf of an alleged victim to the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women. The State party involved was Denmark, and the initial submission was made on November 14, 2016. The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication at its seventy-third session, which took place on July 15, 2019, as the author had been issued a residence permit and no longer risked deportation.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, specifically related to the residence permit and protection against deportation. The specific article of the Covenant and procedural issues were not specified in the provided information.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication at its seventy-third session, having been informed that the author had been issued a residence permit and no longer risked deportation.

Impact and Significance:
The decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication has implications for the rights of individuals under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, particularly in relation to residence permits and protection against deportation.
