Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/76/D/124/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 26 August 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: L.O. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Catherine Haenni)
  - Party B: Switzerland
- Case Name: L.O. v. Switzerland

Procedural History:
The author, L.O., a national of Mongolia, submitted a communication to the UNCEDAW claiming that her deportation to Mongolia along with her two daughters and son would violate their rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW). The communication was initially submitted on 31 December 2017, and the decision was adopted on 6 July 2020.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include exhaustion of domestic remedies, lack of substantiation, and the substantive issue of deportation to Mongolia. The relevant legal provisions are Articles 1, 2(c)(f), and 3 of CEDAW. The Court's analysis focused on the author's experiences of domestic violence, her asylum application process, and the State party's observations on the admissibility and merits of the communication.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the communication was inadmissible under article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol. The decision was communicated to the State party and the author.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for future cases involving claims of gender-based persecution and domestic violence in the context of asylum applications. It highlights the importance of thorough examination of asylum claims and the need for a gender-sensitive approach in dealing with such cases.

Citations and References:
The decision references previous cases such as R.P.B. v. Philippines (CEDAW/C/57/D/34/2011), N.M. v. Denmark (CEDAW/C/67/D/78/2014), H.D. v. Denmark (CEDAW/C/70/D/76/2014), and M.K.M. v. Denmark (CEDAW/C/71/D/81/2015) to support its legal reasoning and findings.
