Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/77/D/133/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: R.G. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Sardorbek Abdukhalilov)
  - Party B: Kyrgyzstan
- Case Name: R.G. v. Kyrgyzstan
- Case Summary: R.G., a national of Kyrgyzstan, alleges a violation of her rights under articles 2 (a), (b), (d), (e) and (f), 3 and 5 (a) of the Convention, due to her detention in various temporary isolation wards where she was subjected to inhuman conditions, lack of gender-sensitive penitentiary facilities, and inadequate medical care.

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: May 10, 2018 (initial submission)
- Date of adoption of views: November 3, 2020
- Jurisdiction and Competence: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Gender-based discrimination against a prisoner, lack of gender-sensitive penitentiary facilities
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 2 (a), (b), (d), (e) and (f), 3 and 5 (a) of the Convention
- Court's Analysis: The author, a female detainee, was held in temporary isolation wards in Kyrgyzstan and subjected to poor living conditions, lack of gender-sensitive facilities, and discriminatory treatment based on her gender.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found the State party responsible for violations of the Convention and recommended appropriate compensation and measures to rectify the situation.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for addressing gender-based discrimination and lack of gender-sensitive facilities in detention facilities, with implications for the protection of the rights of women detainees in all detention facilities.

Citations and References:
