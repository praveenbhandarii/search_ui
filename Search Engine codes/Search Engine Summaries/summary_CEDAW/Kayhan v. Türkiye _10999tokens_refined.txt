Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/34/D/8/2005
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2006
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Rahime Kayhan (represented by counsel, Ms. Fatma Benli)
  - Party B: Turkey
- Case Name: Rahime Kayhan v. Turkey

Procedural History:
The case involves Rahime Kayhan, a teacher of religion and ethics, who was dismissed from her position after wearing a headscarf in the classroom. She claimed that her dismissal was a violation of her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The case was brought before the United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW) during its thirty-fourth session in 2006.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the violation of the right to work, freedom of religion, conscience, thought, and freedom of choice. The relevant legal provisions include Article 11 of the Covenant. The Court analyzed the procedural and substantive issues related to employment, education, and religious freedom.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the communication was admissible ratione temporis and that the State party had not demonstrated that the application of domestic remedies was unreasonably prolonged or unlikely to bring effective relief. The Committee concluded that the communication was admissible and that the author's rights were violated when she was dismissed from her job for wearing a headscarf.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the protection of individuals' rights to religious expression and freedom from discrimination based on gender and religion in the workplace. It highlights the importance of upholding the rights enshrined in the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
