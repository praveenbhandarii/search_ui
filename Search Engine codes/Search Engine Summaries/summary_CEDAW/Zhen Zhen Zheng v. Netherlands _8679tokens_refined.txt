Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/42/D/15/2007
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2009
- Key Parties Involved: Ms. Zhen Zhen Zheng (represented by counsel, Mr. Michel Arnold Collet) vs. The Netherlands
- Case Name: Ms. Zhen Zhen Zheng v. The Netherlands
- Case Summary: Ms. Zheng, a Chinese asylum seeker, claims to be a victim of a violation by the Netherlands of article 6 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, which protects women from trafficking and forced prostitution. She was trafficked into the Netherlands and forced into prostitution, and subsequently applied for asylum. Her application was denied, and she was ordered to return to China.

Procedural History:
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women considered the communication at its 42nd session on 20 October 2008.
- The Committee adopted a decision on admissibility on 27 October 2009.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Violation of article 6 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 6 of the Convention
- Court's Analysis: Ms. Zheng claimed to be a victim of human trafficking and criticized the Dutch authorities for failing to protect her. The State party challenged the admissibility of the communication, arguing that the author had failed to exhaust domestic remedies and that the communication was not sufficiently substantiated.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the author had not exhausted all available domestic remedies, but her allegations relating to article 6 of the Convention were sufficiently substantiated for purposes of admissibility.
- The Committee declared the communication admissible and found that the State party had violated the author's rights under article 6 of the Convention.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the protection of victims of human trafficking and the obligations of state parties under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
