Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/57/D/39/2012
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: Decision adopted by the Committee at its fifty-seventh session, 1028 February 2014
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: N. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Ilse van Kuilenburg)
  - Party B: The Netherlands
- Case Name: N. v. the Netherlands

Procedural History:
The case involves N., a Mongolian citizen, seeking asylum in the Netherlands, but her application was rejected. The author claimed that the denial of her asylum application constituted a violation by the Netherlands of articles 1, 2(e), 3, and 6 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal details include the articles of the Covenant, procedural and substantive issues, subject matter, and references. The issues addressed include whether the Netherlands violated N.'s rights under CEDAW, failed to protect her from gender-based violence and discrimination, and whether the rejection of her asylum application constitutes a violation of the Netherlands' obligations under CEDAW.

Decisions and Rulings:
The State party challenged the admissibility of the case, arguing that the communication is inadmissible ratione materiae. The author claimed that the State party violated her rights under the Convention by denying her a temporary asylum residence permit. The Committee found the communication admissible and determined that it is competent to examine the communication.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the protection of women seeking asylum from gender-based violence and discrimination. It highlights the importance of considering the non-refoulement principle and the obligation of States parties to protect women from third countries seeking asylum.

Citations and References:
The case references the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, general recommendations, and other international legal frameworks.

Overall, the case of N. v. the Netherlands addresses the admissibility of a communication alleging violations of CEDAW by the Netherlands in the context of asylum and gender-based violence. The Committee found the communication admissible and emphasized the importance of protecting women from gender-based violence and discrimination in the asylum process.
