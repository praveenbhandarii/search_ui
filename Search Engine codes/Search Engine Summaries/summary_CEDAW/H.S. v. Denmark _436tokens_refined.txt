Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/70/D/113/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2018
- Key Parties Involved: H.S. (author and alleged victim) vs. Denmark (state party)
- Case Name: H.S. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The author, H.S., submitted a communication to the UNCEDAW claiming that she faced a risk of deportation from Denmark, where she had been living for several years. The Committee considered the communication and, after being informed that the author had been granted a residence permit in Denmark, decided to discontinue its consideration of the case.

Procedural History:
- Initial submission date: 28 February 2017
- Decision adopted by the Committee at its seventieth session (July 2018)
- Participating members of the Committee: Aye Feride Acar, Gladys Acosta Vargas, Nicole Ameline, Magalys Arocha Domnguez, Gunnar Bergby, Marion Bethel, Louiza Chalal, Esther Eghobamien-Msheila, Nala Gabr, Hilary Gbedemah, Nahla Haidar, Ruth Halperin-Kaddari, Yoko Hayashi, Lilian Hofmeister, Dalia Leinarte, Rosario G. Manalo, Lia Nadaraia, Aruna Devi Narain, Bandana Rana, Patricia Schulz, Wenyan Song, and Aicha Vall-Verges

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Substantive issue: Risk of deportation
- Subject matter: Deportation, Residence permit

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication after the author was granted a residence permit and no longer faced deportation.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for cases involving the risk of deportation and the granting of residence permits in the context of discrimination against women.
