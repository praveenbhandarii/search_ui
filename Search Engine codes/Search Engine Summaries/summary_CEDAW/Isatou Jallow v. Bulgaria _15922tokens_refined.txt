Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/52/D/32/2011
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2012
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Isatou Jallow (represented by counsel, Albena Koycheva)
  - Party B: Bulgaria
- Case Name: Isatou Jallow v. Bulgaria
- Case Summary: Isatou Jallow, a Gambian citizen, submitted a communication to the UNCEDAW claiming that she and her minor daughter, M.A.P., were victims of violations by Bulgaria of their rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The communication alleged that Ms. Jallow was subjected to physical and psychological violence, sexual abuse, and forced marriage by her husband, a Bulgarian national, and that their daughter was also subjected to abuse. The UNCEDAW adopted views under article 7, paragraph 3, of the Optional Protocol, finding that Bulgaria had violated Ms. Jallow's rights under articles 1, 2, 3, 5 and 16, paragraphs 1 (c), (d), (f) and (g), of the Convention.

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: 15 November 2010 (initial submission)
- Reference: Transmitted to the State party on 5 May 2011 (not issued in document form)

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Violations of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 1, 2, 3, 5 and 16, paragraphs 1 (c), (d), (f) and (g) of the Convention
- Court's Analysis: The author and her daughter have been subjected to domestic violence and have not received adequate protection from the State party. The State party has failed to provide the author with adequate legal remedies and has treated her unfairly in court proceedings.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee concludes that the facts before it reveal a violation of the State party's obligations under article 2, paragraphs (d) and (e), of the Convention.
- The Committee recommends that the State party take all appropriate measures to ensure that the author and her daughter are provided with effective protection from domestic violence and that the author's rights under article 2, paragraphs (d) and (e), of the Convention are restored.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for the protection of women and children from domestic violence and discrimination under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. It highlights the importance of providing effective legal remedies and protection for victims of domestic violence.
