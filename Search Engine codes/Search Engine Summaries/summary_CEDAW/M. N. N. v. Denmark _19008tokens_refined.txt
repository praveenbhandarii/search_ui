Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/55/D/33/2011
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2013
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: M. N. N. (the author and victim)
  - Party B: Denmark (the state party)
- Case Name: M. N. N. v. Denmark

Procedural History:
- The author, M. N. N., sought asylum in Denmark, claiming that her deportation to Uganda would violate the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) considered the complaint and requested Denmark to refrain from expelling M. N. N. while her communication was under consideration.
- The Committee examined the communication and found M. N. N.'s claims to be admissible under the Optional Protocol to the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal issues addressed include the admissibility of the communication, violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, and the risk of female genital mutilation (FGM) faced by the author.
- Relevant legal provisions include articles 1, 2(c), 2(d), and 3 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Committee analyzed the factual background of the case, including M. N. N.'s personal history, the risk of FGM, and the Danish authorities' handling of her asylum application.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that M. N. N.'s expulsion from Denmark would constitute a violation of articles 1, 2(c), 2(d), and 3 of the Convention.
- The Committee made a decision under the Optional Protocol to the Convention, requesting Denmark to refrain from expelling M. N. N. to Uganda while her communication was under consideration.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the protection of individuals at risk of gender-based violence, specifically female genital mutilation, under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- It highlights the importance of considering the specific circumstances and risks faced by individuals seeking asylum due to gender-based discrimination.
