Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/84/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 27 February 2020
- Key Parties Involved: O.D.A. (alleged victim) vs. Denmark (state party)
- Case Name: O.D.A. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The Committee, having lost contact with the author of communication No. 84/2015, decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The communication was initially submitted on November 14, 2016, and the decision to discontinue consideration was made on July 15, 2019. The Committee lost contact with the author, O.D.A., and therefore decided to discontinue the case.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal details of the case were not specified, and the substantive issue was also not specified. The only procedural issue mentioned was the loss of contact with the author of the communication. The subject matter of the case was discrimination against women.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of Communication No. 84/2015 due to the loss of contact with the author, O.D.A., who submitted the complaint against Denmark. The decision was made at the Committee's 73rd session on July 15, 2019.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the case due to the loss of contact with the author sets a precedent for the handling of similar cases in the future. It highlights the importance of maintaining communication with the parties involved in legal proceedings.

Citations and References:
The decision adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 84/2015 on 27 February 2020 serves as the primary reference for this case.
