Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/61/D/52/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2015
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: D. G. (the author and victim, represented by P. Fischer and J. Kruseman of Fischer Advocaten)
  - Party B: The Netherlands
- Case Name: D. G. v. The Netherlands
- Case Summary: D. G., a Bulgarian national of Roma origin, alleges that the Netherlands has violated her rights under articles 2, 5, 11, 12, and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. She claims to have been subjected to exploitation and human trafficking and to have been denied social benefits due to her nationality and Roma origin.

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: 14 August 2012 (initial submission)
- Date of adoption of decision: 23 July 2015
- Jurisdiction and Competence: The Committee considered D.G.'s communication admissible and found that the Netherlands had violated D.G.'s rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, admissibility, denial of social benefits, exploitation, and human trafficking.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 2, 5, 11, 12, and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women
- Court's Analysis: The Committee found that the Netherlands had violated D.G.'s rights under the relevant articles of the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the Netherlands had violated D.G.'s rights under articles 2, 5, 11, 12, and 16 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Committee recommended that the Netherlands provide D.G. with adequate compensation and take measures to prevent similar violations in the future.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the protection of vulnerable individuals, particularly victims of exploitation and human trafficking, under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The ruling highlights the importance of providing access to social benefits and shelter for individuals in vulnerable situations, regardless of their residence status.
