Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/94/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 10 September 2019
- Key Parties Involved: A.N.A. (represented by counsel, Rabih AzadAhmed) vs. Denmark
- Case Name: A.N.A. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: A.N.A., a Somali national, sought asylum in Denmark, but her application was rejected, and she risks deportation to Somalia. She claims that her deportation would violate articles 3, 5, and 16 (b) of the Convention.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted by A.N.A. (represented by counsel, Rabih AzadAhmed) on 14 September 2015.
- The decision was adopted by the Committee on 15 July 2019.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The author claimed that her deportation would violate articles 3, 5, and 16(b) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW).
- The State party argued that the Refugee Appeals Board's decision was based on a thorough assessment of the author's statements and evidence.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the planned deportation violates articles 3, 5, and 16 (b) of the Convention and that all domestic remedies have been exhausted.
- The Committee decided that the communication is admissible under the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the protection of women's rights in asylum cases and highlights the importance of considering the specific circumstances and risks faced by female asylum seekers.
