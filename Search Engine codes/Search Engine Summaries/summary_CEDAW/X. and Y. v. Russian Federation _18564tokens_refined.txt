Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/73/D/100/2016
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - X. and Y. (represented by counsel, Valentina Frolova and Mary Davtyan) vs. Russian Federation
- Case Name: X. and Y. v. Russian Federation
- Case Summary: X. and Y., Russian citizens, claim to be victims of violations by the Russian Federation of their rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. They allege systematic physical and psychological abuse, including beatings, death threats, severe injuries, frequent arguments, insults, and humiliation, by X.'s husband, K., from 2010 to 2013. They also allege that X. was subjected to physical and psychological abuse by K. in the presence of their elder daughter, and that K. continued to harass X. even after she left him.

Procedural History:
- The authors, X and Y, have been victims of domestic violence and have been denied effective protection and investigation by the authorities.
- They have made numerous complaints to various authorities, but their complaints have been dismissed or ignored.
- The authors have invoked their rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW) and other international human rights instruments, but their claims have been rejected.
- They have exhausted all available domestic remedies and have no other effective remedies available to them.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The authors claim that the State party's private prosecution system for domestic violence cases is inadequate and violates their rights under the Convention.
- They also allege that the authorities have failed to provide adequate training to law enforcement officials, have not collected statistics on the prevalence of domestic violence, and have not provided rehabilitation programs for perpetrators.
- The State party has challenged the admissibility of the communication on the grounds of non-exhaustion of domestic remedies.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee finds that the State party's failure to provide adequate legal protection against domestic violence and to effectively investigate and prosecute such crimes violates the authors' rights under the Convention.
- The Committee recommends that the State party adopt comprehensive legislation to prevent and address violence against women, including domestic violence, and ensure that victims have access to immediate means of redress and protection.

Impact and Significance:
- The Committee's decision sets a precedent for holding states accountable for failing to protect victims of domestic violence and for not providing effective legal remedies.
- The decision highlights the importance of comprehensive legislation to prevent and address gender-based violence, including domestic violence, and the need for immediate means of redress and protection for victims.
