Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/76/D/63/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 28 February 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: C.E.R. (alleged victim)
  - Party B: Argentina (State party)
- Case Name: C.E.R. v. Argentina
- Case Summary: The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women considered a communication submitted by C.E.R. alleging sexual violence by Argentina. The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication after the State party and the complainants accepted an amicable settlement reached by the parties.

Procedural History:
The communication was initially submitted on 5 August 2013. The Committee reached an amicable settlement between the parties and accordingly, decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication at its seventy-fifth session held on 28 February 2020.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The communication concerned sexual violence, and the Committee considered the request for discontinuance made by the State party and accepted by the complainants.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication after an amicable settlement was reached by the parties.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for the resolution of cases involving sexual violence through amicable settlements, potentially impacting future cases brought before the Committee.
