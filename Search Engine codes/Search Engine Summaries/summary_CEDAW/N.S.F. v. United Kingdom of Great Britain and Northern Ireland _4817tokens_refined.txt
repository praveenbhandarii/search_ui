Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/38/D/10/2005
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2007
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Ms. N. S. F. (the author)
  - The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
- Case Name: Ms. N. S. F. v. The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland

Procedural History:
Ms. N. S. F., a Pakistani asylum seeker, submitted Communication No. 10/2005 to the CEDAW Committee under the Optional Protocol. Her asylum claim in the UK was denied, and she exhausted all domestic remedies, including a judicial review application to the High Court and an application to the European Court of Human Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case included the admissibility of the communication under the Optional Protocol, exhaustion of domestic remedies, and the author's claims of gender-based violence and discrimination. The Committee analyzed the State party's challenge to the admissibility, the author's response, and the relevant legal provisions under the Optional Protocol.

Decisions and Rulings:
The CEDAW Committee found the communication inadmissible under Article 4, paragraph 1, of the Optional Protocol due to the author's failure to exhaust all available domestic remedies. The Committee also noted that the State party's view of an allegation of sex discrimination would be relevant for consideration by the Home Office when reconsidering the author's case.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for the requirement to exhaust all available domestic remedies before submitting a communication to the CEDAW Committee under the Optional Protocol. It also highlights the importance of considering allegations of gender-based discrimination in asylum and human rights cases.
