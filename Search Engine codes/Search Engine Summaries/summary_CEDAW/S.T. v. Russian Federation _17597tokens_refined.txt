Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/72/D/65/2014
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 2019
- Key Parties Involved: S.T. (the author and victim) vs. Russian Federation (the state party)
- Case Name: S.T. v. Russian Federation
- Case Summary: S.T., a national of the Russian Federation, alleges that the Russian Federation has violated her rights guaranteed under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) by failing to prevent and effectively investigate severe physical and psychological violence committed against her by her former husband.

Procedural History:
- The author, S.T., represented by counsel, Aleksei Ponomarev and Vanessa Kogan, submitted the communication on 8 November 2013.
- The case involves the failure of the authorities to prevent and effectively investigate severe physical and psychological violence committed against S.T. by her former husband.
- The jurisdiction and competence lie with the United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal issues addressed include the violation of S.T.'s rights guaranteed under article 2 (c) and (d), read in conjunction with article 1, and article 5 (a) of the Convention.
- Relevant legal provisions include the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW) and the Committee's general recommendations No. 19 and No. 28.
- The Court's analysis focuses on the failure of the authorities to prevent and effectively investigate severe physical and psychological violence committed against S.T. by her former husband.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the Russian Federation violated S.T.'s rights under the Convention by failing to provide effective legal protection against domestic violence.
- The State party was recommended to take immediate measures to modify social and cultural patterns of conduct to eliminate prejudices and customary practices that perpetuate gender-based violence.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for the State party's obligation to protect women from all forms of gender-based violence, including domestic violence.
- The Committee's decision has broader legal implications for the implementation of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and the promotion of gender equality.

Citations and References:
- The legal citations and references include the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW), the Committee's general recommendations, and related documents submitted by the parties.
