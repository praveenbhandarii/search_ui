Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/68/D/93/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2018
- Key Parties Involved:
  - K.I.A. (alleged victim)
  - State party (Denmark)
- Case Name: K.I.A. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the unknown whereabouts of the author and loss of contact with her counsel.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 93/2015 was submitted by K.I.A. against Denmark. The decision to discontinue consideration was adopted at the sixtyeighth session of the CEDAW Committee, with the participation of 14 members.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The procedural issue of discontinuation of consideration due to the unknown whereabouts of the author and loss of contact with counsel was the key issue addressed in this case. The relevant legal provisions were not specified in the document.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the unknown whereabouts of the author and the loss of contact between the author's counsel and the Committee.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of consideration in this case has implications for future communications submitted under the Optional Protocol of the CEDAW. It sets a precedent for cases where the whereabouts of the author are unknown and contact with counsel is lost.
