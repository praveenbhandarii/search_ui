Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/63/D/60/2013
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2016
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Svetlana Medvedeva (represented by counsel, Dmitri Bartenev)
  - Party B: Russian Federation
- Case Name: Svetlana Medvedeva v. Russian Federation
- Case Summary: Svetlana Medvedeva, a Russian national, claimed that her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women were violated by the Russian Federation when she was denied employment as a helmsperson-motorist due to her gender.

Procedural History:
- The author, Svetlana Medvedeva, submitted the communication on 5 May 2013.
- The Russian Federation ratified the Convention and the Optional Protocol thereto on 23 January 1981 and 28 July 2004, respectively.
- The District Court dismissed the author's case, holding that the contested decision did not violate the author's right to employment.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Discrimination based on gender in employment, violation of rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Articles 1, 2 (c), (d), (e) and (f) and 11 (1) (b), (c) and (f) of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- Court's Analysis: The Committee found violations of the author's rights and made recommendations to the Russian Federation to address the violations and ensure compliance with the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women found the communication admissible and examined the merits of the communication, finding violations of the author's rights.
- The Committee made recommendations to the Russian Federation to address the violations and ensure compliance with the Convention.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for addressing gender discrimination in employment and upholding the rights of women under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Committee's recommendations have broader legal implications for promoting gender equality in the workplace and eliminating discriminatory practices.
