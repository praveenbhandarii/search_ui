Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/39/D/5/2005
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: Year 2007
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: The Vienna Intervention Centre against Domestic Violence and the Association for Women's Access to Justice
  - Party B: The State party (Austria)
- Case Name: The Vienna Intervention Centre against Domestic Violence and the Association for Women's Access to Justice v. Austria
- Case Summary: The authors of the communication claim that Ahide Goekce, a victim of domestic violence, was not adequately protected by the State party, leading to her tragic death. The authors allege violations of articles 1, 2, 3, and 5 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted to the UNCEDAW in 2005 by the Vienna Intervention Centre against Domestic Violence and the Association for Women's Access to Justice.
- The State party (Austria) provided a response to the communication, which was considered by the Committee.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The legal issues addressed in the case include the State party's failure to protect Ahide Goekce from domestic violence and the alleged violations of her rights under the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- Relevant legal provisions include articles 1, 2, 3, and 5 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
- The Committee's analysis focused on the State party's obligations to protect women from domestic violence and provide effective remedies for victims.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that the State party violated its obligations under the Convention by failing to exercise due diligence to protect Ahide Goekce from violence and death.
- The Committee made recommendations to the State party to strengthen its implementation and monitoring of laws related to domestic violence, vigilantly prosecute perpetrators, ensure coordination among law enforcement and judicial officers, and strengthen training programs and education on domestic violence.

Impact and Significance:
- The Committee's views and recommendations have significant implications for the protection of women's rights and the prevention of domestic violence. The case sets a precedent for holding State parties accountable for failures to protect women from violence.

Citations and References:
- The Committee's views and recommendations are based on the legal provisions of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women and relevant international human rights standards. The case is referenced in the context of addressing domestic violence and protecting women's rights.
