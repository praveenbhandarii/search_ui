Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/49/D/17/2008
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 2011
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Maria de Lourdes da Silva Pimentel (the author and victim), represented by the Center for Reproductive Rights and Advocacia Cidad pelos Direitos Humanos
  - State party: Brazil
- Case Name: Maria de Lourdes da Silva Pimentel v. Brazil
- Case Summary: The author alleges that her daughter, Alyne da Silva Pimentel Teixeira, died due to a violation by the State party of her right to life and health under articles 2 and 12 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee found that the State party had violated the victim's rights and recommended compensation and preventive measures.

Procedural History:
The case was submitted by Maria de Lourdes da Silva Pimentel, represented by the Center for Reproductive Rights and Advocacia Cidad pelos Direitos Humanos. The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women adopted the present views under article 7, paragraph 3, of the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the right to life and health under articles 2 and 12 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women. The Committee's analysis focused on the State party's failure to provide timely and adequate medical care during pregnancy and childbirth, as well as the systemic problems in Brazil's health system.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the State party violated the victim's rights and recommended compensation and preventive measures to address the systemic problems in Brazil's health system.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for holding states accountable for violations of women's rights to life and health, particularly in the context of maternal mortality and access to healthcare. It highlights the importance of addressing systemic issues in healthcare systems to prevent similar violations in the future.

Citations and References:
The Committee referenced articles 2 and 12 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women, as well as general recommendations No. 24 and No. 14 of the Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women and the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The case also cited the State party's obligations under the Brazilian Federal Constitution regarding the right to health.
