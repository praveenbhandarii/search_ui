Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/69/D/80/2015
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Dates: 26 April 2018
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A. (A.S., represented by counsel, NielsErik Hansen)
  - Party B (Denmark)
- Case Name: A.S. v. Denmark
- Case Summary: A.S., a national of Uganda, sought asylum in Denmark but her application was rejected. She claims that her deportation to Uganda would violate her rights under articles 13 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.

Procedural History:
The case involves A.S., a Ugandan national, seeking asylum in Denmark based on her sexual orientation and fear of persecution in Uganda. The Danish Immigration Service and Refugee Appeals Board rejected her application, leading to a complaint to the UN Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include the proper assessment of the risk of serious gender-based violence in Uganda, the credibility of the author's claims, and the application of national law in asylum decisions. The Committee considered the author's submissions, the State party's response, and relevant general recommendations and jurisprudence.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the communication is inadmissible under article 4(2)(c) of the Optional Protocol. It determined that there was no irregularity in the decision-making process regarding the author's asylum application and that the State party's authorities did not fail to properly assess the risk of serious gender-based violence in the event of her return to Uganda.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for the assessment of asylum claims based on gender-based persecution and the application of international conventions in national asylum decisions. It emphasizes the importance of substantiating claims and addressing procedural irregularities in asylum cases.

Citations and References:
The decision shall be communicated to the State party and to the author. The case is referenced as Communication No. 80/2015 and provides insights into the interpretation and application of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women.
