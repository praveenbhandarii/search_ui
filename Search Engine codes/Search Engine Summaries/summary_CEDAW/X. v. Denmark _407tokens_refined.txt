Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CEDAW/C/71/D/73/2014
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women (UNCEDAW)
- Document Date: 29 October 2018
- Key Parties Involved: X (alleged victim) vs. Denmark (state party)
- Case Name: X v. Denmark
- Case Summary: The Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication since the author no longer resided in Denmark and was not at risk of deportation.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 73/2014 was submitted by X (alleged victim) against Denmark (state party) on October 7, 2014. The decision was adopted by the Committee at its seventy-first session, which took place from October 22 to November 9, 2018.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was discrimination against women. However, the specific article of the Covenant and procedural issues were not specified in the document.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the author no longer residing in Denmark and not being at risk of deportation.

Impact and Significance:
The decision sets a precedent for cases where the alleged victim no longer resides in the state party and is not at risk of deportation. It also highlights the importance of the author's current residency and risk of deportation in the Committee's consideration of communications.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 73/2014. The participating members of the Committee during the examination of the communication were listed in the document.
