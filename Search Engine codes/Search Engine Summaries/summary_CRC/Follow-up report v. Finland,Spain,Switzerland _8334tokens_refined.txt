Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/90/2
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 26 August 2022
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: M.B.S., M.B., B.G., S.M.A., C.O.C., and R.Y.S. (all unaccompanied child migrants)
  - Party B: Spain
- Case Name: M.B.S. v. Spain, M.B. v. Spain, B.G. v. Spain, S.M.A. v. Spain, C.O.C. v. Spain, and R.Y.S. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The report evaluates Spain's compliance with the Convention on the Rights of the Child and the Optional Protocol, specifically regarding the age determination procedure for unaccompanied children, detention in migrant detention centers, and the provision of effective reparations for violations suffered.

Procedural History:
The case involves individual communications submitted under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The communications address the violation of rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child by Spain in relation to unaccompanied child migrants.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The report identifies issues with Spain's age determination procedures, inadequate protection for unaccompanied minors, and insufficient training for immigration officers, police officers, and other relevant professionals. It emphasizes the need for Spain to ensure that all procedures for determining the age of young persons claiming to be children are in line with the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee recommends that Spain provide effective reparations for the violations suffered by the unaccompanied child migrants and prevent similar violations in the future. Specific recommendations include ensuring competent guardianship for unaccompanied young persons and developing an effective redress mechanism for age determination procedures.

Impact and Significance:
The case has significant implications for Spain's compliance with international human rights law and its obligations under the Convention on the Rights of the Child. It highlights the importance of protecting the rights of unaccompanied children and ensuring appropriate care and protection.
