Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/83/D/30/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - N.R. (the author and victim)
  - State party: Paraguay
- Case Name: N.R. v. Paraguay
- Case Summary: N.R., a national of Argentina, submitted a communication on behalf of his daughter, C.R., alleging violations of the Convention on the Rights of the Child by the State party, Paraguay. The communication claimed that the State party failed to ensure the daughter's right to maintain personal relations and direct contact with her father.

Procedural History:
The case involved a legal dispute between N.R. and the State party, Paraguay, regarding the visitation rights of N.R.'s daughter, C.R. The case had been ongoing since 2015, with various judgments and orders issued by the court.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The main legal issues addressed in the case included the enforcement of visitation rights, non-compliance with court orders, and the best interests of the child. The author alleged violations of articles 3, 4, 5, 9(3), 10(2), 18, and 19 of the Convention on the Rights of the Child by the State party, Paraguay. The court's analysis focused on the failure of the State party to ensure the daughter's right to maintain personal relations and direct contact with her father.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the State party had violated the author's and daughter's rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The Committee recommended that the State party provide effective relief for the violations suffered, including through counseling and other appropriate support services intended to rebuild the relationship between the girl and her father.

Impact and Significance:
This case sets a precedent for the enforcement of visitation rights and the protection of children's rights in custody disputes. It emphasizes the importance of ensuring the best interests of the child are protected and the enforcement of court orders in a timely and effective manner.
