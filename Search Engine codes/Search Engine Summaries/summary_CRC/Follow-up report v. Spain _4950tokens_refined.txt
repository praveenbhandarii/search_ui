Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/85/2
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 29 October 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: D.D. (author of the communication)
  - Party B: Spain (State party)
- Case Name: D.D. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, an unaccompanied minor, was deported from Spain to Morocco without being subjected to any form of identity check or assessment of his situation, which exposed him to the risk of violence and cruel, inhuman, and degrading treatment in Morocco. The Committee on the Rights of the Child found that Spain had violated the author's rights under Articles 3, 20, and 37 of the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Procedural History:
The case involves the complaint filed by D.D. against Spain before the UNCRC regarding the violation of his rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The Committee issued Views on the matter, finding Spain in violation of Articles 3, 20, and 37 of the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The main legal issues addressed in the case revolve around the violation of the author's rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child, specifically related to the deportation of an unaccompanied minor from Spain to Morocco without proper assessment and identity checks. The Committee's analysis was based on the principles of the Convention, emphasizing the importance of protecting the rights of children, particularly those who are vulnerable and unaccompanied.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee ordered Spain to provide the author with adequate reparation, including financial compensation and rehabilitation for the harm suffered. Spain was also requested to take all necessary steps to prevent similar violations in the future, particularly by revising relevant laws and practices.

Impact and Significance:
The D.D. v. Spain case has significant implications for the protection of unaccompanied children from summary deportations and the need for proper age-determination processes. It also highlights the importance of implementing the Views and recommendations of the CRC Committee to ensure the rights of children are protected.
