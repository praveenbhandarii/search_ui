Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/87/D/66/2018, CRC/C/87/D/67/2018, CRC/C/87/D/68/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: N.A.B., N.M.C. and K.A. (represented by counsel, Vanessa Hernandez Delgado and Mara Victoria Gutierrez Yumar) vs. Spain
- Case Name: N.A.B. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: Three unaccompanied minors from the Gambia claimed that Spain violated their rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child by improperly determining their age, conducting medical tests without consent, and denying access to legal representation.

Procedural History:
The authors, represented by counsel, submitted communications to the Committee on the Rights of the Child under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The initial submissions were made on 3 December 2018, 11 December 2018, and 17 December 2018.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The main issues in this case include the right to an identity, the right to be heard, the right to a guardian, the right to development, and the right to special protection and assistance from the State. The authors claimed violations of their rights under articles 3, 8, 18 (2), 20, 27, and 29 of the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee considered the violations of the authors' rights and the implications for the treatment of unaccompanied minors in Spain and beyond. The case highlighted the importance of upholding the rights of children and ensuring fair age determination processes.

Impact and Significance:
This case has implications for the treatment of unaccompanied minors and underscores the need for States to ensure fair, transparent, and rights-respecting age determination processes for children.

Context Summary:
This case concerns the deportation of two authors from a State party to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The authors claim that they were not assisted by a specialist in the age determination process and that they did not have a lawyer present during the procedure. The use of panoramic dental X-rays to determine age is a controversial practice that has been criticized by human rights organizations.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case raises issues related to the rights of children under the Convention on the Rights of the Child, specifically the right to a fair trial and the right to protection from mistreatment while in custody. The State party's failure to provide a specialist in the age determination process and the lack of legal representation for the authors during the procedure may constitute a violation of their rights under the Convention.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communications after the authors' lawyers notified them that they had lost contact with the authors and did not know their whereabouts.

Impact and Significance:
This case highlights the importance of ensuring that children's rights are protected when they are in custody and that they receive a fair trial. It also raises questions about the use of controversial methods, such as panoramic dental X-rays, to determine age in immigration proceedings.
