Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/69/D/1/2014
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 2015
- Key Parties Involved: A.H.A. (the author and victim) vs. Spain (the state party)
- Case Name: A.H.A. v. Spain
- Case Summary: A.H.A., a national of Ghana, claimed that Spain violated his rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child by denying him protection as a minor. The dispute arose from the determination of A.H.A.'s age and his entitlement to child protection.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted to the UNCRC under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The Committee considered the admissibility of the communication and found that all relevant events occurred before the entry into force of the Optional Protocol for Spain, precluding the Committee from examining the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed included the determination of A.H.A.'s age and his entitlement to protection as a minor under the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The Committee's analysis focused on the admissibility of the communication and the temporal jurisdiction of the Optional Protocol.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found the communication inadmissible due to the fact that all relevant events occurred before the entry into force of the Optional Protocol for Spain. As a result, the Committee was precluded from examining the communication.

Impact and Significance:
This case highlights the importance of adhering to the temporal jurisdiction of international human rights instruments and ensuring that all necessary documents and evidence are considered in determining a person's age, particularly when seeking protection as a minor.
