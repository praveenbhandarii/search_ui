Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/77/D/18/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 2018
- Key Parties Involved: R.L. (alleged victim) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: R.L. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The Committee on the Rights of the Child considered a communication submitted by R.L., an unaccompanied child migrant, who alleged that Spain had subjected him to a medical test for determination of age and detained him in a migrant center for adults.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by R.L. on May 19, 2017, under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of the Child on a communications procedure. The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 18/2017 during its seventy-seventh session (January 15-February 2, 2018).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The CRC Committee did not provide specific reasoning for its decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication. However, it is possible that the Committee found the communication to be inadmissible or without merit. The decision was made in accordance with Rule 26 of its Rules of Procedure under the Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on the Rights of the Child decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 18/2017, without providing detailed legal reasoning for the decision.

Impact and Significance:
This decision emphasizes the importance of protecting the rights of unaccompanied child migrants and ensuring that their best interests are taken into account in all decisions affecting them. It also highlights the need for States to ensure that age determination processes are carried out in a manner that is fair, humane, and takes into account the child's rights and well-being.
