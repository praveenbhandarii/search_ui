Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/86/D/51/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 12 March 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - A.B. (the author and victim)
  - State party: Finland

Case Name: A.B. v. Finland

Procedural History:
The case involves a complaint submitted to the UNCRC by A.B., a minor born in the Russian Federation, who alleges that Finland violated his rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The author's family sought asylum in Finland due to persecution and discrimination they faced in Russia based on their sexual orientation. The Finnish Immigration Service rejected their applications, leading to a series of legal proceedings.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The author invoked various provisions of the Convention, including articles 2, 3, 13, 14, 16, 17, 19, 22, and 29, arguing that Finland failed to protect his rights under these provisions, particularly in relation to his family's sexual orientation. The main issues in the case were discrimination based on sexual orientation, the best interests of the child, and protection from persecution.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that Finland failed to conduct a proper assessment of the best interests of the child and violated its obligations under the Convention. The authorities did not adequately consider the real risk of a serious violation of the author's rights, including violence and harassment, upon his return to the Russian Federation.

Impact and Significance:
This case highlights the importance of protecting the rights of children from discrimination based on their family's sexual orientation. It also underscores the need for States parties to ensure that the best interests of the child are respected and protected, particularly in cases involving asylum seekers and refugees. The outcome of this case may have implications for similar cases involving LGBTQ+ individuals and families seeking protection from persecution.
