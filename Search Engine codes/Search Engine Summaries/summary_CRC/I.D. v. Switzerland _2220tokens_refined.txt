Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CRC/C/93/D/128/2020
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)
- Document Date: 8 June 2023
- Key Parties Involved:
  - I.D. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Boris Wijkstrm and Gabriella Tau)
  - State party: Switzerland
- Case Name: I.D. v. Switzerland
- Case Summary: I.D., a national of Guinea, alleges that Switzerland wrongly determined that he was an adult and ordered his removal to Spain under the Dublin III Regulation, thereby violating his rights under the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Procedural History:
The author, I.D., submitted a complaint to the Committee on the Rights of the Child, alleging that Switzerland violated his rights under the Convention. The Committee requested interim measures to prevent the author's removal to Spain, which the State party opposed. The Committee ultimately decided to discontinue consideration of the communication due to the author's provisional admission to Switzerland.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The Committee analyzed the complaint under Article 3, 6(1), 8, 12, 20(1), 22, 24, and 28 of the Convention on the Rights of the Child, focusing on the rights of children to protection from economic exploitation, access to education, and protection from torture and inhuman treatment. The case also addressed the issue of exhaustion of domestic remedies before submitting a complaint to the Committee.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the author had been recognized as a child at the time of his entry into Switzerland and had been provisionally admitted to the State party. It also determined that the author had exhausted domestic remedies before submitting his complaint to the Committee.

Impact and Significance:
This case highlights the importance of protecting children from deportation and ensuring that they are recognized as children under the Convention. It also emphasizes the significance of exhausting domestic remedies before submitting a complaint to the Committee.
