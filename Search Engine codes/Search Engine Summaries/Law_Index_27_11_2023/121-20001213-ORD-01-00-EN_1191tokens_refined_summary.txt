Title: Case Concerning the Arrest Warrant of 11 April 2000 (Democratic Republic of the Congo v. Belgium)

Case Overview:
The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) filed a case against Belgium regarding an international arrest warrant issued on 11 April 2000. The warrant targeted the acting Congolese Minister for Foreign Affairs, Mr. Abdoulaye Yerodia Ndombasi, for alleged crimes constituting "grave violations of international humanitarian law." The DRC sought his detention and subsequent extradition to Belgium. The arrest warrant was transmitted to all States, including the DRC.

Important Details:
- The DRC initiated proceedings against Belgium over the dispute concerning the arrest warrant.
- The warrant was issued by a Belgian investigating judge against the DRC's Minister for Foreign Affairs.
- The Court adjudicated upon the DRC's request for a provisional measure.
- The Parties agreed on the timelimits for filing written pleadings.
- The written pleadings will address jurisdiction, admissibility, and the merits of the case.
- The document was signed by Gilbert GUILLAUME, President, and Philippe COUVREUR, Registrar.
- The document is written in French, with the French text being authoritative.
- The document was issued on 13 December 2000 at the Peace Palace in The Hague.
