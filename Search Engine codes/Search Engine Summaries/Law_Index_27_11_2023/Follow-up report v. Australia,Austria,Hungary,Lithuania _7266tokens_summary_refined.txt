Combined Summary:

Case Details:

- Case Name: Nyusti and Takcs v. Hungary
- Case ID: CRPD/C/9/D/1/2010
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Views adopted on 16 April 2013
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Nyusti and Takcs (authors) vs. Hungary (State party)

Procedural History:

- The authors alleged that the State party had violated their rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities by failing to provide them with equal access to financial services, including automatic teller machines.
- The State party had paid compensation to the authors, but the Committee found that the measures taken were not sufficient to address the issues raised.
- The Committee had adopted follow-up views in this case, and the State party had provided information on the implementation of the Committee's views.
- The authors had provided additional information, and the Committee had assessed the measures taken by the State party as largely satisfactory.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:

- The Committee had found that the State party had violated the authors' rights under the Convention, and had ordered the State party to take specific measures to address the issues raised.
- The State party had paid compensation to the authors, but the Committee had found that the measures taken were not sufficient to address the issues raised.
- The case references the Constitution of Lithuania, the European Convention on Human Rights, and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The author cites the Committee's Views on the case, which emphasize the importance of protecting the privacy of individuals with disabilities and ensuring their access to justice.

Decisions and Rulings:

- The State party had installed tactile guide strips in 119 branches of a bank, and 33% of the branches were accessible to persons with visual impairments.
- 98% of the branches were accessible to wheelchair users.
- The State party had formulated a strategy to help facilitate equal access of persons with disabilities to financial services.
- Credit institutions were under an obligation to adopt the strategy and policy by 15 September 2016.
- The authors had received compensation and reimbursement of costs amounting to 300,000 forint (approximately 1,072 United States dollars).
- The contracts for the authors who were under guardianship had been signed by their respectively appointed guardians.

Impact and Significance:

- The case highlights the importance of ensuring equal access to financial services for persons with disabilities, including those with visual impairments.
- The decision emphasizes the responsibility of States to take appropriate measures to ensure that persons with disabilities have equal access to financial services, including automatic teller machines.
- The case has implications for the protection of privacy and access to justice for individuals with disabilities in Lithuania and beyond.
- It underscores the need for States to ensure that their laws and policies comply with international human rights standards, including the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
