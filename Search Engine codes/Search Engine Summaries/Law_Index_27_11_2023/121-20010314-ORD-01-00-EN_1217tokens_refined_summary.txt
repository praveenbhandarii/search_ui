Title: Arrest Warrant of 11 April 2000 (Democratic Republic of the Congo v. Belgium)

Case Overview:
The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) filed a case against Belgium regarding an international arrest warrant issued against the acting Congolese Minister for Foreign Affairs, Mr. Abdoulaye Yerodia Ndombasi. The DRC alleged that the warrant, issued on 11 April 2000, sought his detention and extradition to Belgium for crimes constituting grave violations of international humanitarian law. The warrant was transmitted to all states, including the DRC, which received it on 12 July 2000.

Important Details:
- Case Number: 121
- Court: International Court of Justice
- Date: March 14, 2001
- Judges: President Guillaume; Vice President Shi; Judges Oda, Bedjaoui, Ranjeva, Herzog, Fleischhauer, Korom, Vereshchetin, Higgins, ParraAranguren, Koomans, Rezek, and AlKhasawneh
- Registrar: Cououvreur
- Document Details: International Court of Justice Reports of Judgments, Advisory Opinions and Orders, Case Concerning the Arrest Warrant of 11 April 2000 (Democratic Republic of the Congo v. Belgium), Order of 14 March 2001
- Case Related Details:
  - The ICJ issued an arrest warrant on April 11, 2000, regarding the dispute between the DRC and Belgium.
  - The DRC requested an extension for filing its Memorial, which was granted.
  - The timeline for filing the DRC's Memorial was extended to April 17, 2001.
  - The timeline for filing Belgium's CounterMemorial was extended to July 31, 2001.
  - The subsequent procedure was reserved for further decision.
- Decision Details:
  - The decision was made on March 14, 2001, at the Peace Palace in The Hague.
  - The French text of the decision is authoritative.
  - The decision was signed by Gilbert GUILLAUME, the President of the ICJ, and Philippe COUVREUR, the Registrar.

Additional Information:
- The case involves a dispute between the DRC and Belgium.
- The ICJ is the primary judicial organ of the United Nations.
- The decision was made in three copies, with one placed in the Court's archives and the others transmitted to the Governments of the DRC and Belgium, respectively.
