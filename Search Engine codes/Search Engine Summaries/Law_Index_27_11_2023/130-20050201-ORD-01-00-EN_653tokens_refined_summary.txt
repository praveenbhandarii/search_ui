Title: Case Concerning Sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks and South Ledge (Malaysia/Singapore)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Malaysia and Singapore over the sovereignty of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge. The parties jointly approached the International Court of Justice (ICJ) on 24 July 2003 through a Special Agreement signed on 6 February 2003. They requested the court to determine which country has sovereignty over the disputed territories. Both parties agreed to accept the court's judgment as final and binding.

Important Details:
- The Special Agreement between Malaysia and Singapore was signed on 6 February 2003 and came into force on 9 May 2003.
- The ICJ order, dated 1 September 2003, established the deadlines for filing Memorials and Counter-Memorials by each party.
- Malaysia and Singapore submitted their Memorials and Counter-Memorials within the specified time limits.
- The parties agreed that the written pleadings should include a Reply from each party, to be submitted no later than 10 months after receiving the certified copy of the Counter-Memorial from the other party.
- The ICJ order, dated 1 February 2005, sets 25 November 2005 as the deadline for filing the Reply by each party.
- The subsequent procedure is reserved for further decision.

Document Details:
This order, issued by the ICJ on 1 February 2005, sets the deadline for filing the Reply by each party as 25 November 2005. The judges presiding over the case include President SHI Jiuyong, Vice President RANJEVA, and Judges GUILLAUME, KOROMA, VERESHCHETIN, PARRAARANGUREN, KOOIJMANS, REZEK, ALKHASAWNEH, BUERGENTHAL, ELARABY, OWADA, and TOMKA. The registrar of the court is Philippe COUVREUR.
