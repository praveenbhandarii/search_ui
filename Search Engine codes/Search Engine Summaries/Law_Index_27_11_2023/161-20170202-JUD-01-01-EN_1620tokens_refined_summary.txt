Title: Maritime Delimitation in the Indian Ocean (Somalia v. Kenya)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Somalia and Kenya regarding the delimitation of maritime spaces in the Indian Ocean. Somalia filed an Application in 2014, seeking the International Court of Justice (ICJ) to determine the maritime boundary between the two countries based on international law.

Important Details:
- The case number is 161, and the document is a judgment type document.
- The judgment was issued on March 24, 2017, by Vice President Yusuf of the ICJ.
- The dispute revolves around the interpretation of a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Kenya and Somalia.
- Both countries attribute different legal implications to the provisions of the MOU.
- The MOU was proposed by a third party, but the dispute arose despite this.
- The case highlights the issue of State succession and the cleanslate doctrine, which African States have objected to since their independence in the 1960s.
- The Nyerere doctrine of State succession is relevant to the case.
- The MOU was signed but had minimal contributions from the respective governments.
- The importance of active participation by States in the creation of international legal instruments and rules is emphasized.
- Governments should carefully negotiate and contribute to the contents of bilateral legal instruments.
- The case may have implications for other disputes related to State succession and the cleanslate doctrine.
