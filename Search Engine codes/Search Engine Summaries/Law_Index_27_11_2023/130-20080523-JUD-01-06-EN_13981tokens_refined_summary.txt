Title: Sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks and South Ledge (Malaysia/Singapore)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Malaysia and Singapore over the sovereignty of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge, located in the Strait of Malacca. The International Court of Justice (ICJ) ruled that sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh belongs to Singapore, while sovereignty over Middle Rocks belongs to Malaysia. The ownership of South Ledge remains pending and can be settled as part of the delimitation of territorial sea limits among concerned states.

Important Details:
- The case addresses the issue of sovereignty over Middle Rocks and South Ledge, located in the Strait of Malacca.
- The principles of effective possession and consolidation of title through the manifestation of sovereignty were applied.
- No evidence was presented for Middle Rocks and South Ledge, while evidence supported Singapore's possession of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh.
- South Ledge is a low-tide elevation falling within the former customary 3-mile limit of the territorial waters of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh.
- Singapore's exercise of sovereign authority over the waters surrounding Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh led to its sovereignty over Middle Rocks and South Ledge.
- The issue of sovereignty over South Ledge does not need to be decided outright and can be settled as part of the delimitation of territorial sea limits among concerned states.

Document Details:
This judgment, delivered on 23 May 2003, by Pemmaraju SREENIVASA RAO, was published by the ICJ. It cites various cases and legal principles, including the Island of Palmas case and the Eritrea/Yemen case. The judgment was presided over by 17 judges, including the President of the ICJ and a Judge ad hoc.

Additional Information:
The document provides historical and contextual information regarding the ties of loyalty between the Sultanate of Johor and the Orang Laut, who inhabited Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh. It emphasizes the burden and standard of proof in international litigation. The case may have implications for the delimitation of territorial sea limits among concerned states in the region.
