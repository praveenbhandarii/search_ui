Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: M.R. i V. v. Spain
- Case ID: CRPD/C/26/D/48/2018
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Views adopted by the Committee under article 5 of the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 48/2018
- Document Dates: 24 March 2022 (date of adoption of Views), 17 November 2015 (initial submission)
- Key Parties Involved: M.R. i V. (author), Spain (State party)
- Case Summary: The case concerns a complaint filed by M.R. i V., a person with a mental disability, against Spain for violating their rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The author claimed that Spain denied them access to modified duty employment in the Mossos d'Esquadra and failed to provide adequate legal protection and remedies. The author's appeals to the Barcelona Administrative Court No. 13 and the High Court of Justice of Catalonia were dismissed.

Procedural History:
- The author, M.R. i V., a person with a mental disability, claimed that Spain violated his rights under the Convention by denying him access to modified duty employment in the Mossos d'Esquadra (Catalan autonomous police force) and by not providing adequate legal protection and remedies.
- The author appealed the decision to Barcelona Administrative Court No. 13 and the High Court of Justice of Catalonia, but both courts dismissed his appeals.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee found that the State party violated the author's rights under the Convention by denying him access to modified duty employment and by not providing adequate legal protection and remedies.
- The Committee also found that the State party's failure to provide effective protection of the author's rights was a continuing violation of the Convention.
- The Committee emphasized that the State party had a positive obligation to provide reasonable accommodation and failed to assess the author's abilities or consider reasonable accommodation measures.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The State party's denial of the author's access to modified duty employment and failure to provide adequate legal protection and remedies were found to be in violation of the Convention.
- The Committee held that the State party's arguments regarding the admissibility of the communication were unfounded.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of ensuring equal access to employment opportunities for persons with disabilities, particularly in the public sector.
- The decision emphasizes the need for States to provide adequate legal protection and remedies for individuals with disabilities who face discrimination in the workplace.
- The decision also underscores the obligation of States to engage in genuine dialogue and assess reasonable accommodation measures for individuals with disabilities.
