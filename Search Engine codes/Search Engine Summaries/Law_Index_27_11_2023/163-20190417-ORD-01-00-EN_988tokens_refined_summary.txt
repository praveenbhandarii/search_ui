Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic on 13 June 2016. The dispute revolves around the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea, Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue, and the legal status of the building housing the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This order, issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) on 17 April 2019, extends the timelimits for the Republic of Equatorial Guinea's Reply to 8 May 2019 and the French Republic's Rejoinder to 21 August 2019. The subsequent procedure is reserved for further decision. The document is cited as C.I.J. Recueil 2019, p. 355, with the French text being authoritative. The relevant legal provisions are Article 48 of the Statute of the Court and Article 44, paragraphs 3 and 4 of the Rules of Court. The case is listed as General List No. 163. The document is signed by Abdulqawi Ahmed Yusuf (President) and Philippe Couvreur (Registrar).

Important Details:
- The ICJ extends the timelimit for Equatorial Guinea's Reply to 8 May 2019.
- The ICJ extends the timelimit for France's Rejoinder to 21 August 2019.
