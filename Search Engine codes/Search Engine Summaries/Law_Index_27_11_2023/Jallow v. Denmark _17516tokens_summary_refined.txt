Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: A v. Denmark
- Case ID: Not available
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (UN CERD)
- Document Details: Communication submitted to the UN CERD regarding alleged violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination by the State party.
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: A (author), Denmark (State party)

Procedural History:
- The author filed a communication with the UN CERD alleging violations of the International Convention on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination by Denmark.
- The communication relates to the State party's alleged failure to effectively investigate and prosecute incidents of racial discrimination, specifically exhibitions that incited racial discrimination and hatred.
- The State party responded to the communication, arguing that the investigation was conducted on a fully informed basis and that the decision to discontinue the investigation was not arbitrary.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The author alleges violations of articles 4(a), 3.1(c), and 6 of the International Convention on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination.
- The author argues that the State party's decision to stop the investigation constitutes a violation of article 4(a) of the Convention, as it reveals a failure to effectively investigate hate crimes falling under article 266 of the Criminal Code.
- The State party argues that it is not the Committee's task to review the interpretation of national law made by its authorities and that the decision to discontinue the investigation was not arbitrary.
- The State party asserts that the exhibitions in question were non-public and did not have the intention to promote or incite racial discrimination.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee finds that the State party violated its obligations under article 4(a) of the Convention, read in conjunction with article 6, by not conducting an effective response to the incident of racist hate speech.
- The Committee emphasizes the importance of effective investigations into alleged instances of racial discrimination and racist hate speech.
- The Committee requests the State party to take further measures to ensure the effective application of existing legislation and the development of guidelines and training programs on the prevention of racial hatred and discrimination.
- The State party is also requested to give wide publicity to the Committee's opinion.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of effective investigations into reports of racial discrimination and hate speech.
- The decision reinforces the obligation of states to ensure that artists and cultural institutions do not engage in hate speech or discrimination under the guise of artistic freedom.
- The case sets a precedent for future cases involving allegations of racial discrimination and the effectiveness of investigations.
