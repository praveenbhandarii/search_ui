Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic regarding the immunity of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea and the legal status of the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This order document, dated June 6, 2018, pertains to International Court of Justice Case Number 163. It provides key information about the case, including the judges involved, the registrar, and the key facts and holdings of the ICJ. The document also mentions the next steps in the proceedings and cites relevant articles and protocols.

Important Details:
- The ICJ ruled on a dispute between Equatorial Guinea and France regarding the immunity of the Second Vice President of Equatorial Guinea and the legal status of the embassy building in France.
- The ICJ declared its jurisdiction to entertain the case and deemed the part of the application concerning the status of the embassy building admissible.
- The French Republic is required to file a CounterMemorial by December 6, 2018.
- Citations include Article 48 of the Statute of the Court, Article 79, paragraph 9, of the Rules of Court, and the Optional Protocol to the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations concerning the Compulsory Settlement of Disputes.

Additional Information:
- The document is signed by President Abdulqawi Ahmed Yusuf and Registrar Philippe Couvreur.
- The language of the document is French, with an authoritative English translation.
- The document was signed at The Peace Palace in The Hague.
- The application was filed on June 13, 2016, and the deadline for the CounterMemorial is December 6, 2018.
