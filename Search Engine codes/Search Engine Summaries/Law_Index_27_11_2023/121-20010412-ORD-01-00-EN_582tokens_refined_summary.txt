Title: Arrest Warrant of 11 April 2000 (Democratic Republic of the Congo v. Belgium)

Case Overview:
The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) filed a case against Belgium regarding an international arrest warrant issued on 11 April 2000. The warrant targeted the acting Congolese Minister for Foreign Affairs, Mr. Abdoulaye Yerodia Ndombasi, for alleged crimes constituting "grave violations of international humanitarian law". The DRC received the warrant on 12 July 2000.

- Case Number: 121
- Date of Hearing: April 12, 2001
- Date of Document Issuance: April 12, 2001
- Place of Document Issuance: Peace Palace, The Hague
- Language of Document: French, with English translation being authoritative
- President of the ICJ: Gilbert Guillaume
- Registrar of the ICJ: Philippe Couvreur
- Timelimits extension:
  - Memorial of the Democratic Republic of the Congo: Extended until May 17, 2001
  - CounterMemorial of the Kingdom of Belgium: Extended until September 17, 2001
- Reason for Timelimit Extension: Request made by the Agent of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, with reasons provided
- Agent of Belgium's Response: Did not oppose the extension and requested a longer timelimit for the filing of the CounterMemorial
- Number of Copies Issued: Three
- Copies Issued to:
  - One copy placed in the archives of the Court
  - Two copies transmitted to the Governments of the Democratic Republic of the Congo and the Kingdom of Belgium, respectively
