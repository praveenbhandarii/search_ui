Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: L. R. et al. v. Slovak Republic
- Case ID: CERD/C/66/D/31/2003
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD)
- Document Details: Opinion on the admissibility of the petition
- Document Dates: March 2005
- Key Parties Involved: L. R. and 26 other Slovak citizens of Roma ethnicity (petitioners), Slovak Republic (respondent)

Procedural History:
- The petitioners filed a complaint with the Constitutional Court and the District Prosecutor, claiming violations of their rights under the CERD and the Slovak Constitution.
- The State party argued that the petitioners did not exhaust domestic remedies and that they could have availed themselves of an effective remedy through a petition pursuant to the Constitution and the Right to Petition Act.
- The Committee rejected the State party's request for reconsideration of admissibility and its submissions on the merits.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The case raises issues related to the protection of the rights of minority groups, particularly the Roma community, from discrimination.
- The petitioners argue that the Slovak Republic has failed to ensure that all public authorities and institutions act in conformity with the obligation to engage in no act or practice of racial discrimination.
- The Committee found that the petitioners have exhausted available and effective remedies before the Constitutional Court and that they are victims of an absence of a remedy.
- The Committee also found that the State party had violated several provisions of the CERD and requested the State party to take measures to address the issues raised.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found the petition admissible and proceeded to examine the merits of the case.
- The Committee found that the State party had violated several provisions of the CERD, including articles 2, 4, 5, and 6.
- The Committee requested the State party to take measures to address the issues raised and to provide the petitioners with an effective remedy.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision highlights the importance of ensuring that all public authorities and institutions act in conformity with the obligation to engage in no act or practice of racial discrimination.
- The decision emphasizes the need for states to take measures to prohibit and bring an end to racial discrimination by any persons, group, or organization.
- The case sets a precedent for future cases related to racial discrimination and the exhaustion of domestic remedies.
