Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: Communication No. 74/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Decision adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol
- Document Dates: 12 December 2019 (initial submission), 6 September 2021 (decision)
- Key Parties Involved: A.S. (author), Sweden (state party)
- Case Name: A.S. v. Sweden

Procedural History:
- The author, A.S., a national of Afghanistan, claims that his deportation to Afghanistan would violate articles 10, 15, 16, and 22 of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The State party, Sweden, argues that the communication should be held inadmissible and that the author's claims do not reveal a violation of the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities considered the communication and decided to discontinue its consideration due to the expulsion decision becoming statute-barred and the author no longer facing a risk of being returned to Afghanistan.
- The Committee requests that the State party not remove the author to Afghanistan while his case is pending before the Committee.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the expulsion decision becoming statute-barred and the author no longer facing a risk of being returned to Afghanistan.

Impact and Significance:
- This case highlights the importance of protecting the rights of individuals with disabilities, particularly in the context of deportation and expulsion decisions.
- The decision of the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities sets a precedent for future cases involving the intersection of disability rights and immigration law.
