Title: Sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks and South Ledge (Malaysia/Singapore)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Malaysia and Singapore over the sovereignty of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge. The International Court of Justice (ICJ) ruled that Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh is a rock and not capable of being possessed by either state. The court also found that the British and Singapore did not acquire sovereignty over the island through historical agreements or administration of the surrounding waters. The case is ongoing, with the court examining the conduct of the parties and their predecessors in title to determine the issue of sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh.

Important Details:
- The case concerns the sovereignty of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge.
- Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh is a rock formation located at the eastern entrance of the Straits of Singapore.
- The British discovered the island in the 16th century and established a lighthouse on it in the 19th century.
- The court examined the circumstances surrounding the construction and commissioning of the Horsburgh lighthouse on Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh.
- The court found that the Johor authorities were not involved in the construction and commissioning of the lighthouse.
- The court analyzed the conduct of the parties after the construction of the lighthouse to determine if there were any acts that passed sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh from Johor to the United Kingdom.
- The court considered the legislation relating to the lighthouses in the Straits area and the activities of the parties and their predecessors in relation to the island.
- The court concluded that the Crown has maintained the lighthouse on Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh since its construction in 1850, which confers some rights and obligations on the Colony.

Document Details:
The document is a judgment issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) on May 23, 2008, in Case Number 130. The case was submitted by Malaysia and Singapore under a Special Agreement signed on February 6, 2003. The judgment is reported in the ICJ Reports 2008, page 12, with the official citation as Sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks and South Ledge (Malaysia/Singapore), Judgment, ICJ Reports 2008, p. 12. The judges involved in the case include President Owada, Vice-President Abraham, and Judges ad hoc Tomka, Bennouna, and Xue Hanqin.

Additional Information:
- The case was heard by the ICJ over eight days in November 2006 and January 2007.
- The dispute between Malaysia and Singapore has been ongoing since 1979 and was not resolved through bilateral negotiations.
- The Parties agreed to submit the dispute to the ICJ and accept its judgment as final and binding.
- The judgment marked the first time the ICJ ruled on a dispute between two Asian nations.
- The legal status of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh has been a subject of contention between Singapore and Malaysia.
- The ICJ's judgment determined the ownership of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge, as well as the boundary between the territorial seas of Malaysia and Singapore.
- The ICJ considered the legal effect of a letter dated 25 June 1825 from Sultan Abdul Rahman of the Johor-Riau-Lingga Sultanate to his brother Sultan Hussein.
- The ICJ ultimately concluded that the letter did not serve as Johor's title to its territory, and that the dispute over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh should be resolved based on relevant facts and international law.
