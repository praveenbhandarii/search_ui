Title: Case Concerning Sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge (Malaysia/Singapore)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Malaysia and Singapore over the sovereignty of Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge. The parties jointly approached the International Court of Justice (ICJ) through a Special Agreement, requesting the court to determine the ownership of these territories. Both countries have agreed to accept the court's judgment as final and binding.

Important Details:
- Case Number: 130
- Date: 1 September 2003
- Parties Involved: Malaysia and Singapore
- Key Provisions:
  - The ICJ is requested to determine sovereignty over Pedra Branca/Pulau Batu Puteh, Middle Rocks, and South Ledge.
  - Timelines for initial pleadings are set: a Memorial is to be filed by 25 March 2004, and a CounterMemorial by 25 January 2005.
  - The subsequent procedure is reserved for further decision.
