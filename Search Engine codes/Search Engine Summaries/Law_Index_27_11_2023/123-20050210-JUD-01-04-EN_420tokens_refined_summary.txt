Title: Certain Property (Liechtenstein v. Germany)

Case Overview:
Liechtenstein filed an Application against Germany in the International Court of Justice, initiating proceedings related to a dispute concerning certain property. The case details, preliminary objections, reasoning, evidence, and the standard for determining the existence of a dispute were discussed.

Important Details:
- Parties involved: Liechtenstein and Germany
- Preliminary objections raised by Germany: Six objections were raised, but only the second objection was upheld by the Court. The third, fourth, fifth, and sixth objections were not considered.
- Reasoning for upholding Germany's second objection was not specified.
- Agreement that the finding of a legal dispute does not imply the superiority of one country's position over the other's.
- Evidence presented by Germany, including its position in bilateral consultations and a letter from the Minister for Foreign Affairs on January 20, 2000.
- Insufficient evidence to support the proposition that Liechtenstein's claims were actively opposed by Germany.
- Importance of setting a standard for determining the existence of a dispute that is not too low, to encourage peaceful settlements.

Document Details:
This document is a judgment type document of the International Court of Justice, with the case number 123. It was filed on 1 June 2001. The specific judges and dates are not mentioned in the document.
