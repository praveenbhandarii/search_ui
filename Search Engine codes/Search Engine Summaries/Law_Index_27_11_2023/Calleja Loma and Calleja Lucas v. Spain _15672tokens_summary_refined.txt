Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: CRPD/C/23/D/41/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Views adopted by the Committee under article 5 of the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 41/2017
- Document Dates: 2 May 2017 (initial submission), 28 August 2020 (adoption of Views)
- Key Parties Involved: Rubn Calleja Loma and Alejandro Calleja Lucas (authors/victims), Spain (State party)
- Case Name: Rubn Calleja Loma and Alejandro Calleja Lucas v. Spain

Procedural History:
- Rubn Calleja Loma and Alejandro Calleja Lucas submitted a communication to the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The communication alleged violations of Rubn's rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities by the State party, Spain.
- The Committee considered the communication and adopted its Views on 28 August 2020.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee found that the State party violated Rubn's rights under articles 7, 13, 15, 17, 23, and 24 of the Convention, read in conjunction with article 4.
- The State party's decision to enroll Rubn in a special education center was found to be motivated by discriminatory attitudes towards persons with disabilities and a lack of commitment to inclusive education.
- The State party failed to provide adequate support to ensure Rubn's wellbeing in school and denied him inclusive education.
- The reports drawn up by the school were not based on a comprehensive assessment of Rubn's needs, and the management teams failed to define a process for Rubn's education and ensure cooperation with his family.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee ruled that the State party violated Rubn's rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The State party was found to have subjected Rubn to discrimination and cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment on the basis of his disability.
- The State party failed to provide adequate support for Rubn's wellbeing in school and denied him inclusive education.

Impact and Significance:
- This case highlights the importance of ensuring that children with disabilities have access to inclusive education and are protected from discrimination and abuse.
- It emphasizes the need for schools and education authorities to provide adequate support and accommodations to ensure the wellbeing and successful education of children with disabilities.
- The case sets a precedent for holding states accountable for violations of the rights of persons with disabilities under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
