Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: Arturo Medina Vela v. Mexico
- Case ID: CRPD/C/22/D/32/2015
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Decision adopted by the Committee under article 5 of the Optional Protocol, concerning communication No. 32/2015
- Document Dates: 19 August 2015 (initial submission), 6 September 2019 (adoption of Views)
- Key Parties Involved: Arturo Medina Vela (author), Mexico (State party)

Procedural History:
- The author was deprived of his liberty without due process and was not given the opportunity to dispute the charges against him.
- The Committee considered that the author's rights under the Convention were violated.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee found that the author was deprived of his liberty without due process and that his rights under the Convention were violated.
- The author was not given the opportunity to dispute the charges against him, and his right to legal capacity was not respected.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee adopted the decision that the State party violated the author's rights under the Convention.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision highlights the importance of ensuring equal protection and non-discrimination for persons with disabilities.
- It emphasizes the need for States to respect the legal capacity of persons with disabilities and provide them with equal access to justice.

Citations and References:
- United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)
- Optional Protocol to the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Relevant articles of the Convention (5, 9, 12, 13, 14, and 19)
