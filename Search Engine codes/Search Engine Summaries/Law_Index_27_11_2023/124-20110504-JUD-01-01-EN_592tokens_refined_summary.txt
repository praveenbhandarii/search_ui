Title: Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Nicaragua filed a case against the Republic of Colombia in 2001, seeking resolution for a dispute related to territory and maritime delimitation. Colombia raised preliminary objections to jurisdiction, leading to a suspension of the proceedings on the merits.

Important Details:
- Nicaragua initiated proceedings against Colombia regarding a group of legal issues concerning territory and maritime delimitation.
- Colombia filed preliminary objections to jurisdiction, resulting in the suspension of the proceedings on the merits.
- Judge Awn Shawkat AlKhasawneh concurs with the finding that Honduras' application to intervene in the proceedings cannot be granted.
- The judge questions the distinction between a right and an interest of a legal nature, expressing doubts about its need, wisdom, and practical utility.
- The judge argues that an interest of a legal nature is essentially a right and that the distinction lacks logical support and clarity.
- The judge highlights that the distinction has not been followed in the court's practice and has only made the concept more obscure.

Document Details:
This judgment document, dated June 14, 2013, is part of the International Court of Justice case number 124. Judge Awn Shawkat AlKhasawneh presided over the case. The document number is 30/5 CIJ1020.indb.
