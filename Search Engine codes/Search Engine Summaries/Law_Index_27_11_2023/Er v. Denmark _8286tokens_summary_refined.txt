Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: Murat Er vs. Denmark
- Case ID: Unknown
- Court/Tribunal: Human Rights Committee under the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
- Document Details: Decision on the merits of the case
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Murat Er (the complainant), Denmark (the respondent state)

Procedural History:
- The complainant, Murat Er, alleged that he was denied a training position at a company due to his ethnic background.
- The State party, Denmark, argued that the complaint is inadmissible because the complainant is not a victim for the purposes of article 14 of the Convention.
- The State party also claimed that the complainant has not shown an imminent violation of his rights under the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The State party argued that the Act on Ethnic Equal Treatment offers individuals a level of protection against racial discrimination that goes further than the protection required by the Convention.
- The State party invoked previous cases to support its argument that the complainant was not directly subjected to the alleged discriminatory practice.
- The State party maintained that there was no discrimination in the complainant's case and that it had no existing or imminent effects on the complainant's enjoyment of his rights under the Convention.
- The State party contended that the protection offered to the complainant and the remedies available fully satisfy the Convention's requirements.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Human Rights Committee found that the complainant's rights under the Convention were violated.
- The Committee concluded that the complainant was not offered the same possibilities of education and training as his fellow students, which constituted an act of racial discrimination.
- The Committee recommended that Denmark grant the complainant adequate compensation for the moral injury caused by the violations of the Convention.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of addressing issues of racial discrimination and ensuring equal treatment for all individuals.
- It emphasizes the need for effective protection against discrimination and remedies for reported acts of racial discrimination.

Citations and References:
- International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
- Act on Ethnic Equal Treatment
- Previous cases invoked by the State party

Additional Context:

- The Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination (CERD) found that the State party had violated articles 2, paragraph 1 (d), and 6 of the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination.
- The CERD found that the petitioner's claims were not abstract and did not refer to his own case, but rather to the lack of effective protection against ethnic discrimination in Danish national legislation and the failure of Danish courts to interpret national legislation in accordance with the Convention.
- The CERD recommended that the State party grant the petitioner adequate compensation for the moral injury caused by the violations of the Convention.
- The CERD also requested that the State party give wide publicity to the Committee's opinion, including among prosecutors and judicial bodies.
