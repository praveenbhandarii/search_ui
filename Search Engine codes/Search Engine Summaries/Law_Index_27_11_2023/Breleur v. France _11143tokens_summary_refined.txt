Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: Mr. B vs. France
- Case ID: Communication No. 61/2012
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (UN CERD)
- Document Details: Opinion adopted by the Committee under article 14 of the Convention
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Mr. B (petitioner), France (State party)

Procedural History:
- The petitioner, Mr. B, alleged racial discrimination in the workplace, specifically the "ESCADRE" system, which introduced an ethnic codification system based on skin color and ethnic origin for internal use.
- The petitioner alleged violations of Articles 2, 3, 4, and 5 of the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination.
- The State party, France, refuted these allegations and argued that it has a comprehensive legal framework to combat discrimination in the workplace.
- The petitioner secured partial implementation of the Versailles Court of Appeal judgment.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The case highlights the importance of protecting employees against discrimination based on actual or presumed membership or non-membership of an ethnic group, nation, or race.
- The State party's argument that it has a comprehensive legal framework to combat discrimination in the workplace is notable.
- The case emphasizes the importance of providing easy access to justice for employees who are victims of discrimination, including adjusted burden of proof and the possibility of receiving compensation for pecuniary and non-pecuniary damages.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee held that the State party had violated articles 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 of the Convention by discriminating against the petitioner on the basis of his national origin and by failing to provide him with effective protection and remedies against the act of discrimination.
- The Committee found that the petitioner was entitled to adequate reparation and satisfaction for the harm suffered as a result of the State party's violations of the Convention.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of protecting individuals from discrimination based on their national origin and ensuring that effective protection and remedies are available to victims of discrimination.
- It underscores the responsibility of States to ensure that companies operating within their jurisdiction comply with international human rights standards.
