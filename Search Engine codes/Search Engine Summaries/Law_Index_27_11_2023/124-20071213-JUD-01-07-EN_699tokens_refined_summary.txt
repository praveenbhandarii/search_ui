Title: Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Overview:
The case, numbered 124, involves a territorial and maritime dispute between Nicaragua and Colombia. Nicaragua filed an Application on 6 December 2001, initiating proceedings against Colombia. The dispute revolves around a group of related legal issues concerning territory and maritime delimitation. Colombia filed preliminary objections to jurisdiction on 21 July 2003, leading to the suspension of the proceedings on the merits.

Important Details:
- Declaration by Judge Keith regarding the Territorial and Maritime Dispute case.
- Agreement with the decision of the Court.
- Emphasis on the Court's power and responsibility to decide matters in dispute at a preliminary stage.
- Settlement of the question of sovereignty over three named islands in favor of Colombia in 1948.
- Equal rights for both parties to present evidence and submissions.
- Limit on the power and responsibility to decide, with emphasis on the Court remaining faithful to its judicial character.
