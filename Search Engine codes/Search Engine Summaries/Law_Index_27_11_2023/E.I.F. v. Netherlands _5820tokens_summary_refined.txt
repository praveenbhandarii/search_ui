Case Details:
- Case Name: Communication No. 1111/2002
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Human Rights Committee
- Document Details: Views adopted by the Committee on 27 March 2007
- Case Summary: The author, a police officer of Surinamese origin, alleges discrimination by the teaching staff at the Netherlands Police Academy (NPA) and claims that the Minister wrongly overlooked the Boekraad Committee's recommendation in his case. The author argues that the State party violated articles 2, 5, 6, and 7 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights. The State party disputes the author's claims and argues that there was no violation of the Covenant.

Procedural History:
- Dates: Communication No. 1111/2002, views adopted by the Committee on 27 March 2007
- Key Parties Involved: The author (a police officer of Surinamese origin) and the State party (the Netherlands)
- Jurisdiction and Competence: The United Nations Human Rights Committee has jurisdiction over cases involving alleged violations of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Allegations of discrimination, violation of articles 2, 5, 6, and 7 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
- Court's Analysis: The Committee notes that the author has not substantiated his allegations of discrimination and that the State party has provided reasonable explanations for the decisions taken by the NPA. The Committee finds that the author has not established a violation of article 2, 5, 6, or 7 of the Covenant and declares the communication inadmissible.

Decisions and Rulings:
- Court Decisions: The Committee declares the communication inadmissible.
- Legal Reasoning: The author failed to substantiate his allegations of discrimination, and the State party provided reasonable explanations for the decisions made by the NPA.
- Key Findings: The Committee finds no violation of articles 2, 5, 6, or 7 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights.

Impact and Significance:
- Implications: The case highlights the importance of addressing discrimination allegations in the police force and ensuring appropriate measures are taken to prevent discrimination. It emphasizes the need for thorough investigations into discrimination allegations and the consideration of all relevant evidence.
- Precedents Set: The decision made by the Committee on the Elimination of Racial Discrimination sets a precedent for future cases involving discrimination in the police force.
- Broader Legal Impact: The case underscores the importance of providing equal access to education and protection from discrimination, regardless of race or ethnicity. It also emphasizes the need for adequate remedies and compensation for victims of discrimination.
