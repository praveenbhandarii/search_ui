Case Summary:
The case involves a dispute over the discontinuation of life support for S.S., a patient in a vegetative state. His family members, including his mother (the author), disagree with the court-appointed expert's assessment that S.S. was in a state of minimal consciousness and wanted to be kept alive. The State party argues that the discontinuation of life support is lawful and in the best interests of S.S. The case was brought before the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

Procedural History:
- The case was brought before the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

- The decision was adopted on 17th April 2023.

- The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland was the State party.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The author claims a violation of her son's rights under articles 10, 15, 22 (1), and 25 (f) of the Convention, as well as article 2 (b) (c) and (e) of the Optional Protocol.
