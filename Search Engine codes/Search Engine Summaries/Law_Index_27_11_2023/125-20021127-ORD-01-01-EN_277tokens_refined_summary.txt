Title: Frontier Dispute (Benin/Niger)

Case Overview:
On 3 May 2002, Benin and Niger submitted a joint notification to the International Court of Justice (ICJ) regarding a dispute over the definitive delimitation of their entire boundary. This notification was based on a Special Agreement signed on 15 June 2001 and came into force on 11 April 2002. Pursuant to Article 1 of the Special Agreement, the parties agreed to refer their frontier dispute to a Chamber of the Court, with each party selecting a judge ad hoc. On 27 November 2002, the ICJ unanimously decided to form a special Chamber of five judges to handle the case.

Important Details:
- Benin and Niger jointly notified the ICJ about their boundary dispute.
- The dispute concerns the definitive delimitation of the entire boundary between the two countries.
- A Special Agreement signed on 15 June 2001 and entered into force on 11 April 2002 serves as the legal basis for the dispute.
- The parties agreed to submit the dispute to a Chamber of the Court, with each party choosing a judge ad hoc.
- The ICJ ordered the formation of a special Chamber of five judges to handle the case.

Document Details:
This document is an Order of 8 May 1987, I.C.J. Reports 1987, p. 13, signed by Judge Oda. It emphasizes that for an ad hoc chamber to be constituted under Article 26 of the ICJ Statute, the litigating parties must agree not only on the number of chamber members but also on the selection of those members. Judge Oda refers to previous cases and writings on the chambers procedure of the ICJ.
