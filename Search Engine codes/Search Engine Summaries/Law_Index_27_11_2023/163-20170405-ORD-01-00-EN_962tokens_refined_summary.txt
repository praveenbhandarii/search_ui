Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic regarding a dispute over the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea, Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue, and the legal status of the building housing the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This order, issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) on 5 April 2017, provides important details and information related to the case. The document is cited as Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France), Order of 5 April 2017, I.C.J. Reports 2017, p. 101. The document is part of the ICJ Reports and has the ISSN 00744441 and ISBN 978921-157317-6. The case is listed as General List No. 163, and the jurisdiction lies with the ICJ. France raised preliminary objections to the court's jurisdiction, and the deadline for the presentation of written statements was set for 31 July 2017. The Registrar of the ICJ at the time was Philippe Couvreur. The judges involved in the case include Vice President Yusuf (Acting President), President Abraham, Judges Owada, Tomka, Bennouna, Canado Trindade, Greenwood, Xue, Donoghue, Gaja, Sebutinde, Bhandari, Robinson, Crawford, and Gevorgian.
