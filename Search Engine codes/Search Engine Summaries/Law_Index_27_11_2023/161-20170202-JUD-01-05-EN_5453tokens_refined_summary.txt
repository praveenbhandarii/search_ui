Title: Maritime Delimitation in the Indian Ocean (Somalia v. Kenya)

Case Overview:
This case involves a dispute between Somalia and Kenya regarding the delimitation of their maritime boundaries in the Indian Ocean. Somalia filed an application with the International Court of Justice (ICJ) in 2014, seeking a determination of the complete course of the single maritime boundary between the two states based on international law.

- Somalia and Kenya signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) in 2010 regarding the delimitation of their maritime boundaries.
- Somalia objected to the consideration of Kenya's submission by the Commission on the Limits of the Continental Shelf (CLCS) and refused to implement the MOU.

- Kenya withdrew its objection to the CLCS considering Somalia's submission in 2015, but Somalia did not withdraw its objection until 2017.
- The MOU remains in force, and Kenya may rely on it today.
