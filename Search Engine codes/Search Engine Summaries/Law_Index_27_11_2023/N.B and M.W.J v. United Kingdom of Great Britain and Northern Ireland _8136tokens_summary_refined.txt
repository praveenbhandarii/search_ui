Summary:

Case Details:
- Case name: N.B. and M.W.J. v. the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland
- Case ID: CRPD/C/22/D/43/2017
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities under the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: N.B. and M.W.J. (authors/claimants) and the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (State party)

Procedural History:
- The authors, N.B. and M.W.J., filed a complaint with the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities regarding the closure of the Independent Living Fund.
- The Committee reviewed the case and issued a decision on 6 September 2019.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The authors argued that the closure of the Independent Living Fund without adequate consultation and impact assessment violated their rights under Articles 17, 19, 20, 30, and 31 of the Convention.
- The Committee agreed with the authors and held that the closure of the Fund was indeed a violation of their rights.
- The Committee also found that the failure to provide an effective remedy for the authors further violated their rights.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee ruled that the closure of the Independent Living Fund without adequate consultation and impact assessment violated the authors' rights under the Convention.
- The Committee emphasized the importance of consultation and impact assessment in the development and implementation of policies and programs affecting individuals with disabilities.

Impact and Significance:
- This decision has implications for the rights of individuals with disabilities to equal access to support services and the ability to live independently.
- It highlights the importance of ensuring that policies and programs affecting individuals with disabilities are developed and implemented in a way that respects their rights and dignity.
