Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between the Republic of Equatorial Guinea and France regarding the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea, Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue, and the legal status of the building housing the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This judgment document from the International Court of Justice (ICJ) provides a summary of the case and its key points. It includes relevant citations, key dates, parties involved, and the issues at hand. The document also mentions a dissenting opinion by Judge Xue and provides details about the case brought by Equatorial Guinea against France.

Important Details:
- The central issue in the dispute is the ownership of the building in Paris claimed by Equatorial Guinea as part of its diplomatic mission, which France disputes.
- The ICJ is tasked with interpreting the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations to determine the status of the building.
- The Court holds that the Vienna Convention does not grant the sending state (Equatorial Guinea) the unilateral right to designate premises of its diplomatic mission, and the receiving state (France) has the power to object.
- The Court determines that the issue of ownership of the building is not solely a legal matter under French law but also a matter of international law.
- The majority of the Court interprets the Vienna Convention to require the consent of both states for the building to acquire the status of premises of the mission.
- France's objection to Equatorial Guinea's designation of the building as its diplomatic mission is deemed justified.
- France's conduct of searches and seizures in the building is found not to violate Equatorial Guinea's immunities and criminal proceedings.

Additional Information:
- The case involves the interpretation and application of the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations.
- The events in question took place between July 27, 2012, and June 13, 2016, with Equatorial Guinea instituting proceedings against France on the latter date.
- Judge Xue Hanqin provided a dissenting opinion.
- The case raises issues related to the balance between sending and receiving states in diplomatic relations and the protection of diplomatic mission immunities and privileges.
