Title: Whaling in the Antarctic (Australia v. Japan: New Zealand intervening)

Case Overview:
Australia initiated proceedings against Japan on May 31, 2010, alleging that Japan's large-scale whaling program in the Antarctic, known as JARPA II, violated its obligations under the International Convention for the Regulation of Whaling (ICRW) and other international obligations for the preservation of marine mammals and the marine environment.

Document Overview:
This document is an order issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) in Case Number 148. It was dated February 6, 2013, and pertains to the case "Whaling in the Antarctic (Australia v. Japan: New Zealand intervening)." The document provides important details about the case, including the parties involved, relevant articles, New Zealand's declaration of intervention, and the proceedings.

Important Details:
- New Zealand filed a Declaration of Intervention in the case, asserting its status as a party to the International Convention for the Regulation of Whaling and questioning the construction of Article VIII of the Convention.
- The Declaration was accompanied by a list of supporting documents.
- Australia considered New Zealand's intervention admissible, while Japan did not object to it.
- The Court addressed concerns over procedural equality and confirmed the admissibility of New Zealand's intervention.
- New Zealand will be bound by the Court's construction of the Convention in its judgment.
- The Court clarified that Australia and New Zealand cannot be regarded as parties in the same interest, affecting the appointment of a judge ad hoc.
- Copies of the pleadings and documents were communicated to New Zealand upon request.
- The ICJ unanimously decided that New Zealand's Declaration of Intervention is admissible.
- The ICJ reserved the subsequent procedure for further decision, with a time limit of April 4, 2013, set for certain actions.

Additional Information:
- The order was issued in English and French, with the English text being authoritative.
- It was signed by Peter Tomka, President of the ICJ, and Philippe Couvreur, Registrar.
- Judges Owada, Canado Trindade, and Gaja appended separate opinions to the order.
- The order was issued in accordance with the rules of the ICJ.
