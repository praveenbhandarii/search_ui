Title: Land Boundary in the Northern Part of Isla Portillos (Costa Rica v. Nicaragua)

Case Overview:
The case involves a dispute between Costa Rica and Nicaragua regarding the precise definition of the boundary in the area of Los Portillos/Harbor Head Lagoon and the establishment of a new military camp by Nicaragua on the beach of Isla Portillos. Costa Rica filed an Application instituting proceedings against Nicaragua on 16 January 2017.

Important Details:
- The case number is 165.
- The judgment was given on June 27, 2007.
- The case involves a dispute over maritime delimitation and land boundary.
- Costa Rica did not attempt to invoke the 1980 Treaty, and Nicaragua did not provide proof of the treaty's registration or Costa Rica's agreement to be bound by it.
- Nicaragua relied on the 1980 Treaty to establish that Costa Rica had renounced certain maritime entitlements.
- Only parties to an unregistered agreement can be concerned by the sanction of Article 102, paragraph 2.
- The Court did not acknowledge the parties' obligations under Article 102 of the Charter.
- The judge expresses disappointment that the parties to the 1980 Treaty treated their obligations under Article 102 of the Charter in a cavalier fashion.
- The judge emphasizes the importance of maintaining the international rule of law and states should respect their obligations under Article 102 of the Charter.
