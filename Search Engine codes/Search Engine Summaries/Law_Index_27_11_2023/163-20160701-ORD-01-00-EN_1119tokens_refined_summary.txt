Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic regarding the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea and the legal status of the building housing the Equatorial Guinean Embassy in France.

Document Overview:
This order document, issued by the International Court of Justice, provides details about the case. It is part of the Reports of Judgments, Advisory Opinions and Orders of the Court. The order was issued on July 1, 2016, and the French text is considered authoritative. The case involves Vice-President Yusuf as the Acting President, President Abraham, and several other judges. The official citation for this order is Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France), Order of 1 July 2016, I.C.J. Reports 2016, p. 246.

Important Details:
- The dispute revolves around the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea and the legal status of the building housing the Equatorial Guinean Embassy in France.
- The application was filed on June 13, 2016, and a meeting between the Vice-President of the Court and the Agents of the Parties took place on June 29, 2016.
- The Republic of Equatorial Guinea is required to file its Memorial by January 3, 2017, while the French Republic must file its Counter-Memorial by July 3, 2017.
- The French text of the agreement between the parties is considered authoritative.
- The order was issued in French and English, with the French text being authoritative.
- The document is issued in three copies, with one copy placed in the Court's archives and the other two copies transmitted to the Governments of Equatorial Guinea and France, respectively.

Additional Information:
- The document is part of the proceedings of Case Number 163 at the International Court of Justice.
- The order was issued at the Peace Palace in The Hague.
- Abdulqawi Ahmed Yusuf, Vice-President of the International Court of Justice, and Philippe Couvreur, Registrar of the Court, signed the document.
