Summary:

Case Details:
- Case name: Grainne Sherlock vs. The State Party of Australia
- Case ID: CRPD/C/24/D/20/2014
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Dates: 19 March 2021 (adoption of Views), 30 April 2021 (initial submission)
- Key Parties Involved: Grainne Sherlock (author), The State Party of Australia (party B)

Procedural History:
- The case was brought before the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The Committee adopted its Views on 19 March 2021.
- The initial submission was made on 30 April 2021.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee found that the State Party failed to prove that the refusal of Sherlock's visa application was based on reasonable grounds.
- The State Party's refusal to consider Sherlock's proposals for covering the costs of her treatment was deemed unreasonable.
- The State Party's refusal to permit a private agreement regarding healthcare costs was discriminatory and contrary to the Convention.
- The document raises concerns about the State party's visa criteria and their compliance with the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR), particularly with regard to the consideration of private arrangements between employers and employees concerning healthcare costs.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee held that the State Party violated Sherlock's rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The Committee recommended that the State Party provide Sherlock with an effective remedy, including reimbursement of any legal costs incurred and compensation.
- The Committee also recommended that the State Party ensure that private arrangements regarding healthcare costs are part of the visa criteria and provide a written response to the Committee within six months.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of preventing discrimination against people with disabilities in the immigration process.
- It emphasizes the need for states to ensure that their immigration laws do not perpetuate discrimination.
- The decision sets a precedent for future cases involving disability-based discrimination in immigration.
- The document has implications for the State party's visa criteria and its compliance with the ICCPR, and may also have relevance for other states that have ratified the ICCPR and are considering similar visa criteria.
