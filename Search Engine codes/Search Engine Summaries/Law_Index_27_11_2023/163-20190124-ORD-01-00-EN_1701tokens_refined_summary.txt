Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic on 13 June 2016. The dispute revolves around the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea, Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue, and the legal status of the building housing the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This order, issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) on 24 January 2019, provides important details regarding the case. The document includes the case number (163), the date of the order, and the court involved (ICJ). The President of the ICJ at the time was Abdulqawi Ahmed Yusuf, and the Registrar was Philippe Couvreur. The application filed by Equatorial Guinea against France is mentioned, along with the order issued by the ICJ regarding the filing of a Memorial by Equatorial Guinea and a Counter-Memorial by France. The document also highlights the preliminary objections raised by France and the subsequent judgment of the ICJ on the jurisdiction of the court. Additionally, it mentions the filing of the Counter-Memorial by France and Equatorial Guinea's desire to respond to certain arguments and present new documents in a Reply.

Important Details:
- Equatorial Guinea instituted proceedings against France regarding the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue and the legal status of the Embassy building.
- The ICJ fixed timelimits for the filing of a Memorial by Equatorial Guinea and a Counter-Memorial by France.
- France raised preliminary objections to the jurisdiction of the ICJ.
- The ICJ declared it had jurisdiction to entertain Equatorial Guinea's Application concerning the status of the Embassy building.
- France filed its Counter-Memorial within the timelimit set by the ICJ.
- Equatorial Guinea requested to respond to certain arguments and present new documents in a Reply.
