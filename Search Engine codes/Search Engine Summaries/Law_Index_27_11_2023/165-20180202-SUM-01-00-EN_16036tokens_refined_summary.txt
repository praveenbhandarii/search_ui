Title: Land Boundary in the Northern Part of Isla Portillos (Costa Rica v. Nicaragua)

Case Overview:
This case, numbered 165, involves a dispute between Costa Rica and Nicaragua regarding the precise definition of the boundary in the area of Los Portillos/Harbor Head Lagoon and the establishment of a new military camp by Nicaragua on the beach of Isla Portillos. Costa Rica filed an Application instituting proceedings against Nicaragua on 16 January 2017.

Important Details:
- The International Court of Justice (ICJ) has jurisdiction over both cases based on Article XXXI of the Pact of Bogot and the declarations recognizing the compulsory jurisdiction of the Court under Article 36 of the Statute.
- The disputes between Costa Rica and Nicaragua involve the establishment of maritime boundaries and the land boundary separating the Los Portillos/Harbor Head Lagoon sandbar from Isla Portillos.
- Costa Rica instituted proceedings against Nicaragua on maritime boundaries in the Caribbean Sea and the Pacific Ocean on 25 February 2014.
- The Court decided to join the proceedings in the case concerning Maritime Delimitation and the case concerning the Northern Part of Isla Portillos.
- Key legal issues include jurisdiction, maritime delimitation, land boundary, compulsory jurisdiction, and the validity and interpretation of international agreements.
- The Court held public hearings from 3 to 13 July 2017.

Document Details:
This document, dated 2 February 2018, is a summary of the judgment and orders issued by the International Court of Justice (ICJ) in Case Number 165. The case involves the dispute between Costa Rica and Nicaragua over the land boundary in the northern part of Isla Portillos. The judgment was delivered by President Abraham, Vice President Yusuf, and several other judges.

Additional Information:
- The Court found that Nicaragua violated Costa Rica's sovereignty and territorial integrity by establishing and maintaining a military camp on the beach of Isla Portillos. The camp must be removed from Costa Rica's territory.
- The Court determined the maritime boundary between Costa Rica and Nicaragua in the Caribbean Sea, taking into account the coastlines and special circumstances such as the instability of the sandspit near the mouth of the San Juan River.
- The Court considered the delimitation of the territorial sea and exclusive economic zone between the two countries.
- Previous cases and treaties involving third states were evaluated for their relevance to the delimitation.
- The judgment is final and binding.
- The judgment was based on the principles of international law, including UNCLOS and the judgment of the International Court of Justice in the case of France v. Ireland (1977).
- The Court considered the proximity of the starting point of delimitation to the Elena Peninsula as a crucial factor.
- The Court established a provisional equidistance line for the Caribbean Sea and a simplified adjusted line for the Pacific Ocean.
- The Court's decision was based on the principles of international law, including UNCLOS and previous judgments of the Court.
- The Court did not address the legal effects of the bilateral boundary treaties between Costa Rica, Colombia, and Panama.
- Some judges appended separate opinions and declarations to the judgment, expressing their disagreement with certain aspects of the Court's decision.
- The Court did not address in detail the methodologies employed to delimit the territorial sea and the exclusive economic zone and continental shelf.
- The Court recognized the special circumstances of the enclave and called for a special solution.
- The Court provided sketch maps illustrating the land boundary and the delimitation of the territorial sea in the Caribbean Sea and the Pacific Ocean.
