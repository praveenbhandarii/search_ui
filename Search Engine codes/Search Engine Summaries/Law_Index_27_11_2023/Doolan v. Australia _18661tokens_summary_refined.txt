Summary:

Case Details:
- Case Name: Manuway (Kerry) Doolan v. Australia
- Case ID: CRPD/C/66/D/31/2003
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Dates: 19 September 2013 (initial submission), 22 November 2013 (not issued in document form), 30 August 2019 (views adopted)
- Key Parties Involved: Manuway (Kerry) Doolan (author), Australia (state party)

Procedural History:
- The author, Manuway (Kerry) Doolan, filed a complaint with the United Nations Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities alleging violations of his rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The State party, Australia, submitted a response to the complaint.
- The Committee reviewed the submissions and adopted its views on 30 August 2019.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee found that the State party violated the author's rights under various articles of the Convention, including articles 5, 12, 13, 14, and 15.
- The prolonged detention of the author without being convicted of a criminal offense, his detention with convicted prisoners, and his involuntary treatment were deemed violations of his rights under Article 15 of the Convention.
- The State party's failure to provide the author with an effective remedy, including reimbursement of legal costs and compensation, was also found to be a violation of Article 15.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee recommended the immediate release of the author and the provision of appropriate support for independent living and community inclusion.
- The Committee called on the State party to take measures to prevent similar violations in the future and to provide the author with an effective remedy, including compensation.

Impact and Significance:
- This case highlights the importance of treating individuals with disabilities equally and without discrimination, particularly in the criminal justice system.
- It emphasizes the need for states to protect and respect the rights of persons with disabilities, as outlined in the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The Committee's views and recommendations serve as guidance for states and other stakeholders in ensuring the rights of persons with disabilities.
