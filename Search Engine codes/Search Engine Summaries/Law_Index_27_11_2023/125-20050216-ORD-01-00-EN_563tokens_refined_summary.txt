Title: Case Concerning the Frontier Dispute (Benin/Niger)

Case Overview:
The case involves a frontier dispute between Benin and Niger. The dispute was brought before the International Court of Justice (ICJ) through a Special Agreement signed on 15 June 2001. The agreement entered into force on 11 April 2002, and the Court formed a special Chamber of five judges to handle the case.

Important Details:
- The dispute concerns the definitive delimitation of the entire boundary between Benin and Niger.
- The Special Agreement between the two parties was signed on 15 June 2001 and entered into force on 11 April 2002.
- The Court unanimously decided to form a special Chamber of five judges to handle the case on 27 November 2002.
- The order document is dated 16 February 2005.
- The case is listed as Case Number 125 in the ICJ's General List.
- The President of the Chamber is Judge Ranjeva.
- The judges involved in the case are Judge Kooijmans, Judge Abraham, Judge ad hoc Bedjaoui, and Judge ad hoc Bennouna.
- The Registrar of the Court is Philippe COUVREUR.
- The document is in French, and the authoritative text is also in French.
- The signatories of the document are President SHI Jiuyong and Registrar Philippe COUVREUR.
