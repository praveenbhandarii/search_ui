Title: Immunities and Criminal Proceedings (Equatorial Guinea v. France)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Equatorial Guinea initiated proceedings against the French Republic regarding the immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the Second Vice-President of Equatorial Guinea, Mr. Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue, and the legal status of the building housing the Embassy of Equatorial Guinea in France.

Document Overview:
This judgment document, dated 21/02/19, contains the separate opinion of Judge Abraham. It addresses Equatorial Guinea's claim that France breached Article 4 of the Palermo Convention by initiating criminal proceedings against its Vice-President and attaching part of its property. Judge Abraham agrees with the general tenor of the judgment but disagrees with the Court's reasoning on the scope of Article 4.

Important Details:
- Equatorial Guinea argues that France violated Article 4 of the Palermo Convention, which deals with sovereign equality and non-intervention in the domestic affairs of other states.
- Judge Abraham finds the Court's reasoning on the scope of Article 4 to be complicated and legally flawed.
- The dispute concerns the interpretation and application of Article 4, which deals with the immunities of states, state property, and state officials.
- France disputes Equatorial Guinea's claims, arguing that Article 4 is a general clause and does not incorporate customary international law.
- The ICJ held that Article 4 does not incorporate customary international law and is a provision stating general principles of international law.
- The case was heard on September 9, 2019, and the judgment was delivered on February 19, 2020.
- Judge Abraham wrote the judgment, joined by Judges Bennouna, Donoghue, Gaja, Greenwood, Hernandez, Keith, and Simma.
