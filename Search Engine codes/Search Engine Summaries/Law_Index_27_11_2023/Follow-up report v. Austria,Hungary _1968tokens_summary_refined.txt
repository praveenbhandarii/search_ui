Case Details:

- Case ID: CRPD/C/10/D/4/2011
- Court/Tribunal: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Document Details: Follow-up progress report on individual communications
- Document Dates: 9 September 2013, 26 August 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Hungary, Bujdos et al.
- Case Name: Bujdos et al. v. Hungary

Procedural History:

- The Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities adopted follow-up views on 9 September 2013.
- The State party provided replies on 26 March 2014, 8 July 2014, and 29 June 2015.
- The Committee held a closed meeting with the State party on 6 April 2017 and adopted a decision on 17 August 2017.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:

- The Committee found that Hungary was noncompliant with article 12 of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.
- The noncompliance was regarding the authors' access to compensation amounts transferred to their bank accounts due to the restrictive conceptualization of guardianship.
- The Committee urged Hungary to revisit its conceptualization of guardianship and ensure the authors' effective access to the compensation amounts.

Decisions and Rulings:

- The Committee decided to discontinue the follow-up procedure with C assessment for the individual remedy and A assessment for the general remedy.

Impact and Significance:

- The decision to discontinue the follow-up procedure may have implications for the authors and their access to compensation.
- The decision may also set a precedent for future cases involving the restrictive conceptualization of guardianship.
