- Document Dates: March 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Author (Makarov) vs. State Party (Lithuania)
- Case Name: Makarov v. Lithuania

Case Summary:
The case of Makarov v. Lithuania was brought before the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The author alleged that the State party had violated several articles of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities, including the right to access to justice, the right to equality before a court, and the right to compensation for damages incurred. The Committee found that the State party had not provided the author with an effective and comprehensive remedy for the violations committed and had not ensured the author's access to justice on an equal basis with others.

Procedural History:
The case was submitted to the Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities by the author, Mr. Makarov, against Lithuania. The Committee adopted Views on the case on August 18, 2017, finding that Lithuania had violated the author's rights under the Convention.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The State party failed to provide the author with access to the minutes of the court hearings and did not reimburse the author for the legal costs incurred throughout the legal proceedings. Additionally, the State party did not amend its legislation to ensure the author's right to access to justice and equality before a court. The Committee recommended that the State party provide the author with an effective and comprehensive remedy for the violations committed and ensure the author's access to justice on an equal basis with others.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities found that Lithuania had violated the author's rights under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. The State party was instructed to provide the author with an effective and comprehensive remedy for the violations committed and to ensure the author's access to justice on an equal basis with others.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of ensuring access to justice for persons with disabilities and the obligation of States parties to provide effective and comprehensive remedies for violations of their rights. It has implications for the implementation of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities and the protection of the rights of persons with disabilities worldwide.
