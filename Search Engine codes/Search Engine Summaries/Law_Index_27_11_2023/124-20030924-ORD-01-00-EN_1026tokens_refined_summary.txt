Title: Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia)

Case Overview:
The Republic of Nicaragua filed a case against the Republic of Colombia regarding a dispute over territory and maritime delimitation. Nicaragua submitted its Memorial within the given time limit, but Colombia filed preliminary objections to jurisdiction, leading to the suspension of the proceedings on the merits.

Important Details:
- Case Number: 124
- Case Name: Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia)
- Date of Application: 6 December 2001
- Date of Memorial: 28 April 2003
- Date of Preliminary Objections: 21 July 2003
- Timelimit for Nicaragua's written statement: 26 January 2004
- Subsequent procedure: Reserved for further decision
- Ruling: The Court rejects Colombia's preliminary objections and proceeds to the next stage of the proceedings.

Document Details:
This document is an order issued by the International Court of Justice on 24 September 2003. It addresses the preliminary objections raised by Colombia and refers to the case's subsequent procedure. The ruling is written in both English and French, with the English text being authoritative. It was signed by the Court's President, SHI Jiuyong, and Registrar, Philippe COUVREUR. Three copies were made, with one placed in the Court's archives and the others transmitted to the Governments of Nicaragua and Colombia.

Additional Information:
- The ruling was issued at the Peace Palace in The Hague.
- The document is cited as International Court of Justice Reports 2003, p. 158 (official citation) and Territorial and Maritime Dispute (Nicaragua v. Colombia), Order of 24 September 2003 (short citation).
- The document was issued in three copies, with the English version being authoritative.
