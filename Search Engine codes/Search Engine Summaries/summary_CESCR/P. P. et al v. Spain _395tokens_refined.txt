Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/58/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: Alleged victims: P.P. et al. State party: Spain
- Case Name: P.P. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors submitted an individual communication to the Committee on October 1, 2018, regarding their eviction from a property they were occupying without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide the authors with adequate alternative housing. The authors later requested to set aside their communication as they had obtained social housing, and the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights received an individual communication (No. 58/2018) from alleged victims (P.P. et al.) against Spain on October 1, 2018. The Committee discontinued its consideration of communication No. 58/2018 at its meeting on February 22, 2021, in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol. The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixty-ninth session (February 15-March 5, 2021).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The communication concerned the right to adequate housing (Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights) and the eviction of the authors without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the authors.

Decisions and Rulings:
The authors later requested to set aside their communication as they had obtained social housing, and the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the role of international bodies in addressing issues related to housing rights. It also demonstrates the significance of genuine consultation with affected individuals in the context of interim measures.
