Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/68/D/111/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: V.D.N. et al. (authors)
  - Party B: Spain (State party)
- Case Name: V.D.N. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, including V.D.N., submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that their eviction from their home for failure to vacate after the expiration of their lease violated their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on February 15, 2019, and concerned the eviction of the author, her son, and her mother from their home due to failure to vacate after the expiration of their lease.
- The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the authors, on February 17, 2019.
- The Committee was unable to contact the authors and decided to discontinue consideration of the communication at its sixty-eighth session (September 28 - October 16, 2020) in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: 11(1) (right to adequate housing)
- Procedural issue: Request for interim measures to suspend eviction or provide adequate alternative housing
- Substantive issue: Right to adequate housing
- Subject matter: Eviction for failure to vacate after expiration of lease

Decisions and Rulings:
- The UNCESCR requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction of the authors and their family pending the consideration of the communication or to provide them with adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with them.
- The Committee discontinued consideration of the communication under article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol due to the inability to contact the authors.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of genuine consultation with affected parties in cases involving the right to adequate housing.
- The decision to discontinue consideration due to lack of contact with the authors sets a precedent for the Committee's handling of similar cases in the future.
