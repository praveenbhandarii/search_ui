Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/192/2020
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - S.S.G. et al. (authors and their two children)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: S.S.G. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, who were occupying a property without legal title, were evicted and requested adequate housing from the State party. The Committee considered the communication and decided to discontinue its consideration due to the authors taking up residence in different municipalities following the eviction.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 192/2020 was submitted by S.S.G. et al. alleging eviction for occupation without legal title and violation of the right to adequate housing. The Committee's decision to discontinue consideration of the communication was adopted on 25 March 2021 during the sixty-ninth session.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the eviction for occupation without legal title, which raised concerns regarding the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The authors requested interim measures, but the Committee discontinued the consideration due to the authors' withdrawal of the case.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 192/2020 following the authors' request to withdraw the case, as they had taken up residence in different municipalities following the eviction.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the case highlights the importance of procedural issues and the impact of the authors' actions on the Committee's consideration of the communication. It also emphasizes the significance of the right to adequate housing in cases of eviction for occupation without legal title.
