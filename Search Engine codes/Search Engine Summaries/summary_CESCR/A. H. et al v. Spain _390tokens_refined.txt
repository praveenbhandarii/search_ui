Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/71/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: Alleged victims: A.H. et al. State party: Spain
- Case Name: A.H. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors submitted an individual communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. Later, the Committee discontinued consideration of the communication as the authors had left the apartment and lost contact with the organization representing them.

Procedural History:
- The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights received an individual communication (No. 71/2018) from alleged victims A.H. et al. against Spain on October 29, 2018.
- The communication concerned the right to adequate housing (Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights) and the eviction of the authors without legal title.
- The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the authors.
- The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication at its sixty-ninth session (February 15-March 5, 2021) after the organization representing the authors reported that they had left the apartment and lost contact with them.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: 11 (1)
- Submitted by: Alleged victims: A.H. et al.
- Procedural issue: Request for interim measures
- Substantive issue: Eviction for occupation without legal title
- Subject matter: Right to adequate housing
- Date of communication: 29 October 2018

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights considered an individual communication against Spain regarding the eviction of alleged victims without legal title and requested interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. However, the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication due to the lack of contact with the authors.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of genuine consultation with victims in cases of eviction and the right to adequate housing as protected under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
