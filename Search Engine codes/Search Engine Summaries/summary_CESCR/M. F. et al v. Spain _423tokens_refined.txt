Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/174/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - A.M.F. and J.A.M.F. (authors)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: A.M.F. and J.A.M.F. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, A.M.F. and J.A.M.F., submitted a communication to the Committee on behalf of themselves and their three children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. Later, the authors and Spain agreed to withdraw the case as the family had concluded a private rental contract for another dwelling with assistance from the Valencia Social Inclusion Income scheme.

Procedural History:
- The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights received an individual communication from A.M.F. and J.A.M.F. on behalf of themselves and their three children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction without legal title.
- The Committee requested Spain, the state party, to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, and registered the communication on December 11, 2019.
- At its meeting on February 22, 2021, the Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication, as the family had concluded a private rental contract for another dwelling with assistance from the Valencia Social Inclusion Income scheme.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Right to adequate housing, eviction without legal title
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11 (1) of the Covenant
- Court's Analysis: The Committee considered the communication and requested interim measures to address the alleged violation of the right to adequate housing. The case was later withdrawn after the family found alternative housing with assistance from a social inclusion scheme.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication after the family had found a new dwelling with assistance from a social inclusion scheme.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of interim measures in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights, particularly in cases of eviction without legal title. It also emphasizes the role of social inclusion schemes in providing alternative housing for vulnerable individuals and families.
