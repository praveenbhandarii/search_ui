Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/72/D/90/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Mohamed Bouayad El Mejbar and Bouchra Chabou (authors and alleged victims)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: Mohamed Bouayad El Mejbar and Bouchra Chabou v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, Mohamed Bouayad El Mejbar and Bouchra Chabou, along with their three minor children, faced eviction due to the termination of their lease. They claimed a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted a communication to the UNCESCR on December 18, 2018, requesting interim measures to suspend the eviction pending the consideration of the communication or to provide adequate alternative housing. The Committee discontinued consideration of the communication on October 10, 2022, after the authors requested discontinuance, citing that they had found alternative housing.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issue addressed was the eviction of the authors due to the termination of their lease, which raised concerns about their housing rights under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee considered the request for interim measures and the substantive issue of eviction.

Decisions and Rulings:
The UNCESCR discontinued consideration of the communication after the authors' request for discontinuance, as they had found alternative housing.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of protecting the right to adequate housing under international human rights law. It also emphasizes the role of the UNCESCR in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, concerning communication No. 90/2018.
