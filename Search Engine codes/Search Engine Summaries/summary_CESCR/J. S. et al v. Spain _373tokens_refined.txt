Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/57/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: Alleged victims: J.S. et al. State party: Spain
- Case Name: J.S. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors submitted an individual communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction for occupation without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. Later, the authors requested to withdraw the case as they had secured accommodation in social housing, and the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
- The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights received an individual communication (No. 57/2018) from alleged victims J.S. et al. against Spain on September 27, 2018.
- The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the authors.
- The authors requested to withdraw the case on February 22, 2021, as they had secured accommodation in social housing.
- The Committee discontinued its consideration of communication No. 57/2018 at its sixty-ninth session (February 15-March 5, 2021) in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: 11 (1)
- Submitted by: Alleged victims: J.S. et al.
- Procedural issue: Request for interim measures
- Substantive issue: Eviction for occupation without legal title, right to adequate housing
- Subject matter: Housing
- Date of communication: 27 September 2018

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights considered an individual communication against Spain regarding the eviction of the authors without legal title and their right to adequate housing. The Committee requested interim measures from Spain but later discontinued the consideration of the communication after the authors withdrew their case following the provision of social housing.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the role of international bodies in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights. It also demonstrates the potential for resolution through alternative means, such as the provision of social housing.
