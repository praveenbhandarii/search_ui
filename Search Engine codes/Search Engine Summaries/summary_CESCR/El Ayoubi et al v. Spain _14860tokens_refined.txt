Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/54/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 23 March 2021
- Key Parties Involved: Ftima El Ayoubi, Mohamed El Azouan Azouz, and their young son, Haron El Azouan El Ayoubi (Authors) vs. Spain (State Party)
- Case Name: Ftima El Ayoubi and Mohamed El Azouan Azouz v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, who are a Moroccan citizen and a Spanish citizen, respectively, and their young son, are alleging a violation of their rights under article 11 (1) of the Covenant due to their eviction from a property they have been living in since 2016 without alternative accommodation.

Procedural History:
- The authors, acting on their own behalf and on behalf of their young son, submitted the communication on 17 September 2018.
- The Committee registered the communication and requested interim measures on 19 September 2018.
- The State party requested the lifting of interim measures and the discontinuance of the communication.
- The Committee refused the State party's request to lift interim measures and to discontinue the communication on 31 October 2019.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The main issues addressed in the case include the right to adequate housing, protection against forced eviction, social security and social protection, and equality and non-discrimination.
- The Committee analyzed the State party's obligations under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR) and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found that Spain had violated the family's right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant.
- The Committee recommended that Spain provide the family with an effective remedy, including reassessing their application for social housing and providing financial compensation for the violations suffered.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for the protection of the right to adequate housing and the prohibition of forced evictions under the ICESCR.
- It highlights the importance of ensuring alternative housing for those affected by eviction and the need for states to fulfill their obligations in providing adequate housing.
