Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/96/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 21 November 2019
- Key Parties Involved: M.P. and others (authors), State party (Spain)
- Case Name: M.P. and others v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, who were tenants of a building that was foreclosed upon by the previous owner, were evicted by the new owners. They claimed that their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights had been violated.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted a communication under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, alleging violations of their rights by the State party, Spain. The communication was withdrawn by the authors after signing a new lease with the owners.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue involved the eviction of the authors due to termination of their lease by the new owners following foreclosure on the previous owner. The case raised issues related to housing and property rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights discontinued its consideration of communication No. 96/2019 in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Impact and Significance:
The decision highlights the Committee's approach to cases involving housing and property rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. It also underscores the importance of parties' actions, such as withdrawal of a case, in the Committee's decision-making process.
