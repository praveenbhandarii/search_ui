Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/36/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: M.H. and I.M. (authors) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: M.H. and I.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, a disabled couple facing eviction without legal title, submitted an individual communication to the Committee on June 15, 2018, requesting interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. However, they failed to respond to the Committee's requests for comments, leading the Committee to discontinue its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The authors, M.H. and I.M., submitted an individual communication to the Committee on June 15, 2018, regarding their eviction for occupation without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. However, the authors did not respond to the Committee's repeated requests for comments, and the Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issue addressed in this case is the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The authors, M.H. and I.M., alleged a violation of their right to adequate housing due to their impending eviction without legal title. However, the Committee's analysis was hindered by the authors' non-response to requests for comments.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication under rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure due to the authors' failure to respond to the Committee's requests for comments.

Impact and Significance:
This decision sets a precedent for the importance of active participation and cooperation with the Committee in individual communications. It highlights the significance of responding to requests for comments in order to facilitate the Committee's consideration of alleged violations of economic, social, and cultural rights.
