Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/75/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 23 April 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - N.J.M.C. (author and alleged victim)
  - Spain (state party)
- Case Name: N.J.M.C. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, N.J.M.C., submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that her rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights had been violated due to an eviction on foreclosure by the owner of her home. The Committee considered the communication and requested additional documentation from the author, which she failed to provide despite numerous requests. Therefore, the Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication due to the author's lack of interest in the proceedings.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by N.J.M.C. on 9 November 2018, alleging violations of the right to adequate housing under article 11(1) of the Covenant. The State party, Spain, was involved in the proceedings. The Committee considered the communication under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the eviction on foreclosure by the owner, which raised concerns about the right to adequate housing under the Covenant. The Committee's analysis focused on the failure of the author to provide requested documentation, which led to the discontinuation of the consideration of the communication.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 75/2018 due to the author's lack of interest in the proceedings, in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication highlights the importance of active participation and cooperation in the proceedings before the UNCESCR. It also emphasizes the Committee's adherence to its procedural rules and the consequences of non-compliance by the author.
