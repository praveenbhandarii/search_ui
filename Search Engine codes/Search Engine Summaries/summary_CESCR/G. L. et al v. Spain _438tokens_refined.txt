Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/59/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: G. L. (author and alleged victim) and Spain (State party)
- Case Name: G. L. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, a single mother, was facing eviction from social housing that was subsequently sold to a private fund. She submitted a communication to the Committee on behalf of herself and her minor children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. Later, the author withdrew the case, claiming to have found alternative housing with assistance from local associations and the community. The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by G.L. on behalf of herself and her minor children to the United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The alleged victim was the State party, Spain. The Committee requested interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. The author later withdrew the case, leading to the discontinuation of consideration by the Committee.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the case was the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee considered the withdrawal of the case as a procedural issue and discontinued its consideration in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing. However, following the withdrawal of the case by the author, the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the role of international bodies in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights. It also underscores the significance of local associations and community support in assisting individuals facing housing challenges.
