  - G.J. and others (authors) vs. Spain (State party)
Case Name: G.J. and others v. Spain
Case Summary: The authors, G.J. and others, submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that they were facing eviction from their homes without legal title. Despite repeated attempts, the Committee was unable to contact the authors, and therefore decided to discontinue the consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on May 18, 2018
- The Committee attempted to contact the authors but was unable to do so

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: Not specified
- Submitted by: G.J. and others
- Procedural issue: Failure to contact the authors
- Substantive issue: Eviction without legal title
- Subject matter: Housing and property rights

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 33/2018
- The decision was made in accordance with article 17 of the Committee's provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixtysixth session (September 30 to October 18, 2019)

Impact and Significance:
- The decision to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 33/2018 was communicated to the parties concerned
- The Committee's decision is final and there are no further steps to be taken in this matter
