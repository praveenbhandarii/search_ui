Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/28/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2019
- Key Parties Involved: T.H. (author), Spain (State party)
- Case Name: T.H. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, T.H., submitted a communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights alleging that Spain had violated their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights by evicting them from their home without legal title. Despite repeated attempts, the Committee was unable to contact the author and therefore decided to discontinue the consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights received a communication (No. 28/2018) from T.H., alleging eviction without legal title in Spain. Despite repeated attempts, the Committee was unable to contact the author. The Committee discontinued the consideration of the communication in accordance with Article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixtysixth session (30 September to 18 October 2019).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: Not specified
- Submitted by: T.H.
- Procedural issue: Failure to contact the author
- Substantive issue: Eviction without legal title
- Subject matter: Housing and property rights
- Date of communication: 9 April 2018

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights discontinued the consideration of a communication alleging eviction without legal title in Spain due to the lack of response from the author despite repeated attempts to contact them.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication has implications for the handling of similar cases in the future, particularly in relation to the procedural requirements for communication submissions and the Committee's decision-making process.
