Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/80/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - M.I. and E.M. (authors and alleged victims)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: M.I. and E.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, M.I. and E.M., alleged that they were facing eviction from a dwelling they had rented from someone who turned out not to be the owner. The Committee requested the State party to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. The authors later requested discontinuance of the communication, which was granted by the Committee at its 70th session.

Procedural History:
- The authors, M.I. and E.M., submitted a communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights on 16 November 2018.
- The communication was on behalf of themselves and their two minor children.
- The Committee registered the communication and requested the State party to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing.
- The authors requested discontinuance of the communication on the grounds that they had secured alternative housing.
- The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication at its 70th session (27 September - 15 October 2021).

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11(1) of the Covenant
- Court's Analysis: The authors alleged a violation of their right to adequate housing and requested interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication at its meeting on 12 October 2021, as the authors had secured alternative housing.

Impact and Significance:
- The discontinuance of the communication by the Committee signifies the resolution of the authors' housing issue and the fulfillment of their right to adequate housing.
