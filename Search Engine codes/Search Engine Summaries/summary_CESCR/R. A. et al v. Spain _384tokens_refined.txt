Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/72/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: Alleged victim (P.R.A.), State party (Spain)
- Case Name: P.R.A. v. Spain
- Case Summary: P.R.A. submitted a communication to the UNCESCR regarding their eviction from a property they were occupying without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, but ultimately discontinued consideration of the communication due to lack of contact with the author.

Procedural History:
The communication was registered by the Committee on 8 November 2018 and interim measures were requested to suspend the eviction of the author and her son pending consideration of the communication or to provide them with adequate alternative housing. The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication at its meeting on 22 February 2021 due to loss of contact with the author.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the case is the eviction for occupation without legal title and the right to adequate housing. The relevant legal provision is Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR). The Committee's analysis focused on the discontinuation of consideration due to lack of contact with the author, in accordance with Article 17 of the provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the ICESCR.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee ultimately discontinued consideration of the communication due to the loss of contact with the author. This decision was based on the provisions of the Optional Protocol to the ICESCR and the Committee's rules of procedure.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of consideration due to lack of contact with the author sets a precedent for similar cases in the future. It underscores the importance of maintaining communication with the Committee in order to pursue a communication regarding alleged violations of economic, social, and cultural rights.
