Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/110/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 21 November 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - M.T. and others (authors of the communication)
  - Spain (state party)
- Case Name: M.T. and others v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, facing eviction from a property they had rented without legal title in Spain, submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging violation of their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. They later withdrew their case, stating that they had rented social housing from the Badalona Municipality housing institute. The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication in accordance with its rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted the communication on 11 February 2019, and the Committee adopted a decision on 21 November 2019 to discontinue its consideration following the withdrawal of the case by the authors. The jurisdiction and competence lie with the United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case include eviction for occupation without legal title of a property, housing and property rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The Committee's analysis considered the withdrawal of the case by the authors and the alternative housing arrangements provided by the Badalona Municipality housing institute.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol. The key finding was the withdrawal of the case by the authors and their rental of social housing from the Badalona Municipality housing institute.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the case has implications for similar cases involving eviction and housing rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. While no specific legal precedent was set, the case highlights the importance of alternative housing arrangements in addressing eviction issues.

Citations and References:
The document reference is E/C.12/66/D/110/2019. The legal provisions cited are under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights and its Optional Protocol. Related documents include the communication submitted by M.T. and others to the UNCESCR.
