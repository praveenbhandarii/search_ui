Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/98/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 21 November 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: E.P.P. and daughter (authors)
  - Party B: State party (Spain)
- Case Name: E.P.P. and daughter v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, E.P.P. and her daughter, submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that they were facing eviction due to their failure to pay rent. The Committee considered the communication and decided to discontinue its consideration due to the withdrawal of the case by the authors, who had been allocated social housing.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted the communication on 17 January 2018. The Committee considered the communication at its sixty-sixth session (30 September to 18 October 2019). The authors withdrew their case after being allocated social housing, leading to the discontinuation of the consideration of the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was eviction for failure to pay rent, falling under the subject matter of housing. The withdrawal of the case by the authors was the procedural issue considered by the Committee.

Decisions and Rulings:
The United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 98/2019, which was submitted by E.P.P. and her daughter, due to the authors' withdrawal of the case after they had been allocated social housing. The decision was made at the Committee's sixty-sixth session in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication highlights the importance of addressing housing issues and the impact of social housing allocation on legal proceedings. It sets a precedent for cases involving similar circumstances and emphasizes the significance of providing adequate housing for individuals facing eviction.

Citations and References:
The decision adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, concerning communication No. 98/2019 on 21 November 2019 serves as the primary reference for this case.
