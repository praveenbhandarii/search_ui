Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/73/D/83/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 21 April 2023
- Key Parties Involved: P.A.M.M. and G.H.F.M. (authors and alleged victims) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: P.A.M.M. and G.H.F.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, facing eviction from a dwelling rented from someone who turned out not to be the owner, claimed a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on 20 November 2018.
- The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing.
- The authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the state party's request for discontinuance.
- The Committee discontinued consideration of the communication at its seventy-third session in accordance with rule 18 of its rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: 11(1) (right to adequate housing)
- Procedural issue: Request for interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing
- Substantive issue: Right to adequate housing
- Subject matter: Eviction from a dwelling rented from someone who turned out not to be the owner

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication, noting the authors' failure to respond to the state party's request for discontinuance.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the Committee's handling of communications regarding the right to adequate housing under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
