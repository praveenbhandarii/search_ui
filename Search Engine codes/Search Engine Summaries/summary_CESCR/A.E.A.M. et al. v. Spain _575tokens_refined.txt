Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/72/D/68/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved: A.E.A.M. et al. (authors and their children) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: A.E.A.M. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors and their children, facing eviction without legal title, submitted a communication to the UNCESCR claiming a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted the communication on 24 October 2018. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction of the authors and their children pending the consideration of the communication or to provide them with adequate alternative housing. The authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on Spain's observations, leading the Committee to discontinue consideration of the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issue addressed was the violation of the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The Committee's analysis focused on the failure of the authors to engage with Spain's observations and requests for comments.

Decisions and Rulings:
The UNCESCR discontinued consideration of the communication due to the authors' failure to respond to requests for comments on Spain's observations.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of active engagement with the Committee and the responding party in communications submitted to the UNCESCR. It also emphasizes the significance of genuine consultation in addressing issues related to the right to adequate housing.
