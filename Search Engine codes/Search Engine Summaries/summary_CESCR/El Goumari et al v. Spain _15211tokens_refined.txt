Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/85/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Hakima El Goumari and Ahmed Tidli (authors)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: Hakima El Goumari and Ahmed Tidli v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, Moroccan nationals residing in Spain, were evicted from their home due to non-payment of rent, claiming a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant.

Procedural History:
The authors, Hakima El Goumari and Ahmed Tidli, submitted a communication to the Committee under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, concerning communication No. 85/2018. The communication addressed the admissibility and merits of the case, focusing on the right to adequate housing.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case included the violation of the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The State party argued that it had taken appropriate measures to meet the housing needs of the authors and their family, while the authors claimed that the State party's actions had not provided them with adequate alternative housing.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found that the eviction of the authors without adequate safeguards violated their right to housing. The temporary accommodation provided did not meet the standards of dignity and safety, and the State party had not demonstrated that it considered the particular circumstances of the family's case and took all reasonable measures to the maximum of its available resources.

Impact and Significance:
The Committee's decision sets a precedent for cases involving the right to adequate housing, emphasizing the State party's obligation to provide alternative housing to individuals in need, to the maximum of its available resources.

Citations and References:
The decision is based on the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, relevant legal provisions, and the State party's obligations to uphold the right to adequate housing.

Overall, the case of Hakima El Goumari and Ahmed Tidli v. Spain highlights the importance of protecting the right to adequate housing and the State party's duty to provide alternative housing solutions to individuals in need.
