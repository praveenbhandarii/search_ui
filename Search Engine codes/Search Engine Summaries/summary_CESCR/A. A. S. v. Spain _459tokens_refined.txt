Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/116/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: A.A.S. (author) and her children (alleged victims), State party (Spain)
- Case Name: A.A.S. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author and her children were facing eviction from a dwelling they were occupying without legal title. They submitted a communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by A.A.S. on behalf of herself and her two minor children. The substantive issue was the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The author requested interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. However, the author failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's observations. As a result, the Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issue addressed in this case was the right to adequate housing as provided for in Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee's analysis focused on the request for interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing, as well as the failure of the author to respond to the State party's observations.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication due to the author's failure to respond to the State party's observations. This decision was made in accordance with the Committee's provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication highlights the importance of active participation and cooperation in the legal process. It also emphasizes the significance of responding to the State party's observations in order to ensure a fair and thorough consideration of the case.
