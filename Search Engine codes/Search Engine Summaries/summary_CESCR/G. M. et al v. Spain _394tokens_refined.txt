- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - G.M. (author and alleged victim)
  - Spain (state party)
Case Name: G.M. v. Spain
Case Summary: The author and her minor daughter faced eviction from their dwelling following the withdrawal of the owner's consent to occupancy. The author submitted a communication to the Committee alleging violation of the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by G.M. on 22 June 2018, requesting interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures, but the author later withdrew the communication for personal reasons.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case addressed the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant, as well as the procedural issue of interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee discontinued its consideration of communication No. 40/2018 at its meeting on 22 February 2021, pursuant to rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol, due to the author's withdrawal of the communication for personal reasons.

Impact and Significance:
The case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the procedural mechanisms available under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
