Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/46/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Alleged victims: Belkis Escobar Armijo and Juan Carlos Bedoya Acosta
  - State party: Spain
- Case Name: Belkis Escobar Armijo and Juan Carlos Bedoya Acosta v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, who were renting a dwelling from someone who turned out not to be the owner, were facing eviction. They submitted a communication to the Committee on their behalf and on behalf of their three minor children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. Later, the authors requested the Committee to discontinue the consideration of the communication as they had found stable housing, and the Committee agreed to do so.

Procedural History:
- The authors submitted a communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights on 4 July 2018.
- The Committee registered the communication on 16 July 2018 and requested the State party to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing.
- At a meeting on 12 October 2021, the authors requested the Committee to discontinue the consideration of the communication as they had found stable housing.
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of the communication in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Court's Analysis: The Committee considered the communication and requested interim measures from the State party.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of the communication in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the role of international bodies in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights.
