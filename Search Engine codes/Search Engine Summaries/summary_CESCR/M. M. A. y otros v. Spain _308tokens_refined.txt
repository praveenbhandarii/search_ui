Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/53/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 24 November 2020
- Key Parties Involved: M.M.A. et al. (authors) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: M.M.A. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, facing eviction for non-payment of rent, alleged a violation of their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The Committee discontinued consideration after the State party reported that the authors had been assigned a social housing unit and were now living in it permanently, and the authors agreed with this decision.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on 6 September 2018.
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 53/2018 on 11 October 2019, in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: Not specified
- Submitted by: M.M.A. et al.
- Procedural issue: Withdrawal of the State party
- Substantive issue: Eviction for non-payment of rent
- Subject matter: Housing

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 53/2018.

Impact and Significance:
- The decision has implications for cases involving eviction for non-payment of rent and the right to housing under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
