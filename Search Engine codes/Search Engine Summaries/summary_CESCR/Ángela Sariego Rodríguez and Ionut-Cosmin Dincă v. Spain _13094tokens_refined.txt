Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/92/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Angela Sariego Rodriguez, Ionut Cosmin Dinc (represented by counsel Maria Pilar Galan Lujan of Plataforma de Afectados por la Hipoteca of Oviedo) vs. Spain
- Case Name: Angela Sariego Rodriguez and Ionut Cosmin Dinc v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors claim that the State party has violated their rights under article 11 of the Covenant by failing to provide them with adequate housing.

Procedural History:
- The authors, Ángela Sariego Rodríguez and Ionut Cosmin Dinc, are facing eviction from their home in Oviedo, Spain.
- They are representing themselves and their son in this case.
- The communication was registered by the Committee on January 7, 2019, and the State party was requested to suspend the authors' eviction while the communication was being considered.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The authors claim a violation of their rights under Article 11 of the Covenant, specifically the right to adequate housing.
- The State party argues that the authors have failed to exhaust domestic remedies and have abused the right to submit a communication.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The State party's arguments are being disputed by the authors, citing their low income and the unavailability of adequate alternative housing.
- The State party's obligations under Article 11(1) of the Covenant are being questioned.

Impact and Significance:
- The case involves the fundamental right to adequate housing and the obligations of the State party to provide for vulnerable individuals and families facing eviction.

Citations and References:
- International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- State party's domestic housing policies and practices
