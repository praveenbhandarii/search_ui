Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/73/D/103/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2023
- Key Parties Involved: L.J.F. et al. (authors and their son) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: L.J.F. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors and their son submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that their eviction by the State party would violate their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The authors submitted the communication on 4 February 2019, requesting interim measures to suspend eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. The Committee discontinued consideration of the communication on 27 February 2023, due to the authors' failure to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's request for discontinuance.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case addressed the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The Committee's decision to discontinue consideration was based on rule 18 of its rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication at its seventy-third session on 27 February 2023.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the communication has implications for the authors' eviction and their right to adequate housing. It also sets a precedent for future communications regarding similar issues.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee on 20 April 2023. Participating and non-participating members of the Committee were listed, along with the relevant rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.
