Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/25/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: G.M.S. (author and alleged victim) and Spain (State party)
- Case Name: G.M.S. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, G.M.S., submitted a communication to the UNCESCR on behalf of herself and her three children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing, but later discontinued consideration of the communication after the author signed a lease for a social housing unit.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 25/2018 was submitted by G.M.S. on behalf of herself and her three children to the United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The alleged violation was related to the right to adequate housing due to eviction without legal title. The author requested interim measures to suspend eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication after the author signed a lease for a social housing unit.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal details of the case include the specific article of the Covenant (11(1) - right to adequate housing), the procedural issue of the request for interim measures, the substantive issue of eviction for occupation without legal title, and the subject matter of the right to adequate housing. The date of communication was 15 February 2018.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights discontinued its consideration of communication No. 25/2018 after the author signed a lease for a social housing unit.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication by the Committee has implications for the author and her children, as well as potential broader legal impact in similar cases involving the right to adequate housing.

Citations and References:
The legal document is Decision adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, concerning communication No. 25/2018, with the case ID E/C.12/69/D/25/2018.
