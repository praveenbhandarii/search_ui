Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/62/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: T.E.M.O. (author and alleged victim), her two minor children (alleged victims), and Spain (State party)
- Case Name: T.E.M.O. v. Spain
- Case Summary: T.E.M.O. and her two minor children were evicted from a dwelling they occupied without legal title. The author submitted an individual communication to the Committee on her behalf and on that of her two minor children, alleging a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- 4 October 2018: Author submits individual communication to the Committee
- 7 November 2018: Committee registers the communication and requests Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing
- 12 October 2021: Committee notes author's failure to respond to requests for comments on Spain's observations and decides to discontinue consideration of the communication
- 27 September-15 October 2021: Committee holds its seventieth session
- 28 January 2022: Committee adopts decision on the communication

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Article of the Covenant: 11(1) (right to adequate housing)
- Procedural issue: Request for interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing
- Substantive issue: Right to adequate housing
- Subject matter: Eviction from a dwelling occupied without legal title

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication due to the author's lack of interest.

Impact and Significance:
- The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication sets a precedent for the importance of active participation in legal proceedings and the consequences of failing to respond to requests for comments.

Citations and References:
