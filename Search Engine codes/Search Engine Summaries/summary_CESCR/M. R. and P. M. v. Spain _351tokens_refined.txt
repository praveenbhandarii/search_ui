Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/34/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2019
- Key Parties Involved: M.R. and P.M. (authors) vs. Spain (state party)
- Case Name: M.R. and P.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, M.R. and P.M., submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that they were facing eviction from their home without legal title. Despite repeated attempts, the Committee was unable to contact the authors, and therefore decided to discontinue the consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
- The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixtysixth session (30 September to 18 October 2019)
- The document is distributed in accordance with the Committee's provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The Committee noted the procedural issue of discontinuation of consideration of communication due to lack of contact with authors
- The substantive issue addressed was eviction without legal title
- The subject matter of the case was housing and property rights

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue the consideration of communication No. 34/2018

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for cases involving eviction without legal title and the Committee's approach to communications where contact with the authors is not established

Citations and References:
- The document is dated 19 November 2019
- The reference number for the decision is E/C.12/66/D/34/2018
