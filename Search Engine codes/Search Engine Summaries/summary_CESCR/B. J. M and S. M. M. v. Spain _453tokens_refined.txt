Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/31/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: B.J.M. and S.M.M. (authors) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: B.J.M. and S.M.M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, who were occupying a dwelling without legal title, were facing eviction. They submitted a communication to the Committee on behalf of themselves and their two minor children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. However, the authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on Spain's observations, leading the Committee to discontinue its consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 31/2018 was submitted by B.J.M. and S.M.M. on behalf of themselves and their two minor children to the United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The alleged violation was related to the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee requested interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing. However, the authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's observations, leading to the discontinuation of the consideration of the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in this case include the right to adequate housing as per Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee's analysis focused on the failure of the authors to respond to requests for comments on the State party's observations, which led to the discontinuation of the consideration of the communication.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee discontinued its consideration of communication No. 31/2018 in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol, due to the authors' failure to respond to requests for comments on the State party's observations.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the consideration of the communication sets a precedent for the importance of timely and substantive responses to requests for comments in cases brought before the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. It underscores the significance of active participation and cooperation from the parties involved in such communications.
