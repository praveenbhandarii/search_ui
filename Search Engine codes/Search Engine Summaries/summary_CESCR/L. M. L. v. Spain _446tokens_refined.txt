Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/81/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - L.M.L. (author and alleged victim)
  - Spain (state party)
- Case Name: L.M.L. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, L.M.L., submitted a communication to the Committee on behalf of herself and her three minor children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to eviction from their dwelling for failure to pay rent.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted on 17 November 2018 and registered by the Committee on 20 November 2018. The Committee requested Spain, the State party, to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the author. The author failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's observations, resulting in the Committee discontinuing its consideration of the communication in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The case addressed the substantive issue of eviction from a dwelling for failure to pay rent, which is a violation of the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee's analysis focused on the failure of the author to respond to requests for comments on the State party's observations, leading to the discontinuation of the case.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee discontinued its consideration of the communication due to the author's loss of interest, in accordance with rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the case highlights the importance of active participation and cooperation from all parties involved in the communication process. It also emphasizes the need for genuine consultation and engagement in addressing human rights violations related to housing.

Citations and References:
The decision was adopted by the Committee at its 70th session held from 27 September to 15 October 2021.
