Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/20/2017
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - M.L.B. (the author and victim, represented by counsel, Frdric Fabre)
  - Luxembourg (the state party)
Case Name: M.L.B. v. Luxembourg
Case Summary: M.L.B., a French citizen, alleges that Luxembourg has violated his rights under article 8 (1) (a) and (3) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The author was dismissed from his job as a trade union delegate for serious misconduct, and the court ruled that the dismissal was justified.

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: 28 November 2016 (initial submission)
- Date of adoption of the decision: 11 October 2019
- Jurisdiction and Competence: Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights entered into force for the State party on 3 May 2015.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Dismissal of a trade union delegate, exhaustion of domestic remedies, jurisdiction ratione temporis, jurisdiction ratione materiae, evaluation of facts and evidence, manifestly groundless communication and lack of substantiation of claims
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 8 (1) (a) and (3) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Court's Analysis: The author alleges violations of his rights under the Covenant in relation to his dismissal from employment, and raises issues regarding the failure of the Court of Appeal to consider relevant evidence and the lack of distinction between the misconduct of an ordinary worker and a protected union delegate.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee found the communication admissible under the Optional Protocol to the Covenant.
- The Committee held that the appeal allowed the national courts to examine in detail the evidence submitted by the author and consider the alleged violations, with a view to providing redress if appropriate.

Impact and Significance:
- The case sets a precedent for the admissibility of communications under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- It highlights the importance of thorough examination of evidence and consideration of alleged violations by national courts.
