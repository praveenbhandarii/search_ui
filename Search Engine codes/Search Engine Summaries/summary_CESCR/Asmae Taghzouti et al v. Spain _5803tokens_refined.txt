Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/56/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2021
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A: Asmae Taghzouti Ezqouihel (author and applicant)
  - Party B: Spain (State party)
- Case Name: Asmae Taghzouti Ezqouihel v. Spain
- Case Summary: Asmae Taghzouti Ezqouihel, a Spanish citizen, filed a complaint with the UNCESCR claiming a violation of her rights under Article 11(1) of the Covenant due to the eviction of her and her children from their home. The Committee requested Spain to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing to avoid irreparable harm.

Procedural History:
The author, Asmae Taghzouti Ezqouihel, filed the complaint on September 21, 2018, with the UNCESCR. The State party, Spain, argued that the communication was inadmissible as domestic remedies had not been exhausted and that the right to submit a communication had been abused.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The legal issues addressed in the case included the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee analyzed the State party's arguments regarding the exhaustion of domestic remedies and the author's lack of due diligence in applying for alternative housing.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee found the communication inadmissible under Article 3(2)(e) of the Optional Protocol, citing the author's failure to sufficiently substantiate the claim that the State party failed to meet its obligations under Article 11(1) of the Covenant.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the importance of substantiating claims and diligently pursuing domestic remedies before filing a communication with the UNCESCR. It also highlights the challenges faced by individuals seeking protection of their right to housing under international law.

Citations and References:
The decision adopted by the Committee under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, concerning communication No. 56/2018 serves as the primary reference for this case. Additionally, the legal provisions of Article 11(1) of the Covenant and the Optional Protocol were central to the Committee's analysis.
