Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/69/D/74/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: P.E. (author and alleged victim), State party (Spain)
- Case Name: P.E. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author, on behalf of herself and her minor children, submitted a communication to the Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing due to foreclosure proceedings against their landlord.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted by P.E. on 6 November 2018, under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction of the author and her children pending the consideration of the communication or to provide them with adequate alternative housing. The author later withdrew her communication after obtaining social housing, leading to the Committee discontinuing its consideration of the communication.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the right to adequate housing, as provided under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee considered the request for interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide alternative housing in genuine consultation with the author.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction of the author and her children pending the consideration of the communication or to provide them with adequate alternative housing. The author's withdrawal of the communication led to the Committee discontinuing its consideration in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol.

Impact and Significance:
This case highlights the importance of the right to adequate housing and the role of international bodies in addressing violations of economic, social, and cultural rights. It also demonstrates the potential impact of obtaining alternative housing on the consideration of communications under the Optional Protocol.
