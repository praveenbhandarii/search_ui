Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/29/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Party A. (G.V.S.O. - the author and alleged victim)
  - Party B (Spain - state party)
- Case Name: G.V.S.O. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author and her children were evicted from a dwelling they occupied without legal title. The author submitted a communication to the UNCESCR claiming that the eviction violated their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on 11 April 2018
- The author withdrew their consideration of the communication
- The Committee decided to discontinue the communication in accordance with Rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11(1) of the Covenant
- Court's Analysis: The Committee noted the withdrawal of consideration and decided to discontinue the communication

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee discontinued the communication in accordance with Rule 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol

Impact and Significance:
- The decision sets a precedent for the discontinuation of communications in cases where the author withdraws consideration
- It reaffirms the Committee's commitment to following its procedural rules under the Optional Protocol
