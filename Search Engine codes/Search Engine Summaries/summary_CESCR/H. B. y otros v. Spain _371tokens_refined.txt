Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/87/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2020
- Key Parties Involved: H.B. et al. (authors and their children) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: H.B. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors and their children alleged that they were evicted from their homes without legal title, and that the State party failed to provide them with adequate housing, in violation of Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights considered communication No. 87/2018 at its meeting on 5 March 2020
- The authors of the communication had previously withdrawn their case, stating that they had been provided with alternative housing
- In light of this, the Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication, in accordance with Article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixty-seventh session (17 February-6 March 2020)

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The substantive issue addressed was the eviction for occupation without legal title and the right to adequate housing
- The relevant legal provision was Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- The Committee's analysis focused on the withdrawal of the case by the authors and the provision of alternative housing to them

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 87/2018 in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights

Impact and Significance:
- The decision to discontinue the consideration of the communication has implications for similar cases involving the right to adequate housing and evictions without legal title
- It sets a precedent for the Committee's approach to cases where the authors withdraw their claims after being provided with alternative housing
