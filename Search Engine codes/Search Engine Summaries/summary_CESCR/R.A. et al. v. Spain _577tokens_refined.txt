Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/72/D/84/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved: R.A. et al. (authors and their children) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: R.A. et al. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, on behalf of themselves and their three minor children, submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging violation of their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights due to their eviction from their home without legal title. The Committee requested Spain to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, but the authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's observations, leading the Committee to discontinue consideration of the communication.

Procedural History:
The United Nations Economic and Social Council's Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (CESCR) received the communication (No. 84/2018) from R.A. et al. on November 18, 2018. The Committee requested Spain, the state party, to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, and registered the communication on November 26, 2018. The authors failed to respond to repeated requests for comments on the state party's observations on the admissibility and merits of the communication. The Committee concluded that the authors had lost interest and decided to discontinue consideration of the communication at its seventy-second session (September 26 - October 14, 2022) in accordance with Rule 18 of its rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol. The decision was adopted by the Committee on November 16, 2022.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the communication was the eviction without legal title and the right to adequate housing, which is protected under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. The authors, R.A. et al., submitted the communication and alleged a violation of their housing and property rights.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication due to the authors' failure to respond to requests for comments on the State party's observations. This decision was adopted on November 16, 2022.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of the communication highlights the importance of active participation and cooperation in the legal process. It also emphasizes the significance of responding to requests for comments and observations in legal proceedings.
