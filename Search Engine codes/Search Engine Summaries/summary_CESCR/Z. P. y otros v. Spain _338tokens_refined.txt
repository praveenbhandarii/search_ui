Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/43/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 21 November 2019
- Key Parties Involved: Z.P. and others (alleged victims), State party (Spain)
- Case Name: Z.P. and others v. Spain
- Case Summary: The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights considered communication No. 43/2018, which alleged violations of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights in relation to an eviction on foreclosure. Despite repeated attempts, the Committee was unable to contact the authors, and therefore decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on June 27, 2018
- The Committee attempted to contact the authors but were unable to do so
- The decision was adopted by the Committee at its sixtysixth session (September 30 to October 18, 2019)

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- The decision to discontinue consideration of the communication was made in accordance with article 17 of the Committee's provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- The lack of response from the authors made it impossible for the Committee to continue considering the communication

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 43/2018

Impact and Significance:
- The decision brings an end to the case, with no further action to be taken by the Committee or the state party in this matter
