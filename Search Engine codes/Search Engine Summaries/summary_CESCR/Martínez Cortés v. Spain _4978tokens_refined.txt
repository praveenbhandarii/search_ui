Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/73/D/214/2021
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2 May 2023
- Key Parties Involved: Jhon David Martnez Corts (represented by counsel), State party: Spain
- Case Name: Jhon David Martnez Corts v. Spain
- Case Summary: Jhon David Martnez Corts, a Colombian national, claimed that his eviction from a flat in Spain would violate his rights under article 11 (1) of the Covenant. He requested interim measures consisting of the suspension of the eviction or provision of alternative housing.

Procedural History:
- The author arrived in Spain from Colombia in February 2020 and sought asylum in May 2020.
- Entered into a verbal contract to rent an apartment in October 2020.
- Received notice of an eviction order in March 2021 and applied for free legal aid and to appeal against the eviction order.
- Applied for social housing in April 2021.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Substantive issue: Right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant.
- Procedural issues: Eviction for occupation without legal title, non-exhaustion of domestic remedies, insufficient substantiation of claims.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee lifted its request for interim measures and considered the admissibility of the communication separately from the merits.

Impact and Significance:
- The case highlights the challenges faced by asylum seekers in accessing adequate housing and the legal remedies available to them.

Citations and References:
- International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Royal Decree-Law No. 37/2020, 8/2021, and 16/2021 regarding urgent measures for social and economic vulnerability in housing and transport, and social protection measures.
