Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/129/2019
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 2020
- Key Parties Involved:
  - C.P.V.H. (the author and alleged victim)
  - State party (Spain)
- Case Name: C.P.V.H. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The author and her children were facing eviction due to failure to vacate after the expiration of their lease. They submitted a communication to the UNCESCR, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 129/2019 was submitted by C.P.V.H. on behalf of herself and her children on March 19, 2019. The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights decided to discontinue its consideration of the communication in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, as the author had found adequate alternative housing.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed was the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The withdrawal of the case by the author was a procedural issue, leading to the discontinuation of the consideration by the Committee.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 129/2019 due to the author finding adequate alternative housing.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the Committee's approach to cases involving the right to adequate housing and the withdrawal of the case by the author. It also highlights the importance of finding adequate alternative housing in such situations.
