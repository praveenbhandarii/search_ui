Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/70/D/50/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2021
- Key Parties Involved: M.E.M.E.M. and F.B. (authors and alleged victims) vs. Spain (State party)
- Case Name: M.E.M.E.M. and F.B. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, a married couple and their three minor children, faced eviction for non-payment of rent and alleged a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant.

Procedural History:
The communication was submitted on 28 August 2018, and the Committee discontinued its consideration due to the authors' failure to respond to repeated requests for comments on the State party's observations.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed was the eviction for non-payment of rent, with the key legal provision being Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee's decision to discontinue consideration was based on the authors' failure to respond to requests for comments on the State party's observations.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee adopted the decision to discontinue consideration of the communication at its 70th session in 2021.

Impact and Significance:
The case sets a precedent for the importance of timely and active participation in the communication process under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Citations and References:
