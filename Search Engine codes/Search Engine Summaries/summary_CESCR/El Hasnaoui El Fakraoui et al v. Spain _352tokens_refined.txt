Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/66/D/60/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Dates: 21 November 2019
- Key Parties Involved:
  - N.E.H.E.F. and others (authors)
  - State party (Spain)
- Case Name: N.E.H.E.F. and others v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors alleged that Spain had violated their rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights due to eviction from their homes following the termination of their lease by the owners.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on 5 October 2018.
- The Committee considered the communication on 11 October 2019.
- The decision to discontinue consideration was made on 21 November 2019.
- Jurisdiction and Competence: The Committee has jurisdiction under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issues Addressed: Alleged violation of rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Not specified.
- Court's Analysis: The authors withdrew the case after signing a new lease with the owners, leading to the discontinuation of consideration by the Committee.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 60/2018.
- The decision was adopted at the Committee's sixtysixth session.

Impact and Significance:
- The discontinuation of consideration signifies the resolution of the dispute between the authors and the state party, Spain.
- The case sets a precedent for the resolution of similar disputes related to housing and property rights under the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Citations and References:
- Article 17 of the Committee's provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
