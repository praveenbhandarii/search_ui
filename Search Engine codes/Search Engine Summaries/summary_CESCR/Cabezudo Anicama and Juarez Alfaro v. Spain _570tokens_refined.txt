Combined Summary:

Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/72/D/89/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2022
- Key Parties Involved:
  - Victor Wilfredo Cabezudo Anicama and Luz Marlene Juarez Alfaro (authors and alleged victims)
  - Spain (State party)
- Case Name: Victor Wilfredo Cabezudo Anicama and Luz Marlene Juarez Alfaro v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, facing eviction from a dwelling rented from someone who turned out not to be the owner, claimed a violation of their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- The communication was submitted on 28 November 2018 and concerned the right to adequate housing (Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights).
- The Committee requested the State party (Spain) to take interim measures to suspend the eviction or provide adequate alternative housing, in genuine consultation with the authors.
- The decision of the UNCESCR was adopted on 10 October 2022, and it discontinued consideration of the communication due to the authors' request for discontinuance on the grounds that they had found alternative housing.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issue Addressed: Right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.
- Court's Analysis: The Committee considered the authors' request for discontinuance and the finding of alternative housing.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of the communication, in accordance with rule 18 of its rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol, as the authors had found alternative housing.

Impact and Significance:
- The discontinuation of the communication signifies that the authors' request for alternative housing was successful, and the case was resolved without further consideration by the Committee.
