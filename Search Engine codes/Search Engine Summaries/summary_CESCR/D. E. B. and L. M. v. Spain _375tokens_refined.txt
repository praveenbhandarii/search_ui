Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/24/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 23 April 2020
- Key Parties Involved: D. E. B. and L. M. (authors) vs. Spain (state party)
- Case Name: D. E. B. and L. M. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors, D. E. B. and L. M., submitted a communication to the UNCESCR alleging that their eviction from their home without legal title violated their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
- Date of communication: 6 February 2018
- The Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 24/2018 at its meeting on 5 March 2020, as the authors had withdrawn the case and were now renting a room in a shared apartment.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
- Legal Issue: Eviction for occupation without legal title and the right to adequate housing
- Relevant Legal Provisions: Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
- Court's Analysis: The Committee noted the withdrawal of the case by the authors and their current living situation.

Decisions and Rulings:
- The Committee decided to discontinue its consideration of communication No. 24/2018 in accordance with article 17 of its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Impact and Significance:
- The discontinuation of the case has implications for the authors' claim and the broader understanding of the right to adequate housing under the Covenant.
