Case Details:
- Case ID: E/C.12/67/D/35/2018
- Court/Tribunal: United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (UNCESCR)
- Document Date: 2020
- Key Parties Involved: M.J.J.F. and J.A.A. (authors and alleged victims) vs. Spain (state party)
- Case Name: M.J.J.F. and J.A.A. v. Spain
- Case Summary: The authors and their children were evicted from their home due to foreclosure by the owner, and they alleged that this eviction violated their right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Procedural History:
The communication No. 35/2018 was submitted by M.J.J.F. and J.A.A. on behalf of themselves and their children, alleging violation of their right to adequate housing. The Committee discontinued consideration of the communication due to inability to contact the authors, in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Legal Analysis and Reasoning:
The substantive issue addressed in the case was the eviction on foreclosure by the owner, which was alleged to violate the right to adequate housing under Article 11(1) of the Covenant. The Committee's decision to discontinue consideration was based on the procedural issue of inability to contact the authors.

Decisions and Rulings:
The Committee decided to discontinue consideration of communication No. 35/2018 due to the inability to contact the authors, in accordance with its provisional rules of procedure under the Optional Protocol to the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights.

Impact and Significance:
The discontinuation of consideration of the communication has implications for the authors and their claim of violation of their right to adequate housing. It also sets a precedent for cases where the authors cannot be contacted, leading to a discontinuation of consideration by the Committee.
